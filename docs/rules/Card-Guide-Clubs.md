# Card Guide: Clubs

This guide provides the complete mechanical, narrative, and Yarn specifications for all 13 cards of the Clubs suit in the T13 Narrative Game Engine.

## 🃑 Ace of Clubs

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **14**
- **Wound Type:** **Carnage** *(Wound Level 6)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Burden, Dominion, Fury, Rook, Sin, Wyrd*
- **General Meaning:** *Aces signify beginnings, meetings and unions. They can indicate love-interests, chance encounters, alliances, and friendships.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Trap**
- **Normal Position:** *+2 Success Levels to all Actions to do with setting, designing and causing damage from traps (Snare Attacks).*
- **Crossed Position:** *+2 Failure levels to all Actions to do with disarming, avoiding, and soaking damage from traps.*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Green/Turquoise Herbivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Herd, Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *All-path*
- **Example Animal:** *All-Walkers / Brahman*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Nerves (Instinct)*
- **Lea Notes:** *The Paths of the All-Walkers and Brahman can allow Bulmäs to do extraordinary things, like walking an individual’s life-path, so that all of their personal history is known to them. All-Walkers can assume even the forms and souls of other people by using these paths, without directly eating of the Soul. All-Walkers can even Doom Weave and play other cards from their hands as Wyrd Tarot cards, once they have played an Ace on themselves.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *8*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Low-Medium-High*
- **Test:** *Judge (Wyrd)*
- **Obstacles:** *3 Obstacles per Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Finesse*
- **Stage:** *Do something that requires immense amounts of control over your vehicle, or self-control. There is always at least one Obstacle in a Control Stunt Stage.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Someone is trying to get away, across the lava fields / glacier / tidal bridge / mountain-goat road you have to chase them, in a Jeep, for some reason.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Flowing forces are able to direct the battle enough that they can create a trap on the battle-field. This allows the Flowing forces to Prepare 1 additional card from their Pool to their Style Reserves, in addition to any cards they may Prepare as an Action.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The Fight is an ambush by a well prepared opponent with defences and escape strategies in place. The opponents are usually weaker than the party, but have good defences and specialised weapons and tactics that give them at least one Preparation Phase, before combat is begun.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Court** — *The Court card represents both the Courts of Law and the Legal System, and Royal Courts and Parliaments. It can represent legal and political situations, but also social judgements too.*
- **Hook Event:** **Arrested** — *The Story opens with the PCs captured, arrested or detained. It may be a case of mistaken identity, or they could be suspects, or maybe they did break a law or two on the way. This is perhaps justice weighing in on their past misdeeds. Sometimes there may be a bit of a Chase or Escape attempt before they are arrested, in fact a whole Epic can arise from being outlaws.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **I-Ching (First Hexagram)** — *The Hook targets the Character's I-Ching specifically the Gain of their First Hexagram. For example: If a Character has the Cauldron (50) I-Ching, then the Hook needs to have someone hungry turn up asking for help.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Accolade (A knighthood, title, medal or treasure, A Super-Skill Artefact Descendant/Province)*
- **The Fray:** **Court Appearance** — *The Fray is a Court Appearance, this may mean that the PCs will be the defendants in a Court Case, or that they will be judged socially when they appear in the "Royal Court" of the friends of a powerful person. Either way is an Opposed Ordeal (or Test) that has variable Stakes from Low to Paradoxical.*
- **The Snag:** **Dispute** — *The Snag is an additional dispute either during the Fray or arising from the Fray. Often this Dispute results from the Fray as a form of Blame-Storming, as everyone discusses where they went wrong and whose fault it was.*
- **Sweeping:** **Sweet Justice** — *The PCs get some justice, assuming how they behaved this could mean they are awarded damages, accolades, destinies, cause for celebration, get the love interest, or perhaps just laments. This at least grants the PCs a rest, and usually a Gain.*
- **History Context:** **Casebook** — *The Court indicates that the past of this situation revolves around a previous case, this may be a returning litigant, antagonist or criminal, or an old cold case rears its head.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Litigation** — *The Court indicates that the current situation is a litigation, this may be an ongoing court case, a civil, criminal, religious, military, or even political trial, sometimes it may be writing a new law, or even trial by combat.*
- **Solution:** **Arbitration** — *The Court indicates that the solution to the current situation is arbitration, this may mean finding a suitable referee, judge, authority or noble who can hear the case, ideally impartially, as well as actually undergoing the arbitration, which may be a negotiation or be adversarial.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **The Story** — The information is about the current situation and plot. It might include information about one side of the Conflict, all the sides and anything in between. It is normal when providing information to always reveal information about the Dominant Side as well as any others.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Costly** — Information that is valuable and useful, somehow always has a cost. The costs can vary from a simple money transaction to longer, more permanent costs.
  - *Vector (How):* **Records** — Records can be tally marks on clay tablets; written laws and history on vellum; detailed client databases; the tangled, knotted, weavings the Eelafin call writing; musical or speech recordings, even several slightly contradictory witness statements. Regardless of how they tell us, they always have something to share, if we know how to interpret them correctly.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Legal** — The information is legal in nature, this may mean anything from thousands of pages of legal tomes, to a parking ticket. Often legal information will reveal past transgressions, but can also reveal property and business ownership, banking paper trails, familial connections, even contracts, treaties and alliances.
  - *Detail:* **Loophole** — Loophole details reveal an ambiguity or deficiency in a system. Originally loopholes were places for archers to shoot out of a castle, and their placement was important to allow archers to cover the out walls, stopping ladders or sappers from approaching, sometimes during a siege a single archer not manning his loophole, or a loophole being misplaced, or deficient somehow could mean a section of wall was not being covered, and a push there could breach the wall. Later this attention to these details became used to describe other situations where something could be circumvented because of a technical defect.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Sanctioned** — *Sometimes a Character can be told they must cease and desist some activity by lawyers, or a Character may finally let down their guard, or they may be captured and rather than being chained or imprisoned are kept under guard.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character must discard all Clubs (<span class="t13ne-blackcard">♣</span>) cards from Ordeal Pool, Style Reserves and Wyrd Tarot Hand, until they have zero Stress. Each Club Discarded removes 1 Stress, 1 Strain, or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Punishment** — *Trauma can sometimes act as a punishment, sometimes this is deliberate, other times the nature of the punishment maybe karmic, poetic justice or irony. Sometimes this punishment seems unjustified, as if randomly assigned (by a card no less), but usually careful examination of the circumstances can reveal some reasonable justification.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates some kind of punishment for the Character, this is often in the form of a Hitch or a lingering Wound affect (Maiming and Crippling Wounds particularly). The Character can Relieve Stress by complaining about the "punishment" they have received, how unjustified it was etc.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Law** — *An Age of Law is occurring. During this age a society will become increasingly law-abiding and litiguous, where as once in a society members may have discussed their differences, or even dueled, now such disputes are settled before a magistrate or judge, often with the aid of numerous legal counsellors, barristers, lawyers, solicitors, clerks and bailiffs all being involved and paid. An Age of Law will usually involve an improvement in law-enforcement for the society, with police or guards being paid more, better trained and equipped, as well as being bolstered in numbers, typically.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Law Characters will attempt to resolve all disputes by legal proceedings, rather than other, less expensive methods. Criminality will always impart a negative impression during this age, even to other criminals.

## 🃒 Two of Clubs

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **2**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Wyrd*
- **General Meaning:** *Twos signify conflict, opposition and choices. They can indicate crossroads, partings, separations, and changes of direction.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Crisis**
- **Normal Position:** *Automatic Proficiency Crisis. Add a Proficiency to an Annex (or merge two Proficiencies into a Skill).*
- **Crossed Position:** *Re-roll a Proficiency Crisis.*

- **Path Color:** **Green/Turquoise Herbivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Herd, Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *Microfauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Plankton/Phytoplankton/Algae*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Skin*
- **Lea Notes:** *Microscopic animal and algal plant and bacterial paths, often overlooked.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *3*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Low*
- **Test:** *Perceive (Key)*
- **Obstacles:** *1 Obstacle in a Court Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Evade / Dodge*
- **Stage:** *The route ahead is cluttered, perhaps with parked cards, litter bins. Book stacks, filing cabinets, shelves, scaffolding poles, pipes, trees or bamboo poles can also create clutter.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Who is that ahead of you? You need to catch them to find out. Drop a gear and put your foot to the floor.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing enemy position is good, they are in the best place to control the battle, the only issue is the Flowing forces are massing to test these defences. ### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Eelafin** — *The Eelafin card is representative of the hybrid-faeries, who are characterised as having strong, graceful bodies, with just a hint of madness or wanderlust. Normal humans fear the Eelafin Doom-Weaving, that they usually imagine as curses. The Fae consider themselves half-doom and half-dream, the Eelafin they usually call Changeling, and consider them part-doom, part-doomed, part-dream, part-dreamer, and fear their Liberty and envy their mortality.*
- **Hook Event:** **Sensed** — *The Eelafin are know not just for their Doom-sense, but for their heightened senses in general. Perhaps a cry for help is heard, or a crime is witnessed by the Character to Hook them.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **I-Ching (Changing Lines)** — *The Hook targets the Character's I-Ching specifically the lines that change state between their First and Second Hexagram. The 3rd changing Line of The Creative for example rewards caution and responsibility, allowing the Hook to offer a situation where these may be demonstrated.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Bloodline/Doom Descendant (some bloodlines and destinies grant extra Skills, Talents or even Powers, the Character discovers one of theirs)*
- **The Fray:** **Doom** — *A Doom is a profound, Chronolithed event, a predestination paradox, or an event that is part of history and should not be changed, they are always Paradoxical Stakes Ordeals. Dooms are sensed approaching by every Mercari, Eelafin, or Yarn-Teller present. While a Doom is influenced and affected by the cards played, it is only the Ordeal cards that define the final success of the Doom, be careful, you may find yourself on the wrong side of history.*
- **The Snag:** **Hitches** — *The Fray is complicated by a situation that involved many of the PCs Hitches. If a Character has a Woe that hasn't been triggered recently, now is the time. If they have a a few quirks, find a scenario that triggers them all, sequentially or together. For fairness, every Character involved in the Fray should have at least one of their Hitches triggered regardless of side.*
- **Sweeping:** **Destiny** — *Destiny sounds like a simple thing, but what it often means is years of study, months of rigorous training, and at least one Crisis. This is one of those events, Gain, as appropriate
* New Proficiencies and a new Skill Annex
* Increase an Annex one Tier, gaining Proficiencies as required.
* A new Skill/Talent/Power Learning, Training, or Lore Descendant
* A new Monolith Descendant*
- **History Context:** **Betwixt** — *The Eelafin indicates that the current situation has been caused by interaction between two very disparate forces. One side is usually mundane, often ordinary people and the other is usually other-worldly, or super-, preter- or just un-natural such as F&aelig;ries, Increated, Eusaiveans or Toons.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Changeling** — *The Eelafin indicates that the current situation has somebody who has been replaced, this may be with an actor, a mole, a clone, alien puppet-master, a magical realism replacement such as a shape-shifter, face-dancer, doppelganger, or an actual changeling, or a more supernatural explanation, such as a spirit, angelic or demonic possession or something more weird like a Eusaivean deep cover agent. The replacement may act as completely differently, or more interestingly they may behave almost perfectly the same as the original.*
- **Solution:** **Combine** — *The Eelafin indicates that the solution to the current situation is to combine at least two potential, but often unlikely, solutions together to synthesise a new solution that will probably work.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **The Story** — The information is about the current situation and plot. It might include information about one side of the Conflict, all the sides and anything in between. It is normal when providing information to always reveal information about the Dominant Side as well as any others.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Suspicions** — Suspicions vary in scope and accuracy more than any other Information. If a detective suspects some one of a crime, they are probably right more often than a conspiracy theorist is right about the government, but both can be wrong, or ultimately proved right. Suspicions lack evidence, and are often little more than a reaction to a person or group.
  - *Vector (How):* **Tapestry** — The information is present upon a tapestry, poster, billboard, or perhaps is read from Wyrd's Tapestry directly, by Doom-reading from cards in play "The bad guy has a Wyrd Tarot Hand?! ### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Shield** — *The Shield card stands for those that defend us, and those who in turn defend them. The Shield is defence, armour, the thin blue line and a standing army. It can mean that something needs defending, that an attack is coming, and serves as a warning.*
- **Hook Event:** **Tone-Setter** — *The Conflict Hooks the Character with a powerful, Tone setting Scene. The Tone should reflect the Dominant side of the Conflict, usually an under represented Facet on that side of the Conflict. This tone setting serves as a warning of what to expect later.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Incarna** — *The Hook targets the Character's Incarna, this is often a threat to life and limb, or an offer for healing a significant Wound, training or improvement of the Incarna Facet.*
- **Gain Reward:** *A Defence Descendant (armour, gauntlets, shield, anti-virus software, insurance, etc)*
- **The Fray:** **Raise Shields** — *This is total war, and during total war you do not go into battle without putting on armour, strapping on a shield, and raising your deflector fields. This is an Extreme Stakes Opposed Ordeal, where the PCs usually defend a position, vehicle or person that's under attack.*
- **The Snag:** **Redoubt** — *The Fray is set at least partially in a fortress, police station, dungeon, maximum security prison, airbase, or similarly well-defended place. During the Redoubt Snag every Stage has at least one Obstacle and double the number normally expected in this sort of Ordeal. Stakes will be raised one higher (and Black Twos Wild), and add in as many Vex and Chorus Extras as you think the PCs can possibly handle, and then a handful of Cast Extras on top, that will be scattered throughout the Redoubt.*
- **Sweeping:** **Protected** — *The PCs will be protected, usually by an Ally and/or Location from a Warp (The Ends, The Fray & The Snag) that ***does*** affect the other side(s) of the Conflict.*
- **History Context:** **Defence** — *The Shield indicates that before the current situation there was a suitable defensive force that defended everyone from this precise problem, what happened to it is probably the source of the current situation.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Defend** — *The Shield indicates that the current situation will require you to defend yourself, often from a more powerful opponent, although you usually have an advantage in defences.*
- **Solution:** **Guard** — *The Shield indicates that the solution to the current situation is to guard and keep watch, the enemy will reveal themselves eventually, and you must be ready.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **The Story** — The information is about the current situation and plot. It might include information about one side of the Conflict, all the sides and anything in between. It is normal when providing information to always reveal information about the Dominant Side as well as any others.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Defences.** — The information is about the defences of the Embodiment. Do they always have an anti-stab vest, or security detail, is the location a veritable fortress, or does the computer have an AI Firewall?
  - *Vector (How):* **Device** — The information is presented by or in a device of sort sort. This might be the shield of a knight baring his coat of arms, or its modern equivalent the unit patch, or the device may be a scanner, radio, or often now a phone that provides the information.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Privileges** — The information is about the privileges that the Embodiment enjoys. Whether they are from a rich family, inherited land, had a good education, were not rejected by society despite their crimes, or whether they are trusted with a hall pass because of their status as head of some club or another. Privileges were originally private laws that effectively protect an individual or select group, often granting then immunity or exemption from another law.
  - *Detail:* **Ambient** — Ambient details are atmospheric touches and subtle clues about the Embodiment. They are the hints of lilly scent on the air near the vampire, the shadows deepening as the demon draws breath, or the ultra-high pitched electronic whine that only children and animals can hear, that the otherwise perfect android makes.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Complicated** — *Stress can interfere with your life, making it more complicated in every way. Self-sabotage can play a part too, stress acts against our better judgement and instincts at times, interfering in what we do.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Actions in the suited Facets (Burden, Dominion, Fury, Rook, Sin, or Wyrd) add a Difficulty equal to the Character’s current Stress in Tao Sway. If a penalised Action Succeeds it will reduce the Character’s Stress by 1.
- **Trauma Type:** **Defence Mechanism** — *Trauma can occasionally be helpful, just as surgery is a case of trauma excising some weakness of the body, so too can trauma remove a perceived weakness of the mind. Characters who are used to certain forms of abuse or trauma will develop coping and defence mechanisms, some of which are more effective than others.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma teaches the Character a new Defence Mechanism that they may employ in future. [t13ne type="table" array="psychologicalDefences" /]. When the Character uses this preferred psychological Defence mechanism they will Relieve 1 Stress automatically.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Protection** — *An Age of Protection is occurring. During this age the society acts to create a safe environment for itself, its members and its allies. Protective societies often include social outreach, charities for the poor and disadvantaged (at least members, sometimes allies), as well as socialised, and sometimes professional, military and health care (usually).*
  > **Age Rules:** During the Age of Protection the society attempts to protect itself, its members and its allies, often by building defences, obstacles, training guards or police etc.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Inn** — *The Inn card represents the den of iniquity, impropriety, sins and vices that every society has. The Inn may simply be the place that exotic outsiders stay in town, but more often it is the place of shady deals, criminal and immoral activities and recreational intoxicants. It offers satisfaction, comfort, protection, anonymity, and a certain lack of consequences to those who visit, with hints of adventure, rebelliousness, risk, and even danger for those that seek it.*
- **Hook Event:** **The Stranger Arrives** — *The Story opens with a stranger arriving in town. This person may be a Rival or an Embodiment, but more normally is some unknown Ally who brings the PCs some warning or message about a threat beyond the town.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **I-Ching** — *The Hook targets the Character's I-Ching. For example: If a Character has the Cauldron (50) I-Ching, then the Hook needs to have someone hungry turn up asking for help.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Dark Territory (a Twist riddled Location)*
- **The Fray:** **Social Drinking** — *The Fray involves a complex social Ordeal, usually of Medium Stakes, although higher and lower Stakes versions are possible. There will be drinks, usually alcoholic, which may be replaced or augmented by other recreational drugs. This Fray is social and allows Characters to alter their social status with other Characters, earning respect, friendship or enmity as appropriate.*
- **The Snag:** **Illegal or Immoral** — *Something about the situation is either Illegal or Immoral, ideally it should be a choice between immoral or illegal (such as keeping slaves being immoral, but freeing slaves being illegal).*
- **Sweeping:** **Party!** — *The PCs may have cause to celebrate from recent events, more likely they want to distract themselves from recent events with a celebration. There will be drinks, there will be music or games, and there is usually food (often cake). It may be a Character's birthday, or a similar anniversary, or a feast or festival.*
- **History Context:** **Visited** — *The Inn indicates that in the history of the current situation there was a visitation, someone came, or went and visited somewhere. This visit may have been a vacation, a business trip, research, some family emergency, or may even have supernatural or alien connotations.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Hospitality** — *The Inn indicates that the current situation is one where either you find yourself receiving guests, or you are a guest. This situation can be pleasant or stressful as you may be unfamiliar with each other, when there may be culture shocks on both sides, or you may be too familiar with each other, which can create its own stresses. Hospitality always has its limits, although different lines will be drawn in different cultures.*
- **Solution:** **Immorality** — *The Inn indicates that the solution to the current situation lies in the immoral. Sometimes this may mean contacting the "criminal underworld" to make illegal purchases, other times it means not making it home that night.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Pleasures** — The information is about a pleasure that is available. This might be a particular food, drug, or experience unique to the Location or Story, or could be information about some common pleasure.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Pleasures** — Information about what an Embodiment likes to do with their time is always useful. Sometimes we can learn more from someone's Vices than from their words.
  - *Vector (How):* **Advertisement** — Adverts are everywhere in our society and always conveying some information to us. But this one is different, maybe its a private ad, simply saying "Safety not guaranteed", or perhaps it's the yells of a street vendor, telling us of distant lands and the quality of their silks and spices. Then again news vendors yelling out "click-bait" headlines is as old as printing.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Sympathetic** — The information covers the reasons the bad guy is doing what he is doing and it turns out that, yeah, he might be doing the right thing after all. Maybe he is leading an army invading the country because his twin sister has been kidnapped by the king, or perhaps the reason he butchers the desert snake tribes *that way* is because he was raised by a nest of serpent people hiding in the capital city, after they slaughtered his family in front of him.
  - *Detail:* **Mistake** — A Mistake detail could be a misspelled word, or an auto-corrected text, or it could be a deliberate mistake. So are the outlaws you're tracking inside the barn and forgot to post a rear guard, or are they well aware you are out here and have deliberately left a way in?

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Monsters** — *Stress can make monsters of us all, but worse still Stress can make Monsters, not just mountains, out of molehills.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character must pay Chi equal to the Character’s current Stress as Yarn. This Chi is used to purchase a new Character that can be a Force-of-Nature Extra with Monster Facets (Burden, Dominion, Fury, Rook, Sin, or Wyrd). These "Monsters" may actually be physical issues such as natural disasters, or they may be aspects of the Characters own Psyche, given hallucinatory or supernatural form. Then again they can be the Character themselves, unaware in their Stress induced black-outs what they are doing. Other times the Character will project their problems on an actual Monster, tying their Arcs together. The only catharsis available to a Character here is to defeat, or at least survive the Monster, which will relieve Stress up to Monster Facet Boon.
- **Trauma Type:** **Temptation** — *Trauma can create all sorts of problems for Characters, and can place them in situations where they discover things about themselves that even they never suspected. Sometimes the trauma can be directly responsible for a fetish, addiction, or mania for something, othertimes it may be a reaction to the trauma that leads a Character "astray". Medical treatments, as well as recreational activities can become addicting, or problematical, temptations come from many sources, and new temptations are all the more tempting.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma grants the Character the ability to Relieve 1 Stress by indulging in a temptation, however they can Relieve all current Stress if they indulge in a new temptation. New temptations can, of course, be dangerous as well as tempting, with Stakes ranging from Medium to Paradoxical depending upon the genre and temptation.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Decadence** — *An Age of Decadence is occurring. During this age society revels in immorality, iniquity, sinfulness and wrongdoing, rather than just the excesses of wealth. Although the wealthy will display impropriety too, extravagant debaucheries that rival those of an age of ostentation, for the underclasses of society there is just as much excess in some ways, but it often involves stronger or at least cheaper booze.*
  > **Age Rules:** During the Age of Decadence most members of society will develop at least one additional Hitch that reflects the dark hobbies they are developing during the age.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Færy Noble** — *The F&aelig;ry Noble card stands for the Lords of the Fae. It represents power, of an unearthly, paradoxical, fateful, magical, cruel or capricious nature. It can be a sign of erratic, irrational, or even insane seeming behaviour, but says there's a reason beneath. The F&aelig;ry Noble can be involved in important historical events, but you are just as likely to find one engaged in something mundane (at least from their point of view) such as painting the dawn red to warn shepherds of rain. Then again sometimes the card represents a Leader or Mentor who is "(Fairy-)Touched(-in-the-head)". Here again, the irrationality may be signs of genius, prophecy, or insanity, or a mixture.*
- **Hook Event:** **Danger** — *The Hook just consists of some enormous danger, it isn't looming, it isn't coming, it is right here, right now.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **I-Ching** — *The Hook targets the Character's I-Ching. For example: If a Character has the Cauldron (50) I-Ching, then the Hook needs to have someone hungry turn up asking for help.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Extremely Powerful Yarn-Teller Ally / Artefact Power Descendant*
- **The Fray:** **Magic** — *Maybe there's a Ward, a Runic barrier, an Alchemical Seal, or some dark and sinister Psibertek involved, but magic is in the Fray, and the PCs better have a mage on their side. Magic can come in a lot of different flavours, spells and cantrips, glamours and illusions, necromancy, scrying, summoning, invocations and evocations. This is usually a Low to Paradoxical Opposed, Co-operative or occasionally Solitary Ordeal.*
- **The Snag:** **Changeable** — *The Fray is complicated by a changing or changeable situation. Perhaps a race is occurring in a tidal area, or perhaps the Villain is capricious and changes their plan during the middle of the Fray. Maybe the PCs goals should change mid-way through the Fray, as having promised to win, they are made to promise to lose or vice versa.*
- **Sweeping:** **Heirophant** — *The PCs meet a Heirophant or Prophetess Mentor Character who can explain everything, and usually has some special ability the Character's will require. This is a combined Gain Ally/Training/Lore Descendant and Revelation. Think Obi-Wan or the Oracle.*
- **History Context:** **Destined** — *The F&aelig;ry Noble indicates that the history that leads to the current situation is somehow destined or pre-ordained somehow. It may simply be that the historical record includes portents, signs, omens or fore-shadowing of the current situation.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Madness** — *The F&aelig;ry Noble indicates that the current situation is one that would sound mad if you tried to explain it to someone not involved, this may be due to magic, temporal paradoxes, weird alternate dimensions, supernatural happenings or actual mental illness as required.*
- **Solution:** **Instability** — *The F&aelig;ry Noble indicates that the solution to the current problem is to destabilise it somehow, this may be best achieved through illogical, erratic, capriciousness, impulsive, whimsical, or quirky behaviour, such as you might find among geniuses and lunatics.*

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Dangerous** — *Stress is dangerous, it can turn a simple mistake into a deadly danger, make a simple shock cause a heart attack, or make a Character hold a loaded gun to their own head.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Clubs  (<span class="t13ne-blackcard">♣</span>) Suited Ordeal cards that target the Character for an Attack add the Character’s current Stress as Yarn so 13 Stress adds 66 Pips to each card, but then relieves Stress equal to the original Card Pips, plus all Strain and current Shock (although this new attack is a whole other ball game). Unsuited cards add current Stress as Chi (13 Pips), but remove only the current Stress Reduced (4 Stress), 1 Strain or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Sorcery** — *Trauma can unlock abilities that the Character had no idea could even exist, let alone that they had access to them. Usually the Trauma will unlock some latent magical, or mutatagenic, power, this is not often a mental or emotional ability, but some physical effect usually. Of couse, genre and setting will greatly effect what "sorcery" can be unlocked by trauma. Of course, in certain genres and settings any useage of "Magic" can appear delusional, and even in fantasy settings some mages are considered crazy for the things they attempt with their magic.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma unlocks a Character’s "magical potential", typically this means that one of their Annexes can access "Magic" Boldly (as Chi). They will calculate Difficulty as though using magic (which is often easier). The Character with this Trauma can Relieve 1 Stress by laughing maniacally as they use their magic, regardless of the consequences.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Magic** — *An Age of Magic is occurring. During this age there is within a society some belief in magic. This is not necessarily a Mythic age that actually contains magic, although that can occur too, but more a rejection of materialistic mainstream knowledge, particuarly sciences. Magical thinking and beliefs may include fantastical or just misapplied mystical or scientific concepts, that can have positive and negative effects upon the society. Shared rituals, experiences and beliefs can draw a community closer together, but lead to irrational decision-making, social stagnation, and extremism.*
  > **Age Rules:** Magical thinking and beliefs are accepted within the society, often causing a rejection of materialistic scientific beliefs and counter-evidence. Often accompanied by a profound change in the society, often with a shift towards more popularist and extremist politics.
