# Card Guide: Wildcards

This guide provides the complete mechanical, narrative, and Yarn specifications for all 2 cards of the Wildcards suit in the T13 Narrative Game Engine.

## 🃏 Red Joker of Wildcard

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **15**
- **Wound Type:** **Carnage+** *(Wound Level 7)*
- **Associated Facets:** *All*
- **General Meaning:** *Jokers signify creativity, humour and surprises, both good and bad.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Wildcard**
- **Normal Position:** *May be played as any Non-Court card (or as an Ordeal Card Joker with +22 Pips of Liberty Penetration)*
- **Crossed Position:** *May be played as any Non-Court card Crossed*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Rainbow**
- **Bulmas Type:** **All**
- **Animal Category:** *Any*
- **Example Animal:** *Can create new paths that exploit new niches, rather like Broccen digging paths into the Lea.*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Skin Patterns / Coloured Feathers / Camouflage / Chromatophores*
- **Lea Notes:** *These paths open and close naturally through the Lea from time to time, they are easily walked by all Bulmäs, but Vermis and All-Walkers may walk them in Swarm or Dragon configurations.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *12*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Low-Soul*
- **Test:** *Luck (Liberty)*
- **Obstacles:** *3 Obstacles per Non-Court Stage, 4 per Court Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Anything*
- **Stage:** *The way ahead is chaos-ridden. Perhaps bullets strafe the landscape, or shells explode across No-man's land, or maybe the waters swirl in huge whirlpool. Then again perhaps it is just a swirl of litter and flashing lights that blocks the way.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Race around the Stages of the Ordeal repeatedly. Often a vehicle race, but it can be any form as required.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *Shots riddle the air, zinging from the stones of the ground and ricocheting through the melee. Anyone on the Battlefield will be Wounded by a random Unsoakable Extreme Stakes Wound card, every Phase, until one side are all dead or everyone is out of ammo.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *For whatever reason during the combat actual damage is unlikely, perhaps the combatants are Mech-pilots, or these are two immortals crossing swords. The real contest is in the barbs, insults and quips that they make. The right insult, in this context, can be deadly.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The World** — *The World card represents the whole world, not just the Earth, or any particular planet, or dimension but the Omniverse as a whole. In fact, any wholeness, or completion is indicated by the card, as well as the wilderness and wild places in particular (due to the wild-card nature of the Joker). The World is generally seen as a positive card, but it can be negative in the sense of endings mean farewells, good byes and moving on from one phase to another.*
- **Hook Event:** **Wildcard** — *The Hook is just one of those out there crazy starts to a story. Perhaps they come around on a beach near a crashed plane, or wrecked boat, with no memories of where they were going or why. Or maybe they explode from the ground, unsure how or why they were there.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Add Alternate*
- **The Fray:** **Wilderness Encounter** — *A holdover from Old School Gaming. The encounter is a where the PCs meet a larger group of inferior foes (for example 2d20 Vexes, or 2d6 Cast Extras) in the Wild. Encounters are not always balanced, and the PCs may have Flee turning the High Stakes Ordeal into a Chase. Encounters are usually followed by a Gain.*
- **The Snag:** **Double Trouble** — *Add an additional Fray that must be accomplished alongside the first.*
- **Sweeping:** **Exologue** — *The Sweeping is an Exologue, where the Character spends a Short Act of a few Scenes in the world of one of their Alternates. This can be used to allow the Pressed Embodiment recovery time in a different time-line (another universe, a past life, etc).*
- **History Context:** **World** — *The World indicates that the current situation has been caused by major events in world history, such as world-wars, imperial collapses, technological innovations, and natural disasters such as climate change and global pandemics, these can even include events from a cosmic scale, such as planetary collisions, gamma-ray bursts and solar-flares.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Completion** — *The World indicates that the current situation is one of completion, whatever the situation, it is already too late to stop it. A change is coming and it is this change and how it is weathered that is the real situation here that may be "solved".*
- **Solution:** **Finish** — *The World indicates that the solution to the current situation lies in finishing some task or quest that is already under way. Examples include having to finish your prototype, completing you journey, or finding all the remaining magic crystals.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Hidden Influences** — There are Hidden influences in any universe. Sometimes they are the Increated working behind the scenes, other times they are the manipulative Faeries, ensuring the Doom of Wyrd, still other groups include time-travellers and inter-dimensional visitors who have all their own agendas. This Revelation reveals something about one of these groups.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Ref Special** — The Yarn cards can't decide everything that is happening, this one is all down to the Ref to decide.
  - *Vector (How):* **Mystical** — The information arrives in a mystical or via magickal means. This might be the result of scrying or a message spell.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Quest** — The Revelation is actually a Quest that must be followed to find out the important information.
  - *Detail:* **Wild** — Wild details might be details about how wild the situation or a Character is or may just be wild card details, randomly providing truths and lies across the board.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Berserk** — *Stress can cause a Character to snap, becoming a furious engine of destruction. This creates a glorious moment of cathartic release, with the Character burning the Stress to augment their damage, as the Character attacks friend and foe alike, relieving huge amounts of Stress. However, the repercussions of this event may create more Stress than is lost.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Ordeal cards played that attack another Character or Descendant add current Stress as Chi to the Pips of the Attack and reduce the Stress by 1 until the Character has no Stress remaining. However, after the dust has settled the Character will gain Stress equal to highest Character or Descendant type (or the total Group Size whichever is higher) injured or destroyed in Chi. E.g. A Berserker that killed a Hero or almost 100 Extras and Grunts would add 12 Stress after the Berserk had finished.
- **Trauma Type:** **Worldly** — *Trauma will sometimes change everything or anything about a Character, and sometimes Trauma will pass without any repercussions, this is one of those times.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** This Trauma instantly Relieves all Stress. This Stress is used as Chi to remove any effects from the Trauma.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Completion** — *An Age of Completion is occurring. During this age a society riches the end of their story. Typically the society accomplishes some great task, raising a monument for all the ages, crossing some "impassable" barrier such as sailing first across an ocean, conquering some ancient empire and in this great accomplishment, the society then seems to vanish from history. Whatever happened to this society history will not record it, although often that just means that the society changes name, or breaks into a number of smaller societies, occasionally this society collapses, its task complete, the goal accomplished, the society loses cohesion, and the members simply fade away, joining neighbouring societies, or creating a new one.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Completion a society completes some great task, and then collapses, this collapse may be total, partial, or simply a revolution within the society or a change of name. However the story of this society is over.

- **Pips (Value):** **15**

- **Path Color:** **Grey**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Sleeping**
- **Animal Category:** *None*
- **Example Animal:** *A Plant / Fossil*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Bone*
- **Lea Notes:** *The Grey path allows a Bulmäs Soul to sleep in the landscape. Their Soul may live in a skull, horn, tree or fossil for years, millennia or millions of years, until it is passed on, by ingestion of a fruit or nut, or by a Soul-Release ceremony. Fossil Bulmäs include Moon-Staves, Flint Weapons (Axes or Knives) and Elf-shot Armour that can only be worn / controlled by a Bulmäs who have a Soul related to the Fossil Soul.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *12*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Low-Paradoxical*
- **Test:** *Beyond (Yonder)*
- **Obstacles:** *None*
- **Suggested Action:** *None*
- **Stage:** *The way ahead is a void, or huge open space. Maybe there's no cover, maybe not even air. Then again it could just be the space behind the walls, secret passages, or simply a way most people would never go.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *A Chase (or Flee) that goes around through the same Stages again.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Fight is taking place on open terrain, devoid of cover or natural advantages. Only a desperate fool would fight here by choice. Both sides would be best served by retreating now. +1 Pips to any Disengagement Action by either side.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The fight consists of waves of combatants. Each Round a new Wave begins, a Round ends automatically if all Combatants on one side are incapacitated. ### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Beyond** — *The Beyond card represents that which is outside. In many ways it is the opposite of the World, as that card implies a wholeness and completeness, the Beyond tells us that even the Omniverse has its impossibilities. The Beyond can directly represent unknown factors, or the factors from beyond creation that are called the Increated. The Beyond can also represent the Supernatural, as anything beyond nature is within the bounds of the Beyond.*
- **Hook Event:** **Wildcard** — *The Hook is just one of those out there crazy starts to a story. Perhaps they come around on a beach near a crashed plane, or wrecked boat, with no memories of where they were going or why.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Add Alternate*
- **The Fray:** **Wandering Monster** — *A holdover from Old School Gaming. The encounter is against a random Monster (Force of Nature Extra or similar powerful individual) or a group of similar powered Characters (Cast Extras) or a large group (2 to 1 outnumbering) Chorus (Extras) or a tide of Vex Extras.*
- **The Snag:** **Triple Threat** — *A Triple Threat Draws and Plays an additional 2 Cards one as a Second Fray Ordeal and one as a Snag*
- **Sweeping:** **Interlogue** — *The Sweeping is an Interlogue, where we cut away from the Pressed Embodiment to find out what is happening to some other Characters usually in a different Plot, but it can be a Scene or Two for the Dominant Embodiment.*
- **History Context:** **Supernatural** — *The Beyond indicates that the events that have led to the current situation were not natural in nature, some supernatural, preternatural, spiritual, divine, mystical, spectral, magical, occult or other-worldly force (often the Increated or F&aelig;) are involved.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Unlimited** — *The Beyond indicates that the current situation is unlimited, it could destroy the universe, unmake anything, or change everything. It is an ultimate threat and often appears unstoppable, although usually there is a solution.*
- **Solution:** **Outside** — *The Beyond indicates that the solution to the situation lies outside the current situation, this may mean a trip outside the affected area is required, or that the solution lies with some outsider who is new to the area, occasionally it means that no solution will be found without resorting to some kind of time-travel, parallel universes, or magical shenanigans.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Magical / Divine Influences** — Gods and magicians walk amongst us, at least in the Omniverse they do. The gods have great power, but are often limited in how they can apply it, magicians are not so restricted, but don't have as much power. Anyway this card allows you to reveal something about how these mages and gods are reacting to events in the Story.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Player Decides** — So there's something the Players would really want to know (like who is out to kill them, and why, and what they need to do about it, but then the Player's choose which one to know.
  - *Vector (How):* **Direct** — Direct Revelations are where the Villain monologues his plan to the captured hero, demonstrates his super-weapon to the United Nations, or shape-shifts in front of the Characters for the first time. Confessions, suicide notes, scientific papers, mission logs and journal and press conferences can all be Direct Revelations too. I'm sure you can think of others.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Story** — The information contains the entire story, within the story (or play within the play), if anyone can see.
  - *Detail:* **Haphazard** — A random collection of lies and truths that weirdly seem to tie into each other.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Narrative Incursion** — *Stress can break a Character, ruining their tales, derailing their arcs and upsetting Plots that have Embodied in them. Then again sometimes the Plots that are working behind the Scenes can decide to intervene and keep a Character on track, throwing some narrative event at the Character that forces them to confront their own faults and deal with their Stress.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character immediately spends all current Stress increasing the Tension Level of their personal Arc one Level. This will usually bring on a Scene from that Plot that plays out following the the current Scene directly.
- **Trauma Type:** **Beyond** — *Trauma can change a Character in really vital ways, like their vitality, this Trauma kills them.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma immediately Relieves all Stress into Chi. Then the Character "dies", releasing all their Sway as a Yarn-burst, and then moves to another layer of reality, or alters their Incarna as required (See Death and Immortality Rules).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Incursion** — *An Age of Incursion is occurring. During this age a society is infiltrated from beyond, usually this will be simply some foreign power, but in some genres this may be a cross-temporal / hyper-geometric / inter-dimensional / extraterrestrial / ultraterrestrial / supernatural / spiritual / divine / demonic or Increated incursion. The Incursion can be an expedition, infiltration, intrusion, raid, abduction, invasion, usurpation, or just a vacation.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Incursion some force from beyond, either the society, nature or even the genre, appears within the society. This incursion can be for any purpose, but represents some exterior unknowable force. When in doubt use any crazy idea, you can always say it was an Increated that nightmare fueled off a nearby insane person later, if necessaary. Incursions can bring changes to not just the society, but the nature of reality itself, as time-travellers, gods or sleeping dragons awaken and emerge into the society.
