# Facet Sway

When Tao Yin or Tao Yang is stored on a Facet it is changed to become Facet Sway. Each Facet has its own name that it calls its own Sway, and a special optional rule that may be used. We also divide Facet Sway into types based on game rules that define how easily Facet Sway and Tao Sway may be changed, as well as how "powerful" the Sway is perceived to be.

## Facet Sway Potency

* **Banal** — Facet Sway is just a name of Tao Sway and uses no optional rules. In many conditions, without suitable tools, Facet Sway will count as Banal, spotting a clue could have a Difficulty as high as 45 Yang Sway.
* **Intrepid** —  Intrepid Facet Sway are usually referred to by name as they are more powerful than Tao Sway, being half as powerful as Chi exactly. What each Facet Sway can do as Intrepid may vary by genre and era, but largely when specified as Facet Sway the game means Intrepid. For example, Spotting a Clue is always at most 20 Insight.
* **Bold** — Bold Facet Sway is the most powerful form of Facet Sway, and is typical of a Facet being in a Golden Age, die to technology, magic or other forces at play in the setting. Bold Facet Sway behaves as Chi (adding +2 per point and calculating Difficulties as Chi). The individual Facet Sways are always named individually and have special rules for their use if they are Bold. Spotting a clue becomes at most 10 Bold Insight.
* **Monstrous** — Monstrous Facet Sway behaves as Yarn when used to add to Scores and calculate Difficulties. Monstrous Facet Sway are always called by their individual names and are normally only available to characters with an appropriate Monster Facet. Spotting a Clue is always at most only 3 Monstrous Insight.


### Sway by Facet

#### Awe (Sway)
Awe Sway is known as Splendour, and represents beauty, nobility and dignity. Splendour is best spent when you are trying to impress someone, keep your cool, or maintain someone’s respect.

#### Burden (Sway)
Burden Sway is known as Wealth. Wealth is spent to make purchases (such as Descendants). In other words, if you can spend cash on something Wealth is the best Sway.

#### Craft (Sway)
Craft Sway points are called Knacks. Knacks can be spent during physical Descendant Mending, Fixing and Creation Ordeals (to help meet Costs and Difficulties), or on any Skill Annex use.

#### Dominion (Sway)
Dominion Sway is sometimes called Support. Support can be spent to affect people socially, including when it is used to influence or affect a group of people. i.e. When spending 4 Bold Support on the size of a Group this counts as 4 Chi and can influence or affect up to 9 people (instead of 4 for Intrepid Support)

#### Enigma (Sway)
Enigma Sway is sometimes known as Covers, which can be spent to keep secrets and keep things hidden from view.

#### Fury (Sway)
Fury Sway is known as Surges, and can be spent on emotional effects, such as floods of emotion, surges of strength, shrugging off damage with a surge of adrenaline, or powering psionics.

#### Gossamer (Sway)
Gossamer Sway is also known as Breath, and Breath can be spent as Chi spent on conversations, and physical exertion (such as moving large objects).

#### Heresy (Sway)
Heresy Sway is also sometimes known as Guises. Guises can be spent to hide truths, make lies believable, creating, or maintaining, a fiction.

#### Inertia (Sway)
Inertia Sway is sometimes known as Endurance, and Endurance can be spent on any act of resistance or keeping going, from walking for hours, surviving torture without breaking, to ignoring wounds while crawling to safety.

#### Jeer (Sway)
Jeer Sway points are sometimes called Cheers. Cheers can be spent whenever your actions have an audience who can react emotionally whether with a cheer, clap, jeer, boo or laugh.

#### Key (Sway)
Key Sway is sometimes called Insight, and Insight can be spent to gain access to places and information, knowledge of things that a Character perceives, as well as realising where the weak spot in armour is to gain Penetration.

#### Liberty (Sway)
Liberty Sway are sometimes known as Hopes (or sometimes Dreams). Hopes may be spent on actions to do with achieving or having dreams, freedom, and not succumbing to despair.

#### Miasma (Sway)
Miasma Sway is also sometimes called Taint. Taint can be spent on actions that try to corrupt, infect, poison (including envenoming), transform, or blight.

#### Nature (Sway)
Nature Sway is sometimes called Health. Health can be spent on active pursuits, such as tiring work, or exercise, as well as healing Wounds and fighting diseases.

#### Orthodox (Sway)
Orthodox Sway are known as Facts. Facts can be spent on any action that tries to demonstrate or exert knowledge.

#### Phoenix (Sway)
Phoenix Sway are sometimes called Fevers. Fevers can be spent on any action performed with passion, regenerating wounds or fighting off diseases and toxins.

#### Quiet (Sway)
Quiet Sway is also called Serenity, and can be spent on any attempt to keep calm, stay silent, or think deep thoughts.

#### Rook (Sway)
Rook Sway, also known as Securities, can be spent on defences, blocking attacks and neutralising strategies.

#### Sin (Sway)
Sin Sway points are known sometimes as Lapses. Lapses can be spent on actions to tempt others, or that a Character should feel guilty about.

#### Trial (Sway)
Trial Sway is sometimes known as Plays. Plays can be spent on any action that competes with others, especially when attacking them directly.

#### Virtue (Sway)
Virtue Sway is sometimes called Merits. Merits are spent on actions to convert others, particularly changing minds and opinions and catching the attention of the divine for transformation through prayer.

#### Wyrd (Sway)
Wyrd Sway are also known as Axioms. Axioms can be spent on actions to law-make, impose laws, use judgement, or make rulings.

#### Yonder (Sway)
Yonder Sway points are known as Scopes. Scopes can be spent to act at a distance, this could be as a Range, such as long distance communication, throwing, archery or shooting, or as a Reach such as a Descendant has, that might allow you to extend an Annex’s Reach. Usually the difference between when to uses Scopes as Reach and when to use them as Range should be obvious.

#### Zeal (Sway)
Zeal Sway is also called Verve. Verve can be spent on any action that causes a change in position, mind, speed, direction, heart, or approach.



## Facet to Facet Conversion

Sometimes you want to convert Sway between different Facet Sway. How this happens is based on the genre of the Game and / or Story, as follows.

* **Mercurial** — Facet Sway and Tao Sway can be converted between types without cost. This is often how divine beings treat all Sway, generally, effectively converting lowly Tao Sway into Intrepid, Bold or Monstrous Facet Sway as required.
* **Færy** — Fairy folk and their hybrids are generally able to transfer any Facet Sway between two Facets instantly and freely as long as they are both of the same Tao type (Yin for Yin, Yang for Yang). Insight can become Splendour for free, but conversion to any Yin Tao would require filtering through Chi (see Fixed).
* **Transferable** — Facet Sway can be used as Tao Sway when it matches Yin or Yang to the Facets Yin or Yang, e.g. Awe’s Splendour can be spent as Banal generic Yang Tao Sway on a Trial action. Conversion of Facet Sway to Tao Sway usually costs 1 Facet Sway, so spending 4 Splendour would add 3 Yang. This is the standard for most games.
* **Fixed** — Facet Sway cannot be converted between Facets or directly back into Tao Sway, but must be converted to Chi and back, depending upon the Chi conversion system used this can be expensive. This is the only way that Increated can ever transfer Sway unless they have Pact-bonded. It can also make for tense survival games (or Stories) where resources must be very carefully managed.

Facet sway points can be treated differently depending upon the Game, Character and Setting rules. For example, in games with Fæ it is not uncommon for Axioms to be used to empower magic as though the Axioms were Bold, in most settings Axioms only behave this way when working with laws. In a rough and tumble frontier setting, Axioms may only be Intrepid for law-enforcement, and in a lawless super-villain game we might would expect Axioms to be treated as Banal.

The Narrative as well as the setting can effect which Facet Sway we consider Banal, Intrepid, or Bold. If the Conflict involves Dominion oppressing Nature then it would be reasonable for Dominion’s Support to be Intrepid or even Bold, where as Nature’s Health may be treated as Intrepid (giving it High-powered Healing abilities) or even Banal (making it no better at Healing than Support).

## Generating Facet Sway

Creating Facet Sway is as easy as storing Tao Sway that way, but a die roll typically is Banal creating only Tao Sway ordinarily (+1 Score is 1 Tao Sway). Under certain circumstances however it may be necessary to generate a Facet Sway Score directly from the roll. When this happens the Difficulty to create the Facet Sway and the amount created will vary by the type of the Facet Sway and the Type of Facet to Facet conversion that the Character or Story uses.

|  | **Mercurial** | **Færy Tao Match / Not Matched** | **Transferrable** | **Fixed** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Banal** | Diff 2 | Diff 2 / 5 | Diff 2 | Diff 5 |
| **Intrepid** | Diff 2 | Diff 2 / 5 | Diff 3 | Diff 5 |
| **Bold** | Diff 1 | Diff 1 / 2 | Diff 1 | Diff 2 |
| **Monstrous** | Diff &frac12; | Diff &frac12; / 1 | Diff &frac12; | Diff 1 |
