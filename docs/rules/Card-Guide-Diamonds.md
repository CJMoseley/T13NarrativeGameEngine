# Card Guide: Diamonds

This guide provides the complete mechanical, narrative, and Yarn specifications for all 13 cards of the Diamonds suit in the T13 Narrative Game Engine.

## 🃁 Ace of Diamonds

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **14**
- **Wound Type:** **Carnage** *(Wound Level 6)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Awe, Craft, Orthodox, Quiet, Virtue, Zeal*
- **General Meaning:** *Aces signify beginnings, meetings and unions. They can indicate love-interests, chance encounters, alliances, and friendships.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Success**
- **Normal Position:** *+2 Success Levels while atop Target's pile.*
- **Crossed Position:** *+2 Failure Levels while atop the Target's pile*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Brown/Blue Omnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *All-path*
- **Example Animal:** *All-Walkers*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Soul*
- **Lea Notes:** *The Paths of the All-Walkers can allow Bulmäs to do extraordinary things, like walking an individual’s life-path, so that all of their personal history is known to them. All-Walkers can assume even the forms and souls of other people by using these paths, without directly eating of the Soul. All-Walkers can even Doom-Weave or Yarn-Tell and play other cards from their Hand as Wyrd Tarot cards or Yarn cards, once they have played an Ace on themselves.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Solitary Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *6*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Low-Medium-High-Extreme*
- **Test:** *Collect (Burden)*
- **Obstacles:** *3 Obstacles in every Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Acquire*
- **Stage:** *Something needs to be done at speed, maybe its a skid, shooting rapids, speed typing, rapid mental arithmetic, dashing through a closing gate, or speed reading.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *The Ordeal is meant to be done with haste and speed. Get somewhere or do something as quickly as possible, through a dangerous area or dangerous situations.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Flowing side moves to the Flanks of the Ebbing enemy, able to attack ignoring active defences.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *Ambushed by a group of weaker foes (often Chorus or Vex Extras), with numbers greater then those they attack, usually equivalent weapons and armour to the PCs, they may attempt to retreat after the third Phase.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Ambition** — *The Card of new Ambitions, sudden monetary gains, new business opportunities, new treasures or rumours of treasures and money being made. There may be conflict and collisions, new obstacles in the way or a Rival's ambitions may block progress.*
- **Hook Event:** **Opportunity** — *The Story hooks Characters by offering them an opportunity to Gain something they want or need to fulfil an ambition.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Geometry: Goal** — *The Hook targets the Character's Geometry by appealing to the Geometry Goal. This is often tied to a Character&apos; personal ambitions.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Ultimate Power (A Super-Skill Descendant / An Artefact)*
- **The Fray:** **Quest** — *The Fray is a Quest, usually based on the Facets of the Conflict, for something the PC requires or desires.*
- **The Snag:** **Falling Behind** — *The Character's Rival or Opposition makes a Gain (or just advances to the next Stage of a larger Plot's Ordeal). You may Play another Card as a Gain, or use this card's *Ultimate Power*.*
- **Sweeping:** **Win** — *The Pressed side has a Win, this may be a battle against a weak or weakened foe, or they might catch a lucky break, or lottery win. The Win doesn't have to be big, just enough to keep hope alive, but a Gain is in order. Perhaps even the Resolution of a Hitch.*
- **History Context:** **Glory** — *The past is coloured by previous successes. This is not usually just nostalgia, but some past glory that has been lost or ignored.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Rivalry** — *The situation of Ambition is that your ambitions are rivalled by the ambitions of others. Either someone wants the opposite of what you want, or they want the exact same goal, but if they have it you will not (such as wanting the same land, descendant or treasure).*
- **Solution:** **Objectives** — *The way out of this situation involves setting small, accomplishable objectives. By breaking big tasks and broad ambitions into smaller steps and practicable goals.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **World-Building Ambition** — The information is about the background, or setting, not the current plot or situation. It might include pertinent details for the current plot, but is usually just information about a (somewhat) common ambition, aspiration, or hope among the character's peers. The Yarn-Teller that played the card should specify what this ambition is (Holo-Star, Splice-Hockey Player, Dragon-rider etc).
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Ambition** — Information that is about the ambitions of the Embodiment.
  - *Vector (How):* **Source** — A Whistle-blower or some credible source for the information is found (like an old article), or a turncoat from the enemy, who tells you it clearly, but privately. They probably want to keep their identity secret, or certainly protected, but maybe not, perhaps they wish to be paid, or need something else from the Character.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Incarna** — The information is about the Incarna of someone or something. If the Incarna is specified it is usually because it is special. So probably not Flesh and Blood...
  - *Detail:* **Weakness** — A weakness detail may be a Hitch, or some Umbral condition, or just knowledge of a low Facet Boon.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Indifference** — *Sometimes a Character will become so detached and disinterested, that it impacts their ability to feel or care even slightly. In some cases this indifference is nothing more than a brief apathy, a limited lack of drive or moral guidance that will soon pass, but for others this coldness may be a lasting problem.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character must discard all Diamonds (<span class="t13ne-redcard">♦</span>) suited cards from Ordeal Pool, Style Reserves and Wyrd Tarot Hand, this continues with automatic Discarding of all Diamonds until they have zero Stress, Strain and Shock, when the Indifference Effect will end. Each Diamond (<span class="t13ne-redcard">♦</span>) Card Discarded removes Pips Stress or 1 Shock or 1 Strain from a Character or Descendant.
- **Trauma Type:** **Obsession** — *Shocks and Traumas can cause a mind to become obsessed with a particular, persistent thought, idea, impulse, or image. These thoughts are experienced as intrusive thoughts, and are often inappropriate to the current situation, and can seem so intrusive as to seem alien. Characters can often become Stressed by these thoughts and agitated when they occur.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates a specific thought, which is given a rating between 1 and 6 (may be randomly rolled) to determine the "severity" or "inappropriateness" of the thought, image or impulse. A 1 would represent a recurring distraction, a 6 would represent a deeply disturbing image flashing to mind. Obsessions move at random through the Psychosocial space (using a d12 or d8 for directions), when they are in an Unconscious Space they can be ignored, but upon entering a conscious space (such as Thoughts) then the Obsession will immediately cause Stress equal to the "Severity" as the thought appears again.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Opportunity** — *An Age of Opportunity is occuring. Opportunity can come from a lot of different sources, historical examples include, expansion into uninhabited territories, colonization, access to new or better goods and services, creation of faster trade routes, gold (and other) rushes, technological developments, popular social movements, cultural shifts, and they generally follow the rules of supply and demand.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Opportunity multiple new goods and services will appear, along with a demand for each good and service. All Suppliers will be in competition to fulfil the same demands. For the suppliers the goal is to corner as large a proportion of all demands as possible. Securing all of a demand creates a monopoly, an ideal situation for a supplier, but not for the economy, which will often stagnate. Technologically there is always a demand for a "Better ..." that can break any monopoly.

## 🃂 Two of Diamonds

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **2**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Awe*
- **General Meaning:** *Twos signify conflict, opposition and choices. They can indicate crossroads, partings, separations, and changes of direction.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Foe**
- **Normal Position:** *Player and target are "foes". The lowest Interaction Type possible.*
- **Crossed Position:** *Player and target move to a higher, more positive Interaction Type (by one level).*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Brown/Blue Omnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *Microfauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Plankton / Zooplankton / Tardigrade*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Tendrils/Tentacles*
- **Lea Notes:** *Microscopic animal and bacterial paths, often overlooked.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Solitary Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *1*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Low*
- **Test:** *Scare (Awe)*
- **Obstacles:** *Usually none*
- **Suggested Action:** *Intimidate*
- **Stage:** *A short, straight path, street or road, simple preparation or research*
- **Motional Aspect:** *The Ordeal is meant to be done quickly, along the ground in, or on, a vehicle.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing foe falls back (+1 Pips to movement or Disengament actions), and the Flowing forces push forward into the breach (+1 Pips for movement and defence).*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *An aggressive, solitary Vex attacks, usually with a Duck Psych Attack, or a Toxic sting, then it will retreat after the first Phase, if it survives.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Wraith** — *Whether an expression of the supernatural or simply the psychological forces at play. The card is about People's Emotions and especially Fears. These can be as simple as a fear of missing out, or falling behind others, or expressions of deeper phobias. It is a card of small gains and intense, but limited resistance.*
- **Hook Event:** **Banshee's Wail** — *The Story opens with a warning, usually the warning is a Plot Revelation, but it may traditionally be a warning of a death in the family. The point is to make the PC's a little more afraid.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona: Shadow** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona, specifically by appealing to the Shadow of their Persona.*
- **Gain Reward:** *A single coin, suitable for paying a ferryman (Yin/Yang Gain).*
- **The Fray:** **Insubstantial** — *The Fray is an insubstantial, or trifling matter, but failure may have profound consequences later. An example may be a single question asked by a co-worker, it seems unimportant at the time, but is actually part of an human resources investigation. Usually a Test on a single Facet (A,C,O,Q,V,Z) (although an appropriate Annex might help). The difficulty is usually quite low (between 1-15). Facet Tests are meant to be quick, easy checks, a simple roll. Failures may involve negative Psyche effects.*
- **The Snag:** **Haunting** — *The Character may see an actual ghost, or may be reminded of a previous situation, drawing comparisons with the current Fray. The Haunting Snag usually has a magical or psychological effect that may often create new Hitches.*
- **Sweeping:** **Boo!** — *A jump scare that isn't harmful to the Characters, like having someone tap on a the window / knock on the door during a tense moment, or having a dog suddenly bark out of sight. Can act to ease Tension, or ratchet up suspense. A Boo! can also be enough to Resolve a Fear Hitch, under the right circumstances.*
- **History Context:** **Haunted** — *Sometimes the past will not let us go. Some moments stay with us forever, haunting our dreams, asking what if we had done something differently. This past event is always present somehow, whether dredged up by others, present in laments and fears that stay with us, or just fondly remembered, for some important reason.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Past** — *The current situation is actually a problem with the past, some old problem that was not properly dealt with has once again raised its head, or some event years ago has now caused this situation.*
- **Solution:** **Courage** — *The situation will only be dealt with by a demonstration of courage, this may mean facing an old fear, or simply resisting this situation come what may.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **World-Building Ghosts** — The information is about the background, or setting, not the current plot or situation. It might include pertinent details for the current plot, but is usually just information about a local ghost story or haunting. The Yarn-Teller that played the card should specify how the ghost story.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Hints** — Not necessarily information, but the wraiths of information that act as pointers in the right direction. There is a lot of crossover with the Clue Wyrd Tarot Card here. Hints are generally a little more direct, (such as "Perhaps a Guild-Mage could be paid to tell you...") while a Clue must be worked out or pieced together.
  - *Vector (How):* **Phantasm** — The information is conveyed by some supernatural means, often a ghost or spirit that appears and brings word of importance (often their own passing). If you are not comfortable with a ghost in your cyberpunk universe, you can always use a Cyberwraith, a digital soul, or transhuman, or phantasms can also be a holographic image, an illusion or hallucination.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Hitch** — The information reveals a Hitch. Often this revealed Hitch is added to a Lore.
  - *Detail:* **Specific** — A specific detail is a tiny, but important details. It might include knowledge that a particular Annex or Descendant has a particular Umbral or Nimbed, or that a Character uses a specific Descendant, Proficiency, etc. Such specificity usually has a reason behind it, that may also be revealed.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Worries** — *Worries make a Character think about their issues, dwelling on what questions or issues are stressing them out. Time and time again their mind will return to their*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character may reduce their Stress, Strain, or Shock by 1 for each statement they make, that relates to their worries. Additionally they may reduce their Stress by spending Diamond Pips equal to the Stress on pacing back and forth, or they may pay Chi equal to their current Stress to remove a point of Shock, Strain or Stress.
- **Trauma Type:** **Flashbacks** — *Shocks and Traumas can create vivid, incredibly strong memories in the mind. Later the mind can replay these traumatic experiences as though they are currently happening, often doing so involuntarily as a result of Stress. These experiences can include delusions, hallucinations and paranoia in addition to simple revelries and nightmares.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates an Involuntary Memory, which consists of the events leading to the trauma and the exact emotions (including the Pips and Limit card of the emotional effects) felt at the time. Whenever the Character becomes Overstressed or reminded of this Traumatic event (by similar events) the memories will replay as though the Character was living through them again, this will include all Emotional states once again affecting them with the same intensity.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Retrospection** — *An Age of Retrospection is occuring. During this age a society dwells upon its past, such times can be both positive and negative for a society. Fashions, for example, often fixate upon some period look, reproducing, or reflecting it in someway, and similar cycles can be seen in popular arts, music, movies and television programming. Even nation states can go through an age of retrospection, with varied results.*
  > **Age Rules:** An age of retrospection can examine the past truthfully and shortly, without harm. Even celebrating a false (or heavily "edited" or "reframed") version of history can be good for a society, no one wants to be the bad guy in their own history. However, dwelling in the past, trying to recreate past glories, undo progress, or "go back to a simpler time" are all doomed to failure, as they usually always represent that time period unrealistically in the first place, glorifying some aspects while ignoring a host of others. If retrospection continues too long the society stagnates and collapses.

## 🃃 Three of Diamonds

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **3**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Craft*
- **General Meaning:** *Threes signify creativity and and communication. They can indicate hope, imagination, inspiration and conversations.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Proficiency**
- **Normal Position:** *Player gains an additional, applicable Proficiency (May fill a slot).*
- **Crossed Position:** *-1 Prof Die on Target's next roll.*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Brown/Blue Omnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *Meiofauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Small Nematode worms / shrimps*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Carapace*
- **Lea Notes:** *A lot of parasite paths at this level*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Solitary Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *1*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Medium*
- **Test:** *Endurance (Inertia)*
- **Obstacles:** *Usually none*
- **Suggested Action:** *Prepare/Repair*
- **Stage:** *A sharp corner, change of direction, preparation or research*
- **Motional Aspect:** *The Ordeal is meant to involve travel quickly in an aerial vehicle, such as a plane, or somewhere high, perhaps swinging from cranes.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing enemy is backed into a corner, they cannot escape, but may prepare siege defences and counter-attacks. The Flowing force should demand surrender.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *Two or more forces that are evenly matched in size and power can form a Stand-off.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Grindstone** — *The Grindstone is the card of hard work, wearing down opposition, or training yourself to a finely sharpened point. It is a card of determined hard-won success for little reward or bitter, grinding defeat.*
- **Hook Event:** **Working Start** — *The Conflict engages with a Character's current task. This is best expressed in TV by the cold start or In Media Res, that involves the Character's day-to-day job.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Power Root** — *The Hook targets the Character's Power, specifically the Root Facet of that power, this may be a chance to increase a Facet Boon, or a promise of a large amount of Facet Sway.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Wage (Normal Chi Gain or payment, may be daily, weekly, monthly or yearly)*
- **The Fray:** **Working** — *The Test is like a Facet Test (A,C,O,Q,V,Z), but the Difficulty is a little higher (Diff 16+). These Tests are simple, and intended to just check some aspect of the Character, they represent a normal day at work, usually.*
- **The Snag:** **Drudgery** — *Boredom is the enemy in this Snag. Whatever the Fray, the Snag is either the Fray or the aftermath is tedious. The Snag will inevitably have a negative Psychological effect, which can leave people despondent, dismayed and even suicidal.*
- **Sweeping:** **Wages** — *A Wage (or bonus) is a single Cash (Chi) Gain, Wages are a Skill Treasure Descendant*
- **History Context:** **Toil** — *The Grindstone indicates a history of hard work, or exploitation, that has lead to this current situation. Either someone has worked long and hard to bring this about, usually alone, but sometimes with others, or someone has got rich off the back of other people's work.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Grind** — *The Grindstone indicates the current situation is wearing you down. This may be the daily grind getting you down, or it may indicate that you are working too hard.*
- **Solution:** **Effort** — *The Grindstone shows the way out of your current predicament involves a lot of hard work, there is a long slog ahead of you before you will make any real progress at all.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **World-Building Work** — The information is about the background, or setting, not the current plot or situation. It might include pertinent details for the current plot, but is usually just information about a fairly common task, job, occupation or occasionally a hobby. It could be mucking out stables, scrubbing dragon scales, emptying night-soil, shovelling coal into a furnace, downloading an update, uploading a social status, or flamacksing the flange-straightener.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Forte** — The information is about what they are good at, this may reveal their profession, occupation, or simply be a warning about a character's strengths.
  - *Vector (How):* **Details** — The information is hidden as easily overlooked details. Like an addressed, but empty envelope, or a circled phone number in the phone book. Such Details sometimes require a "Spot Hidden" Key (or a "Knowledge" Orthodox or Heresy, as appropriate) Test to reveal them. Maybe it won't be noticed until later.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Skills** — The information reveals some knowledge of the Skills available to the Narrative Element. This may simply be a Lore Skill.
  - *Detail:* **Functional** — A functional detail is information about a function of the Narrative Element being revealed. It might include the fact that a particular car make cannot make certain tyre tracks, or that Rumble mountain isn't actually a volcano, but has a huge machine built within it.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Working** — *You can always work through Stress. Sometimes you can just put your head down and get on with things, and when you lift your head something has changed. Sometimes this time is best spent working a physically demanding job, or one that keeps your mind occupied, other times the best way to work through it is a nap.*
  > **Stress Rules:** A Character may remove 1 point of Stress, Strain or Shock by working on, or working at ignoring, their problems for twenty-minutes.
- **Trauma Type:** **Compulsion** — *Shocks and Trauma can make a Character do things, and more importantly want to do things, that they did not want to do before. Compulsions create compulsive behaviours, where even normal behaviours such as exercise, eating, talking, or shopping, become compulsions. Typically the compulsion will be some normal behaviour that is performed to excess, as in binge-eating, or some lack of impulse control, such as over-spending when shopping.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates a compulsion that affects the Character thereafter. This compulsion is given a "Severity" rating between 1 and 6 when created, this may be assigned randomly or based on how dangerous the compulsion is (1 being a mild, or easily overlooked compulsion and a 6 being attention-gathering self-destructive behaviour). The Compulsion then moves at random around the Character’s Psychosocial space. When in the Unconscious mind the compulsion is ignorable and if performed will relieve Stress equal to the Severity, but when in a conscious part of the Mindscape the Character will be compelled to perform the Compulsion or Gain Stress equal to the "Severity" of the Compulsion
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Endeavour** — *An Age of Endeavour is occuring. During this age a society works towards a common goal, not all members will necessarily be directly working on the goal, but they will support the Endeavour of the age in some way. Examples can include building infrastructure, construction of new buildings, towns or cities (although these are sometimes better handled by an age of expansion), development of some new required technology (such as the clock, space race, or sustainable power generation), survival usually of a diaspora, exodus, or migration. During this age the wealth of the society is tied up in the project and the society may appear poorer as a result.*
  > **Age Rules:** The society sets some enormous goal and then works towards it, with thousands of individuals contributing Score and Sway to complete an enormous Pact Ordeal, often due to the scale of such projects they require years or even decades of work to achieve their final goal, and along the way many individuals will have to complete smaller, but equally important sub-Ordeals, to actually complete the work.

## 🃄 Four of Diamonds

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **4**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Orthodox*
- **General Meaning:** *Fours signify stability and foundations. They usually indicate some foundation, whether an organization or a building, that may have its foundation weakened, damaged or strengthened by the narrative.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Discourage**
- **Normal Position:** *+1 Failure Level on any Psychology Test*
- **Crossed Position:** *+1 Success Level on any Psychology Test*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Brown/Blue Omnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *Mesofauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Greenfly / Midge / Mosquito*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Wings*
- **Lea Notes:** *Tiny Arthropods, often larva forms of larger arthropods*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Solitary Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *1*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *High*
- **Test:** *Authority (Dominion)*
- **Obstacles:** *Usually none*
- **Suggested Action:** *Read/Research*
- **Stage:** *A short, but slight rise, a slope, step or few steps.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Working alone, but riding a beast or a vehicle, quickly. Try to get as far as possible.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Flowing forces have the higher ground and a slight situational advantage. They  add +1 Pip to each card they play to Defend during the Stage. The Ebbing forces have the lower ground, which confers them no advantage, or real disadvantage of note.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *Entrenched or armoured opponents that are greater in number, but lower in quality (start with Extras) or have lesser equipment than the attacking force.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Book** — *The Book is the card of knowledge, authority and responsibility. Books are information in permanent form, frozen thoughts, and as such are technology, language and magic. Books are granted authority, and define monotheistic religions, for example, but they may sometimes contain mistakes (in translation, spelling, or printing), or the author's opinions presented as facts, that can lead one astray. By the Book Characters act with this same occasionally flawed authority.*
- **Hook Event:** **Tone, Theme or Thesis** — *The Conflict hooks the Characters by a powerful scene that sets the Tone for the Story, that may also specify the Theme or Thesis of the Conflict with an immediate Revelation.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **I-Ching: Second Hexagram** — *The Hook targets the Character's I-Ching, specifically the 2nd Hexagram. Usually this 2nd Hexagram defines only a way of gaining Chi, Yin or Yang for the Character, so the Hook will provide whatever circumstances that Gain requires.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Knowledge (Lore or Learning Descendant of Skill or Talent Level)*
- **The Fray:** **Research** — *Usually a Low Stakes Ordeal that involves a Character finding out some information, it may involve reading (web-pages, books, articles, papers), visiting a Location (usually a Library, Archive, or a website), asking people questions, in interviews or by polling, or (more rarely) conducting experiments.*
- **The Snag:** **Missing Page** — *The Book Snag is a missing page or piece of the puzzle. They may have done the research, or reached their goal, only to discover they don't have the final item they require. They may be able to find another copy, or someone who has read that page though, so all is not lost.*
- **Sweeping:** **RTFM** — *You find or are directed to a book, tome or manual that can give you a Gain of a Teaching in a Skill, etc. Example, after asking why a green light started flashing on their flight suits they were handed the Operators Manual by the Technician in the bay. They will still need to find time to read the manual...*
- **History Context:** **Ancient** — *The Book card indicates that the past in this case is some ancient, half-forgotten knowledge that must be researched to be properly understood. This can indicate a forgotten piece of a character's history, or some actual historical situation affects this moment.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Authority** — *The Book indicates the current situation is something to do with authority, often the law or some rule, this may not necessarily be criminal law, property, corporate, civil, commercial, international, family, employment, administrative, religious, physical and temporal laws can apply. This authority may be the direct source of the situation or simply complicate it.*
- **Solution:** **Research** — *The Book indicates that the future, and a route out of this current situation, is certain, all ready decided and written down somewhere, only research and copious amounts of study and reading are required to find out exactly where and what that is.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **World-Building Authority** — The information is about the background, or setting, not the current plot or situation. It might include pertinent details for the current plot, but is usually just about what is the accepted authority of the world. Is there a codified book of laws? Is there a constitution for government? Is this a crypto-anarchist collective that accepts no authority beyond itself.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Facts** — The available information is in the form of raw facts, such as "Character X owns a yacht", "Character X comes from a poor family and neighbourhood", "Character X was arrested fifteen years ago, charges were not pressed at that time" and "Character Y was arrested for running a huge drug laboratory fourteen years ago" usually these facts can be used to draw a number of conclusions, some of which may even be true.
  - *Vector (How):* **Writing** — The information is found in writing. This could be a memorandum, a letter, a text, or a book. Sometimes the information will be hidden with a code or cipher within the text, perhaps even a work of fiction might hide some hidden truths.

Cults vary a lot, some are just reactionary religious sects (Satanists for example), others are led by charismatic narcissists for their own glorification (cults of personality), still others are pervasive terrorist idealogies, you just don&apos;t know what you might find.*

This Trauma can create Hitches for the Character as they become members of the cult.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Schism** — *An Age of Schism is occurring. During this age a society fractures and schisms as the society breaks apart due to internal pressures. ### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Disturbed** — *High Stress for hours, days, weeks or perhaps even years can profoundly alter a Character. Some people freak out if their schedule is disturbed even slightly, others need their sleep cycle disturbed before it becomes a problem. Others find themselves physically or emotionally disturbed. But this all amounts to the same thing.*
  > **Stress Rules:** A Character who has become disturbed (by the latest Stressor) cannot rest or settle while this latest stressor continues. If they can find a way to cease the disturbance they will relieve all current Stress (but not Strain or Shock) and cease being disturbed, but until that time they will add their current Stress and the Tension-Level as a Chi Difficulty to all Actions that are not based on ceasing the disturbance (or at least getting far away). Even Actions that act to cease the disturbance will add the Character’s current Stress in Chi as additional Difficulty.
- **Trauma Type:** **Loner** — *Trauma can cause a Character to change, become insular and introverted, hateful and anti-social, or they can come to their senses and realise that some people they call friends are not really their friends at all. Whatever the reason, whether betrayal, hazing, secrecy, or mental illness, this Trauma urges a Character to think about who their friends and Allies are very carefully.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Character may remove friends and allies from their life (usually without violence, although there may be tears, accusations, and harsh words). Each friend or Ally removed will relieve Stress equal to the Character Type ("unfriending" a Chorus Extra would relieve 5 Stress).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Isolation** — *An Age of Isolation is occuring. During this age a society reduces connections with other societies, this may be an active choice to limit association, or this can be an ostrasization, where they are forced out of contact by an external factor. This insularity within a society will always have a deleterious effect, resulting in stagnation, contraction and occasionally collapse.*
  > **Age Rules:** Connections to other societies are lost, restricted or limited, usually caused by external factors such as criminal enterprises, larger politics, and geo-politcal events such as wars.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Teacher** — *The Teacher card represents not just teachers, but schools in general, and the Teachings that the Teacher gives. The Teacher represents patience and courtesy, but is also a taskmaster, that demands you demonstrate what you have learnt.*
- **Hook Event:** **Exposition** — *The Story opens with exposition, this may be "incluing" where hints about the world are scattered about the text, or more normally with this card, direct narration that describes the setting. This is usually exposition done by a figure of authority, such as a teacher or politician, reciting the information for the PCs and audience.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Geometry (Perfect)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Geometry through the Perfect Harmonic. This is a "Perfect" Character for this one, perhaps a lover, friend, mentor or guide, offered up by the plot as part of the Hook.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Mentor Ally, Learning Descendant, Annex Training or Proficiencies*
- **The Fray:** **Study** — *The Study Ordeal consists of studying, researching, experimentation, and perhaps learning. It might be a Teaching Moment in a classroom, a meticulously planned and executed heist, a science experiment or sitting the bar exam. It may even lead to a scientific discovery, or similar paradigm changing moment.*
- **The Snag:** **Disruptive** — *The Embodiment's plans, Descendants, or allies are damaged, or interrupted by disruptive forces. Like the child that behaves inappropriately the moment the teacher's back is turned, there is a disruptive force present that complicates all of the PCs plans, not necessarily out of opposition, but as often boredom, or ignorance.*
- **Sweeping:** **Mentor Ally** — *The Pressed side Gains an Ally who will act as a Trainer, Coach, or Mentor. This leads to additional Trained and Learning Descendants*
- **History Context:** **School-Days** — *The Teacher indicates that the past of this situation goes all the way back to when the characters were being educated. This can mean a call back to something learnt in school, or that this situation has roots in the characters school-days.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Examination** — *The Teacher indicates the current situation is similar to a formal examination, this doesn't necessarily mean a written exam, but the character is being tested somehow. Such tests can be about trust, honourableness, worthiness, character or ability as well as academic knowledge.*
- **Solution:** **Teaching** — *The Teacher indicates that the solution to this problem is not learning some new skill or knowledge, but sharing the knowledge and skills you have with others, this will enable them to learn to feed, defend and improve themselves in the future.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Mentor** — The information is about a Teacher or Mentor that can educate the Characters in some Skill or ability that is often unique to the Setting or Story.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Education** — Information about a Character that tells you what they are skilled in as well as revealing some of their social circle (school friends) and probably any famous mentors or masters they may have previously had. In some societies knowing what school someone attended, or which wizard taught them their skills can be invaluable in assessing their threat.
  - *Vector (How):* **Broadcast** — The information is announced or pronounced by official sources. Announced by a teacher in form-room or assembly, a priest in a sermon, cried out by a town-crier, or perhaps all over the local press and TV stations, the information is broadcast somewhere the PCs can hear or see it.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Method** — The information is about a method, list, or step-by-step plan. Villains often have a method that they are all too willing to share with their captives as a way of gloating before they finish them off...
  - *Detail:* **Positive** — A positive detail is something pleasant about an otherwise terrible Character (such as how patient they are, or how charming), Location (Castle Donotenta has the most amazing tapestries on the continent), or Descendant (Well, it's called a Death Ray, but actually it started out as a medical scanner).

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Phantoms** — *Stress can have profound effects on the mind, causing one to see and hear all sorts of things that are not happening. Memories, waking dreams, and stress can easily combine to create Phantom Characters, hallucinations who take on the appearance of people from the Character&apos;s past.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character’s current Stress and the current Tension Level are added together to create an amount of Chi. This Chi must be paid by the Character and is used to either purchase a "Phantom" version of a Character or create an entirely new "Phantom". The Phantom may haunt the Character, accusing them, often seemingly feeding on the Character&apos;s own guilt and continues for a period of time equal to the Current Stress as Intrepid Facet Sway after which the phantom will vanish relieving Stress equal to the current Tension Level. Typically the Phantom will reappear anytime the Character’s current Stress plus the Tension Level is high enough to create them again. Phantoms are typically an extension of the Character, even if they do appear as ghosts, grey aliens, faeries, or shadow people, so trying to harm them with magic, for example, will usually harm the Character experiencing the Phantoms, of course sometimes Increated will use Phantoms as the basis of a Nightmare Form, in which case they can be targeted separately.
- **Trauma Type:** **Lessons** — *Trauma can be a teacher, at least there are often lessons to be learned from the traumatic experience. Trauma can teach a Character a new skill, reveal a hidden talent, or unlock a power. Depending upon the genre, trauma may unlock psychic abilities, unusual perceptions or senses, language, art, memory, musical or mathematical abilities.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma allows a Character to purchase with relieved Stress, a new Annex (or just a Proficiency depending on free Slots).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Teacher** — *An Age of a Teacher is occurring. During this age a teacher will be revealed within the society, this teacher may act as a leader, guiding the society towards some improvement or advancement, or more often they will train the next generation, inspiring rather than leading, sometimes to be a revolutionary force within the society. It should be noted though, that while the teacher has a profound impact, this is not necessarily and age of learning, and may be a rejection or reaction to the actual teachings.*
  > **Age Rules:** A Character within the society will become a Mentor to either the whole society or to the youth of the society, that will encourage, lead, or inspire improvements, advancements and change in the society.

- **Tide of Battle:** *The Turning Point is a moment where the Ebbing enemy and Flowing forces are inverted somehow. Some change of situation, such as the arrival of reinforcements, or a terrible accident, causes the Flow and Ebb to reverse.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *One of the Villain's Henchmen (equivalent to any 2 PCs) accompanied by a number of lesser opponents at least equal in number to the Party.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Star** — *The Star represents the pinnacle of success, the height of fame. It is the card of glory, excesses and undreamt of achievements, but it is also the card of jealousy, betrayals and rock and roll suicides. The Star may burn bright, but ultimately the night is dark.*
- **Hook Event:** **Danger** — *The Hook just consists of some enormous danger, it may be looming, it may be coming, or it is right here, right now. The Hook may include a Revelation and/or a High-Stakes Ordeal.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Geometry (Goal)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Geometry via their Goal, offering them what they want.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Stardom brings great wealth and recognition. Mansions, cars, space-yachts, all are available, with only one or two Hitches (usually Exposed and Dependence Hitches).*
- **The Fray:** **Wish** — *The Fray consists of a Quest, usually to accomplish something personally important to the Character.*
- **The Snag:** **A New Star Rises** — *An new Facet Embodiment appears, which destabilises the situation against the PCs, often making them work with Rivals or even former enemies. This could be a separate Facet joining the Conflict (or popping in from another usually larger Plot) or just another, stronger Embodiment of the Dominant side of this Conflict, such as when Guard and a Gladiator who have clashed on the battlefield before, join forces to fight a Dragon that threatens both armies.*
- **Sweeping:** **Darkness** — *The PC makes a powerful gain, but of a dark and terrible nature, such as a Dark Matter Descendant, Dark Territory, Demonic Pact, or a Monster Ally.*
- **History Context:** **Glory** — *The Star indicates that this situation is based on past glories. These may be the glories of a character, or part of a national history, but the point is that pinnacle for the character or society has long passed.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Betrayal** — *The Star indicates that situation is a betrayal of some kind. This betrayal may be a case of an unfaithful lover, reprehensible retailers, snitching subordinates, treasonous underlings, tattling ex-lovers, or treachery, but is often caused by jealousy of the character's perceived success, wealth, fame, rank or position.*
- **Solution:** **Lodestar** — *The Star indicates that a guide will appear that will lead the characters out of this situation. This guide can take the form of a leader, often from among the characters, or some external sign or portent that will lead the way. The difficulty for those in this situation is knowing which sign, guide or portent is the true lodestar and which are not.*

  - *Info (Embodiment):* **How?** — How exactly is what they plan to do, going to be done? You may get the full plan, or just the important pieces. Sometimes you might even know how to stop them, but not what they plan to do. It all depends on the situation at hand.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Dangerous** — *Stress is dangerous, it can turn a simple mistake into a deadly danger, make a simple shock cause a heart attack, or make a Character hold a loaded gun to their own head.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Diamond (<span class="t13ne-redcard">♦</span>) Suited Ordeal cards that target the Character for an Attack add the Character’s current Stress as Yarn so 13 Stress adds 66 Pips to each card, but then relieves Stress equal to the original Card Pips, plus all Strain and current Shock (although this new attack is a whole other ball game). Unsuited cards add current Stress as Chi (13 Pips), but remove only the current Stress Reduced (4 Stress), 1 Strain or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Notoriety** — *Trauma can lead to a Character gaining fame within their community or society. This notoriety can be "positive" with celebrity bringing benefts that may offset the trauma somewhat, but notoriety is as often negative, as some folk will try to exploit their knowledge of who the Character is in negative ways.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma grants the Character recognition by the masses. This becomes tied to a "Media Impression" similar to a Character’s Impression of them. Most Characters who did not meet the Character before the Trauma will match their Impression of the Character to the media impression, although often those with media exposure themselves will be less quick to adopt the media impression.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Glory** — *An Age of Glory is occurring. During this age a society reaches the next level somehow, although often such glories are followed by failures, for the moment everything the society attempts is successful. Ages of Glory are often claimed to be caused by some technological achievement, but there are always multiple foundations for the success in this age. An age of Glory can often cause a society to defeat or even consume their enemies, as kingdoms rise to become empires and colonial powers.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Glory everything the society attempts will be successful (at least until the age is over, when repercussions and consequences may emerge from that initial success).
