# Card Guide: Spades

This guide provides the complete mechanical, narrative, and Yarn specifications for all 13 cards of the Spades suit in the T13 Narrative Game Engine.

## 🂡 Ace of Spades

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **14**
- **Wound Type:** **Carnage** *(Wound Level 6)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Gossamer, Inertia, Key, Miasma, Trial, Yonder*
- **General Meaning:** *Aces signify beginnings, meetings and unions. They can indicate love-interests, chance encounters, alliances, and friendships.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Death**
- **Normal Position:** *Automatically adds +21 Pips to any Attack made by the Target. Stakes are Extreme while atop Pile. All Hitches created by or on the Target are "Dying", "Dead" or "Destroyed"*
- **Crossed Position:** *Automatically adds +21 Pips to any Defence. Stakes are Low while atop Pile. All Hitches created by or on the Target cannot be "Dying", "Dead" or "Destroyed".*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Red/Purple Carnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Hunters, Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *All-path*
- **Example Animal:** *All-Walkers*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Heart (Emotions)*
- **Lea Notes:** *The Paths of the All-Walkers can allow Bulmäs to do extraordinary things, like walking an individual’s life-path, so that all of their personal history is known to them. All-Walkers can assume even the forms and souls of other people by using these paths, without directly eating of the Soul. All-Walkers can even Doom Weave and play other cards from their hands as Wyrd Tarot cards, once they have played an Ace on themselves.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *9*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Medium-High-Extreme*
- **Test:** *Strength (Gossamer) / Secure (Rook) / Escape (Liberty)*
- **Obstacles:** *3 Obstacles per Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Push*
- **Stage:** *Maybe the buildings are collapsing, or an earthquake is happening. You know the sort of thing, jets of flame leap from the ground, the floor bucks and shakes, power cables spark and fall. Danger is all around.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *The smoke and flames are thick, but you can still see the headlights behind you. They aren't giving up...*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing enemy have gone for a pincer tactic and have the Flowing forces pinned between groups that are closing in. The Flowing side has a choice, Good mobility and speed will let you escape the trap, or you can hunker down and hope you can smash them as they come. Either way offers the Flowing side +1 Pips per card*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *This is a special Extreme Stakes Ambush, where the Ambushers actually rapidly assault (rather than being hidden). Shock Attackers have +1 Success Level to all rolls and cards during the opening Phase. Although they are rarely more than a Cast Extra, they may be Vex Monsters. ### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Threshold** — *The Threshold Card represents doorways, portals and liminal spaces. It is the edge of something else, a place where one goes from one place to another, often with different rules. It is a card of paradigm shifts, of new places. Great changes, events and dangers may lie ahead, and it is not too late to turn back, but then fresh understandings will not be gained, and no new discoveries will be made.*
- **Hook Event:** **Kick in the door** — *The Story opens with action, often a Fight, it could be the PCs who kick in a door, or an enemy may burst in.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Ref Special: e.g. (Immortality)*
- **The Fray:** **The Edge of the World** — *This is as bad as a Threshold can get, it is the liminal space between the known and the unknown. It might be the edge of a map, the ocean over the horizon, the edge of space, or the mouth of a cave. Usually such portals and thresholds will have some sort of guardian that must be defeated, sneaked or riddled past with an Ordeal.*
- **The Snag:** **The End of the Day** — *Sometimes the Snag is due to the time which adds some extra complications to the situation. Perhaps the faltering dusk light makes it harder to be spotted, or the first light of dawn may be what shows your silhouettes to the enemy. Then again the night of first frosts, first snows, or the first day of the thaw are all good complications too).*
- **Sweeping:** **On the Threshold** — *A quiet moment, almost an aside from the plot, that allows the PCs a breather before they push on. It usually occurs before some major event, while opening a door, or entering or exiting a space, and is often marked by one of the Plot's Motifs. They may have fallen in a dark hole, but upon climbing out, the PCs see a butterfly.*
- **History Context:** **Beginning** — *The Threshold indicates that the history of the current situation is a literal beginning, this may mean that the current situation is just begun (it has no true past), or that it can be traced back to a single instant that coincides with the the beginning of something else, such as the birth of a child, dawning of new age, or crowning of a monarch, etc.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Brink** — *The Threshold indicates that the current situation is balanced upon the brink of something new, that is only partially known (such as you may know when you are on the brink of a war, but not what the actual war would become). This is a delicate time, where the slightest push may send the situation spiralling out of control.*
- **Solution:** **Crossing** — *The Threshold indicates that the solution to the current situation involves a crossing, this may mean that you must cross to the other side, and join the opposition, or one of them must cross to you, or that you must leave this place behind to solve the issue.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Dominant** — The information revealed is about the Dominant Conflict Embodiment. Sometimes they are Characters, but almost as often they are Locations, Quests, Ordeals, or similar. Where this is true the Revelation should reveal details about future Quests the Pressed Characters must complete, or places they should visit. The Revelation can be little more than a call-back or foreshadowing if handled correctly.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Connections** — The information is about the connections that the character or location has.
  - *Vector (How):* **Aura** — Auras are for some people a spiritual or emotional energy field that they see, or they may just be another word for a fragrance.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Sacrifices** — The information may be about the Sacrifices the Embodiment has made, or Sacrifices that will have to be made. These Sacrifices might be great and grand, or just toll for the tunnel, or it could just be information about a Sacrifice Umbral.
  - *Detail:* **Target** — A target detail is simply that, where, what, or whom the target of the Embodiment is.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Between** — *Sometimes a Character is neither one thing or the other, in neither one place or the other, and this can make it difficult for them to act*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character must discard all Spades (<span class="t13ne-blackcard">♠</span>) cards from Ordeal Pool, Style Reserves and Wyrd Tarot Hand, until they have zero Stress. Each Spade Discarded removes 1 Stress.
- **Trauma Type:** **Uncertain** — *Trauma can push a Character to their breaking point, to the very edge of sanity, or to borderline of the beyond. Some Characters never quite come back after they have been that far, they forever seem to exist in the liminal space at the edge of rational thought, neither fully rational or irrational, and never certain of anything again. To some they may seem to have retreated into a fantasy world, but some others will note that events from the real world are seemlessly integrated into their fantasies, and often this fantasy may offer deeper insights into what is going on than the "real-world" can suppy.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** Trauma pushes a character to the edge of their sanity. Creating a Psychosocial Location (purchased with Relieved Stress acting as Chi) within their mind that may have overlaps or connections to any other point of the Psychosocial mindscape (which will affect the nature of the Location — for example placing it deep in the Dreamscape may make it seem fantastical, connecting it to Supernatural may allow it limited telepathy, even allowing the Character to retreat beyond their own mindscape for safety in another’s mind. The Character can thereafter Relieve 1 Stress instantly by retreating to this Psychosocial Location, or by acting as though they are Uncertain (what is real, what is right, what they shoud do, etc).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Emergence** — *An Age of Emergence is occurring. During this age society stands upon the brink of a great sea-change in thinking, some paradigm shift is underway, and after the age has finished some aspect of society will be forever altered. The whole society will be affected and altered by this shift in perspective, historically these revolutionary ideas have caused huge changes in culture and the path of history, the industrial revolution changed how the world worked, the assembly line altered businesses and factories forever, the internet altered communications and information retrieval completely.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Emergence some new paradigm, theory or technology sweeps the world, changing all societies. Societies that fail to adapt to the Emerging new world usually swiftly collapse, hoever those that can exploit some new niche of the new world quickly thrive.

## 🂢 Two of Spades

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **2**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Miasma*
- **General Meaning:** *Twos signify conflict, opposition and choices. They can indicate crossroads, partings, separations, and changes of direction.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Reroll**
- **Normal Position:** *Reroll any dice including a Proficiency Crisis.*
- **Crossed Position:** *Automatic Proficiency Crisis. You can add a Proficiency to, or create, an Annex (if you have a slot free).*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Red/Purple Carnivore**
- **Bulmas Type:** **Hunters, Broccen, Vermis, Labyrinthine, All-Walkers**
- **Animal Category:** *Microfauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Bacteria / Viral paths*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Lower Intestine / Cloaca*
- **Lea Notes:** *Microscopic viral and bacterial paths, often overlooked, this can mimic any larger animal paths.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *4*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Low*
- **Test:** *Medicine (Phoenix)*
- **Obstacles:** *1 Obstacle in a Court Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Float / Swim*
- **Stage:** *The route ahead is dangerous, littered with sharp stones, broken glass, or even landmines.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Hit the gas! They're right behind us!*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing enemy are at breaking point, one good assault should see them done. The Flowing force need only cause more Wounds than they take this Stage to win the round.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Swamp** — *The Swamp is a card the represents a dichotomy. The Swamp is to be feared, it is a place of corruption, decay, stagnation, and many believe it magical, for those who enter it unprepared will die, but for those that know its secret ways, recognise its dangers, then medicinal plants, herbs, and other medicines and magics are revealed. The Swamp can represent potions and medicines that are as foul-tasting as swamp-water, and yet somehow help.*
- **Hook Event:** **What's That Smell?** — *The Story Hooks the Characters with a direct warning and a question, this is usually what's that smell? But it can be What's that noise, taste or weird light, just as easily. In any case the answer is usually a corpse, occasionally a Monster, and just occasionally a broken sewer.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Dark Territory or Dark Matter Descendant*
- **The Fray:** **Quagmire** — *A Quagmire is a sticky morass or corruption which the PCs find themselves engulfed by. Quagmires can be literal swamps, so called Quicksand, and so on, or more likely they can be sticky situations, economic quagmires are rising debts, negative equity and falling wages, political quagmires are usually the opposite, with money from all sorts of dubious sources flooding in, who only want small concessions and favours. Either way, the PCs will need to extricate themselves somehow.*
- **The Snag:** **Unique Situation** — *The Snag adds the complication to the Fray that the situation or Location is truly unique. It might be that the ingredients for the ritual required grow only in one place, that must be visited, or that the nightclub has the only indoor grass dance floor in the city. It is usual for the unique situation to either threaten the PCs or be threatened by the Opposed Embodiments.*
- **Sweeping:** **Shower Scene** — *The Shower Scene may not take place in a Shower or Bath, but represents the PCs trying to scrub free whatever they feel still clings to them. This may be some mud, or it could be an emotional taint. The Sweeping Scene allows the Character to treat their own wounds (especially Psych Wounds) with some ease (Halved Difficulty usually).*
- **History Context:** **Corrupted** — *The Swamp indicates that the current situation is a corruption of what was before, there will be some similarities left, but this situation is a dark reflection in muddy waters of what came before.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Mired** — *The Swamp indicates a situation where you are caught somehow, often in a dangerous place. Characters can be mired by debt, by fear, by threats or actual violence, most often there is abuse and corruption around these circumstances.*
- **Solution:** **Potion** — *The Swamp indicates that the solution to the current situation is a potion, which may be magical, medicinal, alcoholic, herbal, curative, miraculous or occasionally poisonous. Sometimes this potion may actually be a pill, herb or antidote, and just occasionally it is just a beverage.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Pressed** — The information is about the Pressed Conflict Embodiment, which is often a PC in a game, and is usually the antagonist in a Story.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Corruption** — Corruption can range from a little bribery to actual demonic influences, and everything in between. Finding out about corruption is easy, although rarely will you get the details.
  - *Vector (How):* **Scent** — Scents can tell us a lot, reminding us of places, people, animals and of course events. The lingering scent of gun-smoke or fire can pass more than enough information for action.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Influences** — The information is about the influences that are working in the plot, or on the Character. These might be just the politics of their social media friends, the meddlings of some Seelie Noble, or the machinations of an Increated, you just never know.
  - *Detail:* **Critical** — Critical details are either spotting flaws in an otherwise perfect planet killer space base, or a critique that one can use to improve one's performance.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Worries** — *Worries make a Character think about their issues, dwelling on what questions or issues are stressing them out.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character may reduce their Stress by 1 for each statement they make, that relates to their worries, character that they talk to about it, or they may pay Chi equal to their current Stress as Tao Sway to remove 1 Stress.
- **Trauma Type:** **Toxic** — *Trauma can twist a Character, easily making personality traits and behaviours more toxic. This toxicity will usually express in passive-aggressions, unwarranted criticism of others, snide remarks, sarcastic attitudes, elaborate excuses for their behaviour, seeking to blame others for any failures, manipulating others for their own ends.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma instantly Relieves all current Stress, which is used to purchase a "Toxic" Hitch, that will typically interfere with relationships, create bad impressions and hamper the Character socially. The Toxic Character can Relieve 1 Stress by acting toxic somehow to another.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Corruption** — *An Age of Corruption is occurring. During this age society begins to break down, individual members of the society behave in their own self-interest, rather than the interest of their society. Corruption, extortion, exploitation, fraud, profiteering, racketeering, malfeasence and nepotism are the order of the day, often with a hint of social injustice and base criminality. A corrupt society does not serve its membership and they will often form new societies, abandonning their old, where they cannot fix the society&apos;s ills.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Corruption most members turn aside from the society, whether to exploit the other members, make a quick buck, or to excape the corruption within the society. This allows exploitation narratives and corruption stories to Embody within the society. Allowing an age of corruption to continue unchecked always has a negative effect upon the society, costing them membership to other nearby, and less corrupt societies, and often leading to a complete collapse.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Tower** — *The Tower card can represent physical buildings, places, and stability. Less commonly, in the form a Wizard's or Watchtower it may represent research, viewing, observation or looking-out. The Tower is a stable landmark, not prone to collapse, except when struck by lightning, which happens often before the lightning conductor, or earthquake, which is considered an act of the gods before the modern day also. The card has a hint of divine retribution about this meaning, which is why it may be called the Tower Struck By Lightning in negative positions.*
- **Hook Event:** **Lookout** — *The Story opens with a Lookout or Scout bringing news, of an impending threat, this might be "Land Ho!", or "Smoke!" or "Three Thousand Orcs and Ogres are marching South along the King's road. They will be here in two days, maybe less." or the PCs themselves may be the one's to see the threat, and have to raise the alarm.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Location (often Real Estate or a Province where appropriate)*
- **The Fray:** **Tower Struck By Lightning** — *A terrible, traumatic, life changing, paradigm shifting event. Usually a disaster of this magnitude is a Force of Nature Extra (at least), in an Ordeal that has High-Paradoxical Stakes. It is common for new Hitches to result from this Fray, ideally that will deepen the hooks of the Conflict in the PCs, perhaps even causing them to internalise the actual Conflict within themselves.*
- **The Snag:** **Hold!** — *The Fray ends / leads to a situation where the Embodiment must hold position against a force of lesser opponents (usually Vex and Chorus Extras) that outnumber the Embodiment and Allies. Usually this Snag Ordeal will have a limited duration, with either reinforcements arriving, or some extraction team en-route, or perhaps the enemies will stop with dawn's light, or at nightfall. Otherwise, the Vexes will come in waves, with rest periods in between.*
- **Sweeping:** **Stability** — *The PCs have a stable situation for a while (this may be weeks or months)*
- **History Context:** **Built** — *The Tower indicates that the current situation has been built, brick by brick, as one might construct a tower. This situation was deliberately engineered by someone for a specific purpose.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Stable** — *The Tower indicates that the current situation is essentially stable, sometimes this means if you try to change it, something will happen to stabilise it once again, other times it just means that it would be very difficult to change anything.*
- **Solution:** **Vigil** — *The Tower indicates that the solution to the current situation is to watch or look over the situation carefully. This could be a stakeout, observation, or research about the situation, that is itself the solution, rather than revealing a solution.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Internal** — The information revealed is about the Internal Conflict Embodiment. The Internal side of a Conflict may be within all sides or just one. It might be traitors, moles, spies, spirits or just a Character's internal conflict.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Conclusions** — The available information points to a number of potential conclusions. Usually only one of them is true.
  - *Vector (How):* **Vision** — A Hallucination that reveals information is common enough that it gets a mention. Of course, what we don't hear about are all the times hallucinations aren\t useful.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Safety** — The information is about a place, or time where either Embodiment will feel, or be safe. If you need a safe space, this tells you where to find one, if you have a safe space, it tells you where your Opposed Embodiment feels safe.
  - *Detail:* **Legal** — A Legal detail can be a point of law, a loophole, or miscarriage of justice that can be exploited somehow. You may not have the villain's address, but thanks to this restraining order you know who his lawyers are...

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Coping** — *Coping mechanisms for stress vary from person to person. Some people will talk about what is stressing them, creating a new narrative out of the situation at had, others will let stress get the better of them, corrupting them, or driving them to attack, others will hide themselves away, and very few may lose senses.  These coping mechanisms can grow rapidly becoming Flaws or even Woes as a Character relies on them to cope with Stress.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Coping Characters create a new Binding, Laments, Confined, Corrupted, Offensive or Loss-of-Sense Quirk automatically initially with a Bane of 1. Characters may move Stress into this Hitch, increasing the Banes by 1 for the current Banes in Stress any time the Hitch is Triggered.
- **Trauma Type:** **Tower** — *Trauma can lead a Character to seeking safe spaces, building panic rooms, buying and installing security systems, and generally developing vigilance against future Traumas.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma causes a Character to feel physically unsafe for a majority of their time. It immediately Relieves all current Stress, but creates an "Unsafe" Pool. They may seek to purchase armour, secure Locations and the like, of a value at least equal to the "Unsafe" Pool, as this will reduce the Pool by 1 each time. They may also Relieve 1 Stress gained later by checking every potential entrance and exit is secured.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Expansion** — *An Age of Expansion is occurring. During this age a society will seek out new territories and locations, and construct and build upon this new land they have acquired. Societies in this age will often build an impressive building or buildings that will become historically significant with time, such as churches, cathedrals, tithe barns, exchanges and official market buildings, banks, palaces, henges, castles, citadels, town walls, town halls, public baths, court buildings, etc.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Expansion a society and the individual members will undertake various Ordeals to capture, colonise, explore, find, gain, purchase or rent a new location, and convert that location to their purposes, often by constructing infrastructure and buildings in the new Location. Such a society-wide Ordeal is similar to an Age of Endeavour, where the society works for a common goal, in this case the common goal is less common to all members of the society, but the society will benefit from the expansion as a whole.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Sail** — *The Sail card represents the act of harnessing an external, or higher, power to your needs. Just as a ship's sail, or windmill's turbine harnesses the elemental wind to drive the ship or mill, so the card can imply that you will make headway, perhaps travel.*

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Complicated** — *Stress can interfere with your life, making it more complicated in every way. Self-sabotage can play a part too, stress acts against our better judgement and instincts at times, interfering in what we do.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Actions in the suited Facets (Gossamer, Inertia, Key, Miasma, Trial or Yonder) add a Difficulty equal to the Character’s current Stress in Tao Sway. If a penalised Action Succeeds it will reduce the Character’s Stress by 1.
- **Trauma Type:** **Runaway** — *Trauma can make a Character not feel safe in their own home, and many choose to leave. Some will make careful preparations, purchasing a new place, arranging a new job and selling up, others will just abandon their entire life and escape without a plan. Of course, the second types usually end up in a spiralling descent to more Trauma, and worsening situations, but occasionally they will make progress and build a new life for themselves.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates a desire to escape their home and situation. The Character may Relieve 1 Stress anytime they make any Action that leads towards escape from their current situation (for example, placing your home on the market, notifiying a landlord you are moving out, applying for a job in another country, being interviewed for a job, or riding a shuttle away will all relieve Stress).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Dependency** — *An Age of Dependency is occurring. During this age a society hitches their wagon to another. Sometimes this is a deliberate action by the rulers of the society, to ally with a more powerful society, other times it comes as a result of an invasion or similar geo-political crisis. During the age the society is dependent upon the "root" society for something, often this is a trade agreement, or military self-defence pact with a more powerful partner. Dependence on others can cause friction within a society, especially if this comes after past glories, and the society may feel they are not well enough respected by other allies during this dependence (although often this is simply projection, or rose-tinted nostalgia making some members of the society feel weak compared to their previous position). Typically an age of dependence ends with a breaking away, an indepence revolution, internal collapse, merging into the "root" society, or by politically or technologically manoeuvring into a more dominant position.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Dependency a society is dependent upon another usually larger society, smaller societies may spend the vast majority of their time dependent upon the national or (inter-)planetary society above them. Villages are often associated with towns, towns with counties, counties with states, provinces or principalities, and those with countries, countries with unions and empires etc. Typically a dependent society pays Sway into the root society and the root society returns it as though it were one level higher, but this it is still paid back in the same Sway (e.g. Paying in Chi returns Chi equal to the Value of the Boon).

- **The Snag:** **A Sword Raised** — *A Powerful Character (often a Monster with at least TWO Monster Facets and more normally around 5) such as a Cyborg Commando, Griffin and rider, Dragon, Vampire Assassin, Chimera or Demon, who opposes the PCs. - **Sweeping:** **The Sweeping Sword** — *The PCs get to take it easy, as another side (or new Embodiment) suddenly engages the (old) Opposed Embodiment in a direct conflict (usually of raised Stakes, often of High or Extreme). - **Theme/Yarn:** **The Robot** — *The Robot card represents the automatons, and workers, be they real robots, or people who live rigid, regimented lives, like the Eusaiveans. The Robot can represent the unconscious mind, which operates the body for the consciousness, or the mechanistic body itself. It can also represent those who are unawakened (or ain't woke) to what is really happening. They might be muggles, grunts, slaves, serfs, or actual robots.*

- **Solution:** **Automation** — *The Robot indicates that the solution to the current situation can be automated, this may mean an industrial process, some sort of magical automation (such as a golem), or an actual robot occasionally.*

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Health** — *Stress, especially high stress over time, can have a very deleterious effect upon a Character’s health. Commonly a weakened immune system takes longer to shrug off infections and heal, and the Character may find themselves run down and exhausted from minor illnesses that would not normally affect them so much.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Healing times for all Spades Suited Wounds adds the Current Stress as a Chi Extend Duration, even a Distract Wound can take a while to shake off when you are Stressed. Additionally all Spades Suited Wounds that the Character takes will add Current Stress to their Pips, including the Difficulty to treat and Stabilise (increasing the chances of Wounds Festering).
- **Trauma Type:** **Robotic** — *Trauma can make a Character seem like a robot in a number of ways. Many victims of Trauma will suppress or numb their emotions, some focus on logic and rationalism, others can settle into a strict regime of work, so that they don’t have time to think about their past traumas, and for a few injuries may have been healed with mechanical or cybernetic parts (for some such as Eusaiveans they may even be converted into a Cyberwraith), dissociation, psychoses, and delusions convince them that they are no longer really human, embracing their idea of robotic behaviour.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** Trauma may cause a Character to become Cybernetic (with associated Hitch(es) created from current Stress relief), or simply to behave in a robotic way. The Character can Relieve 1 Stress by acting robotically or "inhumanly" in any given situation (working mechannically, suppressing or numbing their emotions, speaking in a flat monotone to avoid displaying emotional cues, ignoring social pleasantries, having no discernable sense of humour, acting "robotic", "undead", or "inhuman" in some way, etc).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Drudgery** — *An Age of Drudgery is occurring. During this age a society becomes almost robotic, it is in many ways a zombie society, dead already, but still going through the motions. A society in this state may wake up, revitalise and move formward, but then again it does require someone within the membership realising what is happening and changing something before a complete collapse occurs as some new society offers a better way of life to its members.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Drudgery a society appears to continue their last age, although not to the same result. The society may be blindly producing outdated technology, they may be using a traditional manufacturing method that is no longer competitive, may be fighting an enemy that no longer strictly speaking exists, or they may be following some ancient contract or programming that is long out of date in some other way.

## 🂪 Ten of Spades

- **Wound Type:** **Crippling** *(Wound Level 4)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Gossamer*
- **General Meaning:** *Tens indicate a completion, whether the actual end of the story or the end of a character or phase.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot

Wiles, or weapons, may be needed to get by, and it is bound to slow you down. ### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Lion** — *The Lion card represents strength, not just individually (which it does) but also strength in numbers. The Lion may indicate also heroic deeds and actions or proud behaviour. The Lion can be seen as a symbol of success and power, but more often it represents a test of that strength, and shows that pride comes before a fall.*
- **Hook Event:** **Pride Comes Before A Fall** — *The Story Hooks the Character with giving them recognition for some earlier story, this Story is often a Sequel to the earlier episode. Usually makes a Gain (often a Prize, Medal or Award) as acknowledgement of the previous actions of the PC.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Powerful Monster Ally, Super (or Power) Annex or Super-Skill Descendant*
- **The Fray:** **Encounter** — *An Encounter is a Fight against a smaller number of similar powered foes, or a larger group of weaker foes. An Encounter should wear at the party, maybe Crippling or Maiming a few Characters and perhaps giving one Character a new Hitch (Mortal+), Extras (of any size) may die, including Cast Extras. You may want to kill a PC during this Fray if the Stakes are already elevated.*
- **The Snag:** **Test of Strength** — *The Lion Snag is a test of strength, this might be a literal strength test, where the PC must break in a door, or lift a gate, or it may be a more subtle complication where the PCs specific abilities are tested by a Rival, Ally, Mentor, or Opposed Embodiment. This complication can involve asking questions like "How many troops can the PC defeat at once?" and testing that theory. Note that the Test is usually not intended to destroy, and you can capture the party with a "Captured" Confined Hitch rather than a "Dead" Scars Hitch.*

- **Situation/Plight:** **Heroic** — *The Lion indicates that the current situation is in need of a hero, the sort of optimistic, adventurous, possibly psychopathic sort.*
- **Solution:** **Strength** — *The Lion indicates that the solution to the current situation is a show of strength. This may be a display of power, a literal show of strength, or revealing that your forces vastly outnumber theirs.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Pressed** — The information is about the Pressed Conflict Embodiment, which is often a PC in a game, and is usually the antagonist in a Story.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Where?** — Information about a Location.
  - *Vector (How):* **Exchange** — Maybe you overhear an exchanging of words, or maybe you are part of the exchange, trading information you know, for information back.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Ruins** — The information may be about a location, fallen into disrepair, or some hint of the Hitches of the Embodiment.
  - *Detail:* **Coherent** — Coherent details all point to the same conclusion. All the details line up pointed at what must appear a forgone conclusion. Of course, just because it all lines up doesn't mean it can't be wrong or misinterpreted.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Disturbed** — *High Stress for hours, days, weeks or perhaps even years can profoundly alter a Character. Some people freak out if their schedule is disturbed even slightly, others need their sleep cycle disturbed before it becomes a problem. Others find themselves physically or emotionally disturbed. But this all amounts to the same thing.*
  > **Stress Rules:** A Character who has become disturbed (by the latest Stressor) cannot rest or settle while this latest stressor continues. If they can find a way to cease the disturbance they will relieve 10 Stress and cease being disturbed, but until that time they will add their current Stress and the Tension-Level as a Chi Difficulty to all Actions that are not based on ceasing the disturbance (or at least getting far away). Even Actions that act to cease the disturbance will add the Character’s current Stress in Chi as additional Difficulty.
- **Trauma Type:** **Fierce** — *Trauma can unlock hidden strengths and courage within a Character, it can be odd to think that a powerful trauma that can make some Characters afraid of everthing around them, can have the opposite effect on some other Character, making them bold and self-assured to the point of fierceness. Fierce Characters are scary not scared, aggressive not defensive, and have either physical or mental strength and willpower.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma allows the Character to Relieve 1 Stress by being bold, assertive, fierce (aggresive), or demonstrating strength of will, or physical strength.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Predation** — *An Age of Predation is occurring. During this age a society draws closer together, either as they prey upon a weaker society, or as they are preyed upon by a larger society. Typically this predation is in the form of exploitation, manipulation and abuse, whether through invasion, theft, warfare or more nefarious means such as trade agreements, enslavement, post-war reparations, aid foundations, international politics, or unchecked commercial practices. Usually the weaker, prey society is "consumed" by the predatory society, who will gain the members (sometimes as prisoners, slaves or as part of a brain-drain). There is some overlap here with Jumbling Ages, but one society is always clearly dominant in an age pf Predation.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Predation the society preys upon and consumes another, usually smaller or less technological society. Members of the smaller predated society may be able to move into the predatory society and those able to move will often do so. Sometimes a prey society may manage to survive, or at least preserve some of their original culture within the new society.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Explorer** — *The Explorer card represents the urge to wander, to seek out new places, and make new discoveries. Exploration, investigation and travel are all indicated by the card, but there is another side to the Explorer, sometimes they come as an invader, or a crusader. If you are the Explorer it warns you that you may not be seen as just a tourist by the natives.*
- **Hook Event:** **Visitor** — *The Story opens with an unexpected visitor that may  bring the PCs a Revelation and a Gain. This Visitor is usually a friend or Ally, rather than a Rival or enemy, but occasionally it will be a stranger, who claims a connection that will later be validated or proved a lie.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Discover a new or lost Location / Real Estate / Province / Dark Territory*
- **The Fray:** **Explore** — *The Explore Fray is an exploration of the Conflict, it is time to explore some aspect of the interactions of the Facets in the Conflict. This may be done by actually exploring a Locations that Embodies the Conflict directly, or investigating a Descendant that has an Annex like the Conflict's.*
- **The Snag:** **Lost** — *The PCs actions are complicated by there being no map, no GPS or the streets just seem different. The PCs or those they seek are lost, this can be due to High Strangeness like a Yarn-Teller or Time-Traveller side-stepping or ret-conning the area and making the map change.*
- **Sweeping:** **Map** — *The PCs discover, explore, or gain access to a map or directions, that includes a secret passage, short cut or hidden way. This will allow them to appear or escape via this short cut and avoid any police, guards or enemy forces. It may allow them to skip Stages of the next Fray Ordeal or similar.*
- **History Context:** **Explored** — *The Explorer indicates that the current situation has roots in an old exploration. The area explored or the explorer may be more important to the current situation.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Lost** — *The Explorer indicates that the current situation involves being lost, usually by having a clear idea where you want to be, but no idea which direction that may lie in.*
- **Solution:** **Navigate** — *The Explorer indicates that there is a solution to the current situation, but it will involve some navigation. Sometimes navigation will have a map, or a compass, other times you'll have to draw the map*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Opposed Embodiment / A Rival** — The Information is specifically about the Opposed Embodiment, or a particular Rival of the Yarn-Teller.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Who?** — Information about a Character, usually an Embodiment. It might be their name, or genealogy, perhaps their CV.
  - *Vector (How):* **Communications** — The information comes via a communications technology, perhaps the radio, or a cell phone call, or in another age a messenger or pigeon.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Changes** — The information is about changes that have occurred to or because of the Embodiment. It can also signify that the information itself changes. Appearing as one thing to begin with, but then being later revealed by a detail to be something else (or could magically change).
  - *Detail:* **Delicate** — Delicate details are the finer points, little bits of information that might be overlooked easily, and could even be destroyed accidentally before they can be properly examined. A delicate detail might indicate a quirk, vulnerability, or flaw in some cases, but will be hard to spot. "He was a rakish, whip of a man, dressed in black leather, mail and silks. His eyes were dark and tired, close set, above a thin nose, broad cheekbones and confident grin..."

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Hallucinations** — *Stress can have profound effects on the mind, causing one to see and hear all sorts of things that are not happening. Simply missing too much sleep can cause a healthy mind to micro-sleep and confuse the Character’s real and dream lives, and for some minds the confusion is more easily started, unusual brain chemistry and even normal development can lead to all manner of hallucinatory voices and imaginary friends knocking about the place.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character’s current Stress and the current Tension Level are added together to create an amount of Chi. This Chi must be paid by the Character  and is used to either purchase a new "Hallucinatory" Character or to side-step the Character partially into a Psycho-social dream space. The Hallucination continues  for a period of time equal to the Current Stress as Intrepid Facet Sway after which the hallucination will vanish removing 1 Stress. Typically the hallucination will reappear anytime the Character’s current Stress plus the Tension Level is high enough to create them again. Hallucinations are typically an extension of the Character, even if they do appear as grey aliens, faeries, ghosts or shadow people, so trying to harm them with magic, for example, will usually harm the Character experiencing the hallucinations, of course sometimes Increated will use hallucinations as the basis of a Nightmare Form, in which case they can be targeted.
- **Trauma Type:** **Stranger** — *Trauma can confuse and delude the mind, making a Character who should feel at home, feel like a foreigner, a stranger in a strange land. The Character may suddenly be bothered by their own culture, what was once traditional and reassuring, can become alien and disturbing, or they may feel drawn to explore some other culture, retreating from their own, and the traumas they associate with it.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma will often create a Hitch with Relieved Current Stress, that may reflect this oddness, delusion or strangeness that they feel. The Character will Relieve 1 Stress when act as a stranger or outsider to their own culture in someway, questioning or rejecting aspects of a tradition or cultural more.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Exploration** — *An Age of Exploration is occurring. During this age a society explores beyond its borders, seeking unsettled lands that they can add to their own territory. A society often becomes obsessed with the latest exploratory missions, and the discoveries they have found, with new plants, animals, feathers or cultural artifacts. Settlement or colonization of the new land is usually the result of this age.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Exploration a society encounters a new territory that they must explore, uninhabited territories may provide new animals, minerals or plants. Inhabited territories will expose the society to a new external (most often ancient or dead) culture, which captures the public imagination for a while.

Depending upon what is found and how the society responds to this knowledge can profoundly effect whether this age will be perceived later in a positive or negative light.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Inspection some society wide inquiry or inspection takes place, often this becomes an inspection of the society itself. With similar Questions (defined often by the society&apos;s highest Facet) being asked throughout the society.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Butcher** — *The Butcher card represents blood, death and carrion, but it also represents sustenance, food and support within a community. The hard part of reading the card is knowing when the card is a sign of disaster and when it means a mighty feast, or more importantly who that feast is for.*
- **Hook Event:** **Danger** — *The Hook just consists of some enormous danger, it isn't looming, it isn't coming, it is right here, right now.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitches** — *The Hook targets the Character's Hitches, the bigger the better.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Increase Scale*
- **The Fray:** **Hunted** — *Being Hunted (or hunting) is a High Stakes Ordeal, that may begin with a Stalk or Chase, that explodes into a Fight. A Hunt is usually a team sport, with the Hunter being superior in power level to the Prey.*
- **The Snag:** **Big Bad** — *The Opposed Embodiment or at least one Force of Nature Monster (with at least 2 Monster Facets - usually either the Dominant Facet and it's Anti-Facet or the Pressed Facet) Extra engages the party during the Fray. At any time except their Story's Zenith Act, the Big Bad is protected by actual 'Plot Armour' meaning that the Plot Demon will Soak Wounds, etc. for them. Powerful Character's can still defeat them (and the Plot by extension) during the early Acts, so Ref's should handle with care, and usually use a FON Extra instead.*
- **Sweeping:** **Feast** — *The Sweeping grants the PCs a huge feast or festival, there may be gains, in the form of gifts as well as food, drink, music and dancing, and there will be meat.*
- **History Context:** **Slain** — *The Butcher indicates that before the current situation someone or something was killed. This may be an animal, but more often (especially in crime rich genres) is a character, the killing is often deliberate, but can be accidental.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Killer** — *The Butcher indicates that the current situation involves a killer, this may be a lone psychopath, a predatory animal, or occasionally a monster.*
- **Solution:** **Feast** — *The Butcher indicates that the solution to the current situation involves a feast, usually this means a large celebration where people can eat together and the shared experience will allow them to find common ground, sometimes it may imply that the solution is to over-feed the monster or whatever so it falls asleep and can be killed, even less commonly the only solution will be to eat the cause.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **The Big Bad** — The information revealed is about the most powerful foe the Yarn-Teller might encounter. The Big Bad may not even be a part of the current Conflict, but represent a higher tier of Plot (perhaps even the Dominant side of the Cycle Conflict)
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **How?** — How exactly is what they plan to do, going to be done? You may get the full plan or just the important pieces. You might get How to stop it, but not what they do. It all depends on the situation at hand.
  - *Vector (How):* **Subtlety** — Some sweet, subtle details, or a very tricky to decipher clue, conveys the information. It may be the things that were left unsaid in a conversation, a tone of voice or an expression used, but something gives away the information.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Designs** — Designs are sometimes detailed plans, other times they are broad sketches, depending how early in the process the designs were made. Designs can also be what the Embodiment is trying to achieve, without even a sketch of a plan.
  - *Detail:* **Salient** — Salient details are just the most important things that anyone might need to know. "So... We are *all* going to die."

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Dangerous** — *Stress is dangerous, it can turn a simple mistake into a deadly danger, make a simple shock cause a heart attack, or make a Character hold a loaded gun to their own head.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Spades (<span class="t13ne-blackcard">♠</span>) Suited Ordeal cards that target the Character for an Attack add the Character’s current Stress as Yarn so 13 Stress adds 66 Pips to each card, but then relieves Stress equal to the original Card Pips, plus all Strain and current Shock (although this new attack is a whole other ball game). Unsuited cards add current Stress as Chi (13 Pips), but remove only the current Stress Reduced (4 Stress), 1 Strain or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Butcher** — *Trauma can do odd things to people, making them change their diet, or their behaviour, temperament or even make them hear voices in their head that they can only silence by killing. Sometimes an intense trauma will break a person to their moral core, unleashing a predator, that may appear more monster than person.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma Relieves all Current Stress, converting it to Chi and using this to purchase a new Hitch, Alt or Monster Facet (the new Alt can also be a Monster if you like).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Feasting** — *An Age of Feasting is occurring. During this age a society has reasons to celebrate and is often quite rich, but food rather than riches is how society demonstrates those riches. Vast banquets, lavish meals, bountiful harvests, and fat farm animals are the order of the age. Quite what the society is celebrating will depend upon the previous age. Although the age of feasting is a celebration of some sort, it is also a bloody period, death and carrion are all around, flies and rats swarm the back alleys and sickness is never more than a few neighbours away.*
  > **Age Rules:** During the Age of Feasting a society celebrates the last age or its end, the society is food rich due to good harvests or strong trade. All members of society will be well fed, and healthy, at least at the start of the age. Depending how long the situation remains stable, with trade good, harvests rich and animal stocks well maintained, eventually most members of society will be over-fed, and become quite unhealthy, sickness will inevitably sweep through the society, if famine, drought or trade disruption does not end the situation ealier.
