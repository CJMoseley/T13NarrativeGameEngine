# Card Spreads

In T13 how you play cards is almost as important as the cards you play. Referees and Yarn-Tellers will often develop a Plot with cards, using the Yarn-Cards in various ways to play cards in a number of ways.

## Plot Spreads

Each size of Plot has a Spread associated with it. This spread includes the Plot Significator as well as any other Spreads the Plot may require such as:

### Conflict Spreads

When a Plot is defined the first thing that is defined is the Conflict the most simple is a 2x2 pattern that uses 5 cards as this example shows


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Simple** *(Handsize: 5)*



Conflict spreads can increase in complexity several times with greater and more complex conflicts being created with more cards. As you can see on this table:


### Conflict Spreads

| Type | Alternate | Max_Facets | Description | Sides |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Simple | Conflict(2x2) | 2 | The Simplest of Conflict spreads, limited in what it can display. Ideal for simple stories. There are limited Facets involved and that makes these ideal for short stories and especially the early sub-Plots of a larger Conflict / Plot | Dominant, Pressed, Above, DeeperShadows |
| Internalised | Conflict(3x3) | 4 | The ideal size for smaller stories with multiple sides and medium complexity. Internalised Conflicts have at least one side have some internal strife (often the Dominant or Pressed side). | Dominant, Pressed, Above, Internal, Shadows, DeeperShadows |

### Act Spreads

Each Plot has Acts and an Act in T13 has its own unique card spread that helps define it.

#### Frame Act Spread

The Frame Act Spread includes a Hook Spread for each Side (or Character) that must be hooked in the conflict, and one Revelation Spread that defines the Plot somehow.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Frame Act Spread** *(Handsize: 15)*
> *Frame spread positions and example draw:*
>
> 📍 **Position: Hook 1**
> - **Position Description:** *Hook for Side 1*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃁 **Ace of Diamonds**
> - **Hook:** *Opportunity*
> - **Hook Description:** *The Story hooks Characters by offering them an opportunity to Gain something they want or need to fulfil an ambition.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Hook Aspect 1**
> - **Position Description:** *Aspect for Hook 1*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃍 **Queen of Diamonds**
> - **Hook Aspect:** *Geometry (Yang)*
> - **Aspect Description:** *The Hook targets the Character's Geometry specifically how they generate Yang and creates a situation that allows them to generate Yang this way.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Hook 2**
> - **Position Description:** *Hook for Side 2*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🂺 **Ten of Hearts**
> - **Hook:** *Entrance*
> - **Hook Description:** *The Hook is literally an entrance. This usually refers to the sudden, dramatic appearance of a new or returning Embodiment, but it can also be an opening, door or any threshold, or cliff-edge moment. In Old Comedies the players would sing as they paraded in, setting up the story with the first song, in old serials they would show how the Cliff-Hanger ending of the last Chapter was resolved (often with a retcon).*
>
> 📍 **Position: Hook Aspect 2**
> - **Position Description:** *Aspect for Hook 2*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃘 **Eight of Clubs**
> - **Hook Aspect:** *Skills*
> - **Aspect Description:** *The Hook targets the Character's Skills indicating that only someone with those precise skills can truly solve this problem. The opposition may even be targeting people known to possess certain Skills.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Revelation About**
> - **Position Description:** *What the revelation is about*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🂦 **Six of Spades**
> - **About:** *External*
> - **About Description:** *The information revealed is about the External Conflict Embodiment. This is a side that is considered outside of the normal Conflict, this could be aliens, or more likely, the next town over.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Revelation Vector**
> - **Position Description:** *How it is revealed*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃂 **Two of Diamonds**
> - **Vector:** *Phantasm*
> - **Vector Description:** *The information is conveyed by some supernatural means, often a ghost or spirit that appears and brings word of importance (often their own passing). If you are not comfortable with a ghost in your cyberpunk universe, you can always use a Cyberwraith, a digital soul, or transhuman, or phantasms can also be a holographic image, an illusion or hallucination.*

> - **Position Description:** *The information revealed*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃎 **King of Diamonds**
> - **Info:** *How?*
> - **Info Description:** *How exactly is what they plan to do, going to be done? You may get the full plan, or just the important pieces. Sometimes you might even know how to stop them, but not what they plan to do. It all depends on the situation at hand.*

#### Loom Act Spread

Loom Spreads consist of Pairs of Warp and Weft Spreads, or Weft and Warp if you prefer, for each Side in the Conflict. The Loom has as many Warp-Weft pairs as the Yarn-Teller wishes (although usually you will have at least one pair per Side plus another pair). Loom Spreads can become more complicated with complex narrative-weaving techniques.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Loom Spread** *(Handsize: 15)*
> *Loom spread positions and example draw:*
>
> 📍 **Position: Warp 1 Fray**
> - **Position Description:** *Fray for Warp 1*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃁 **Ace of Diamonds**
> - **Fray:** *Quest*
> - **Fray Description:** *The Fray is a Quest, usually based on the Facets of the Conflict, for something the PC requires or desires.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Warp 1 Snag**

Often the Fool will encounter a lesson in the new location, or maybe just rest, heal up a bit, and maybe get a bite to eat or a little spending cash on the way.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Weft 1 Option A**

> - **Fray Description:** *The Fray is an insubstantial, or trifling matter, but failure may have profound consequences later. An example may be a single question asked by a co-worker, it seems unimportant at the time, but is actually part of an human resources investigation. Usually a Test on a single Facet (A,C,O,Q,V,Z) (although an appropriate Annex might help). The difficulty is usually quite low (between 1-15). Facet Tests are meant to be quick, easy checks, a simple roll. Failures may involve negative Psyche effects.*

##### Warp Spreads

Warp Scenes require Warp Spreads. These help define the Warp. No cards are required for "The Ends" of the Warp, as these are defined by what the Characters are trying to achieve. "The Fray" and "The Snag" both require 1 card that define what they are (which may require more cards such as in an Ordeal Spread).


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Warp** *(Handsize: 14)*
> *Warp spread positions and example draw:*
>

> - **Position Description:** *The conflict or struggle.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃁 **Ace of Diamonds**
> - **Fray:** *Quest*
> - **Fray Description:** *The Fray is a Quest, usually based on the Facets of the Conflict, for something the PC requires or desires.*
>
> 📍 **Position: The Snag**
> - **Position Description:** *The complication.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃍 **Queen of Diamonds**
> - **Snag:** *Requirements*

##### Weft Spread

Weft Spreads define firstly how bad the emotional backlash was to the last Warp with a "Recoiling" spread of 0 to 3 cards. This is treated as a Psych attack on the Characters. Then 1 card sets the "Sweeping" up. Finally "Picking" must define the possible choices the Characters can make, these are usually narrative choices not connected to the cards, but cards can be used to provide "routes" if required.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Weft** *(Handsize: 14)*
> *Weft spread positions and example draw:*
>
> 📍 **Position: Sweeping**
> - **Position Description:** *Recovery or gain.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃁 **Ace of Diamonds**
> - **Sweeping:** *Win*
> - **Sweeping Text:** *The Pressed side has a Win, this may be a battle against a weak or weakened foe, or they might catch a lucky break, or lottery win. The Win doesn't have to be big, just enough to keep hope alive, but a Gain is in order. Perhaps even the Resolution of a Hitch.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Picking Option 1**
> - **Position Description:** *First choice for the next path.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃍 **Queen of Diamonds**
> - **Fray:** *Creativity*
> - **Fray Description:** *The Ordeal is about the Character acquiring a Descendant. Maybe they go shopping and hunt around for a deal, but usually they make one themselves (or summon one magically, perhaps). The point is that they have to beat a Summoning / Creation difficulty somehow.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Picking Option 2**
> - **Position Description:** *Second choice for the next path.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🂺 **Ten of Hearts**
> - **Fray:** *Insult Tennis*
> - **Fray Description:** *Insult tennis is a Low-Medium Stakes Opposed Ordeal, the PCs and their foes or Rivals exchange insults, and cutting remarks. In effect the Tennis is a battle or duel fought to the first blood (although in this case first emotional outburst) with Psych attacks.*

##### Logue Act Spreads

Logue Spreads are formed from a Revelation primarily, but may also have an optional Warp and/or Weft Spread. Logues usually are one Sided, affecting and involving only one of the Conflict Sides directly, although more can be involved especially in Revelation and Warp Scenes.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Logue** *(Handsize: 15)*

All Scenes for example will have at least two cards that serves as a Significator for that Scene and define the Beat Type for the Scene. Different Beat Types require different additional cards.

### Ordeal Spreads

Argueably both the most complex and most fun Scene type is the Ordeal. Ordeals can use lots of cards to define details of themselves, with a card for the Stakes, and optional cards for defining the Test, Suggested Action, Stages (including in some cases the whole map), Obstacles, Motional and Types of Ordeal Stage Spreads. For more details about Ordeals see the Ordeal Rules page, but here is a summary of Ordeal types and how they use cards.


### Ordeal Types

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

If they have to build a fire in the forge as the first step and fail, they will never forge that sword. There may be choices (from Kings, etc) in individual Stages, but the choices are generally irrelevant to the next Stage. It matters very little if the forge is lit with wood or paper, or if the main fuel is charcoal or coal, or if the bellows are pumped by hand or foot. | railroad.svg |
| Forking | Any activity that has simple choices, with limited repercussions from those choices. ### Revelations (and Lores)

Revelations can vary in number of cards from 3 to 12. Although sometimes the dealt cards are no more than suggestions if you know what the Revelation is going to reveal already.

The first Revelation card reveals what the Revelation is About, what fact is being revealed here? The Second card is the Vector, how is the information being revealed. The Third card reveals the actual information reveal, and you have a choice on each card of Info or Alternate to reveal.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Revelation** *(Handsize: 3)*
> *Revelation spread positions and example draw:*
>
> 📍 **Position: Significator**
> - **Position Description:** *The theme of the revelation.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃁 **Ace of Diamonds**
> - **Significator:** *[object Object]*
> - **Yarn Name:** *The Ambition*
> - **Yarn Description:** *The Card of new Ambitions, sudden monetary gains, new business opportunities, new treasures or rumours of treasures and money being made. There may be conflict and collisions, new obstacles in the way or a Rival's ambitions may block progress.*
>
> 📍 **Position: About**
> - **Position Description:** *What the revelation is about.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃍 **Queen of Diamonds**
> - **About:** *McGuffin*
> - **About Description:** *The information is about a piece of equipment, treasure or device. A physical (Prop) Descendant that may be required or just desired by the Character. This Descendant is also sometimes known as a Plot Device, Plot Coupon, fetch-quest object, the Dooberry, Big Dumb Object, and the Weenie.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Vector**
> - **Position Description:** *How the information is revealed.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🂺 **Ten of Hearts**
> - **Vector:** *Entertainment*
> - **Vector Description:** *The information is embedded or encoded in a piece of entertainment, this might be a case of the infamous "play-within-a-play", a novelist who commits a murder exactly like one they have written in a book, or a song sung by a court-jester that derides the King's cousin and his many short-comings. Bards and Heralds for centuries used folk songs to create and spread news stories and gossip, and this is something similar.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Info**
> - **Position Description:** *The specific information revealed.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🃘 **Eight of Clubs**
> - **Info:** *How Much?*
> - **Info Description:** *Information that tells us something about amounts, volumes, or weights. It could be as simple as a receipt, or a tax return, or as complex as the number of work-hours something might take, but often reveals something more interesting than you'd think.*
>
> 📍 **Position: Alternate**
> - **Position Description:** *Alternative interpretation or info.*
> - **Example Drawn Card:** 🂦 **Six of Spades**

You should also note that every Revelation also reveals a potential Lore about one of the Characters (usually the opposition). Bigger Revelations can create more powerful Lores as you should see.

## Plight or Situation Spreads

Situation spreads are great for setting up a small problem, or Plight that is larger than an Obstacle, but not as important as a Plot. A Plight Spread usually describes a Quest that the Character must accomplish as some additional complication to a larger Plot (although you can run perfectly good games just using Plights and Pantsing...).


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Plight** *(Handsize: 3)*



## Quest Spreads

Quest spreads are a simple Narrative device that creates a Quest. Quests are situations that must be completed by Characters for various reasons, sometimes for a reward, most often out of duty, devotion or some other Hitch.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Quest** *(Handsize: 6)*



## Geas Spread

Geas spreads are a 6 card spread that reuses the same cards to create both a Quest, a Revelation and a Lore. The Revelation usually works to reveal both the Lore about the Character, such as the nature of the curse, taboo, duty, devotion or whatever enforces the Geas, and often something about nature of the Quest.


> 🃏 **Card Spread: Geas** *(Handsize: 6)*
