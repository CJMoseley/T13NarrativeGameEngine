# Card Guide: Hearts

This guide provides the complete mechanical, narrative, and Yarn specifications for all 13 cards of the Hearts suit in the T13 Narrative Game Engine.

## 🂱 Ace of Hearts

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **14**
- **Wound Type:** **Carnage** *(Wound Level 6)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Enigma, Heresy, Jeer, Liberty, Nature, Phoenix*
- **General Meaning:** *Aces signify beginnings, meetings and unions. They can indicate love-interests, chance encounters, alliances, and friendships.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Ignore Wounds**
- **Normal Position:** *Target may ignore all Wound effects while atop their pile.*
- **Crossed Position:** *All Target Wounds affect as though one level higher.*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Blue Aquatic or Black Migratory**
- **Bulmas Type:** **All**
- **Animal Category:** *All-path*
- **Example Animal:** *All-Walkers*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Heart*
- **Lea Notes:** *The Paths of the All-Walkers can allow Bulmäs to do extraordinary things, like walking an individual’s life-path, so that all of their personal history is known to them. All-Walkers can assume even the forms and souls of other people by using these paths, without directly eating of the Soul. All-Walkers can even Doom Weave and play other cards from their hands as Wyrd Tarot cards, once they have played an Ace on themselves.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *7*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Map-Geography*
- **Stakes:** *Blended Low-Medium-High-Extreme*
- **Test:** *Instincts (Nature)*
- **Obstacles:** *1 per Stage and 3 per Court Stage*
- **Suggested Action:** *Flirt*
- **Stage:** *A dangerous Stunt with flames and explosions, or a medical emergency.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *This Opposed Ordeal involves trying to get through the Stages of dangerous terrain in a vehicle, before the Opposition.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *Bullets are flying and things are exploding, its mayhem wherever you look. No one has any advantages, everyone is threatening or suppressing everyone else, but even attempting to close to melee is likely to involve getting shot.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The fight is a training session, or bout, this is a Low Stakes Fight, intended to teach Proficiencies or similar.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Lovers** — *The Lovers card represents the forces of romance and love, and the tough choices that they bring. As a force, Love is usually seen as positive, building and holding together families even through the most trying times, but love can also be destructive. The torrid affairs of untamed hearts ruin families' lives and destroy homes everyday. These are powerful forces, Soul Stakes that play with people' hearts. They are not for the faint of heart, but then what romantic is truly a coward?*
- **Hook Event:** **Who is That?** — *The Story opens with a new Character appearing who is of interest to the PC (usually as a friend or potential new love interest).*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Geometry (Perfect)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Geometry via their Perfect Harmonic, this may be a soul mate, best friend, lover or just a Perfect business partner.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Monster Lover - A Powerful Ally that even has some Monster Facets.*
- **The Fray:** **Test of Love** — *The Fray is a Test, Ordeal or Quest, usually set by the PC's Romantic Interest as a Test of their Love. Such tests can include humiliating yourself, surviving battle, or going against your normal nature, to prove that your have grown as an individual. Tests of Love can also include Quests where the Quest giver is a friend or member of the family.*
- **The Snag:** **Love Triangle/Jealousy** — *The Embodiment's love interest (who may be the Opposed Embodiment / a Rival / another PC, etc) and the Embodiment's relationship is complicated by a third-party. This may be a Love Rival of the Embodiment, who will try to win the love interest's heart, or it may be an alternative love interest, or any variation (the adulterous husband/wife). This Snag can act as a Hook for a Story in its own right to explore the Tension inherent in the Love Triangle. Or perhaps The Character's Rival or the Opposition Gains a Romantic Interest. Ideally this should be someone the Character is already interested in, but will otherwise just be someone wonderful. If there is no Romantic sub-plot currently in play, then the Opposition makes an important alliance / treaty that will cause the PCs to become jealous.*
- **Sweeping:** **Love** — *The Pressed Embodiment finds love, or a reasonable facsimile there of. This might involve a Gain of an Ally, Romantic Interest, or even a pet. Or it could just be a de-stressing evening in a night club that turned into breakfast.*
- **History Context:** **Affair** — *The Lovers indicates that this current situation owes something to a often passionate and sometimes illicit affair between at least two of the characters involved in the situation. This often implies past love triangles, flirtatious behaviour, intrigues and attachments which will complicate the current problem.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Choice** — *The Lovers indicates a serious choice that must be made by the character, often between two potential lovers, or even lives. Often there is little to decide the issue between these choices, they are rarely so clear cut as one is good and one is bad, but rather each has a balance of pros and cons. This choice is intended to be difficult and often permanent.*
- **Solution:** **Alliance** — *The Lovers indicates that the true way out of the current situation is through an alliance (historically secured with a marriage), sometimes between conflicting sides, but more often with a neutral, but powerful third-party.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Love Interest** — The information is about a Character's Love Interest, it may be a potential new love Interest, or just something you never knew about the one you have.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Decisions** — The Information revealed is about the decisions that the Character must make, or decisions they have made in the past that reveal pertinent things about their character.
  - *Vector (How):* **Whispers** — The information is a secret, perhaps an intimate or delicate one, that is shared in a whisper. Often such whispers can be misheard as they spread, but some inkling of truth should still be discernible.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Happiness** — Information about the happiness, or not, of the Embodiment(s) or population at large. Perhaps there is a holiday approaching...
  - *Detail:* **Intimate** — This is an intimate detail about a character, such as, who are their best friends, who are their past and present lovers, who is their confidante, who do they trust, and sometimes even details about intimate preferences.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Hardened** — *Sometimes a Character can become hardened, insensitive and incapable of caring. Hardboiled detectives, grizzled war veterans and the like, shrug off the ability to heal themselves and empathise, in favour of losing Stress.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character must discard all Hearts (<span class="t13ne-redcard">♥</span>) cards from Ordeal Pool, Style Reserves and Wyrd Tarot Hand, until they have zero Stress. Each Heart Discarded removes Pips Stress, or 1 Strain or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Romance** — *Trauma can change Characters in wonderful and terrible ways, making them more open, truthful, emotional, or simply causing them to show a more vulnerable side for a while. Vulnerability and emotional-openness can be profoundly attractive, and being openly vulnerable and being cared for by another can also be attractive in return. Although it should be noted that this is rarely the case with professional carers such as Doctors or Nurses, where ethics committees and professional bodies would have something to say about it. Such Romances are often short-lived things, although as with any romance Soul-Stakes are involved.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma can alter both Impressions of the Character and the Character’s Impressions of others (increasing the impressions one step for each Stress Relieved) to make them completely trusting of each other, and can create a "Will-They-Won&apos;t-They" Soul-Stakes romance Plot between the two Characters.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Romance** — *An Age of Romance is occurring. During this age narratived (often at least one Arc Plot and several Stories) will shape the society, often these narratives include love, attraction and betrayals, usually this narrative is based upon a choice, perhaps a society will be presented with two potential alliances, siding with one will alienate the other, another example is that two sides within society are opposed to each other, within that context members of both sides fall in love. Note that during an age of Romance many narratives may unfold, concurrently and in series.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Romance the entire age is woven by Plots for the society. Embodiments will be chosen exclusively from within the society. An age of romance can tie directly into Mythic Cycles for that universe, although that is not in any way guarranteed.

## 🂲 Two of Hearts

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **2**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Phoenix*
- **General Meaning:** *Twos signify conflict, opposition and choices. They can indicate crossroads, partings, separations, and changes of direction.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Friend**
- **Normal Position:** *Player and Target are "Confidantes". The highest level of interaction possible.*
- **Crossed Position:** *Player and Target move to a lower, more negative Interaction Type (by one level).*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Blue Aquatic or Black Migratory**
- **Bulmas Type:** **All**
- **Animal Category:** *Microfauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Plankton / Zooplankton / Fungal spores / Bacteria*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Tendrils*
- **Lea Notes:** *Microscopic animal and bacterial paths, often overlooked.*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *1*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Low*
- **Test:** *Abuse (Sin)*
- **Obstacles:** *None*
- **Suggested Action:** *Recover/Wait*
- **Stage:** *A busy road, or path, a social occasion or situation.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Get you and your car through the Stages of the Ordeal faster than your Opponents.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The Ebbing enemy breaks, some of their number flee (or attempt to regroup), the Flowing forces can pursue with a +1 Pip to each card played, or can take this time to regroup themselves.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The Fight is a competitive bout of a contact sport, such as a boxing match, or fencing. Such Fights are usually Medium or Low Stakes against at least Cast Extras.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Burn-Out** — *The Burn-Out is the card that represents the lingering embers of the once great. Where once a mighty, powerful fire roared, now there is just smoke and warm ashes. It can represent collapse after a great effort, or a futility in beginning.*
- **Hook Event:** **Smoke Signals** — *There's no smoke without fire, people say. So when this Conflict starts rumours and gossip, leaving smoking guns and other clues that point to something being up, you can be sure the PCs will jump to all the wrong conclusions, or if they don't someone else will interpret the same signals as the PCs guilt.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona (Shadow)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona specifically via the Shadow of the Persona, which means appealing to the darker nature of the Persona.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Someone's (potential Rival, Ally or Opposed Embodiment) contact details*
- **The Fray:** **Burn-out** — *This Fray is an intense Ordeal designed to exhaust the PC to the point of collapse. This may involve staying active for far too long (working night and day for a week), or expending all of your fuel or ammo during the Ordeal. Exactly how the Fray exhausts the Character' resources will depend on the Character and Situation. Generally speaking the Stakes of the Ordeal are usually Medium or High. It is usual during a Burn-Out for the Plot to use Carnage Wounds (and Carnage+) as defence cards, not attack. Ideally the Ordeal will end with a Fatigue Hitch being triggered.*
- **The Snag:** **Futile Pursuit** — *Regardless of the Fray result, the Snag makes clear that the Fray was a wasted effort. Even though you succeeded, it is a useless success, perhaps you plan the perfect Heist only to discover they emptied the Vault hours before, or you fight your way to the Vampire's castle, only to watch it vanish as you reach the walls.*
- **Sweeping:** **Ashes** — *The Pressed Side has an Test or minor Ordeal that they must deal with. Such as clearing the ash from the Forge after making a sword.*
- **History Context:** **Collapse** — *The Burn-Out indicates that the current situation is rooted in a collapse in the past. This might be a mental, emotional, political, social, economic, or structural collapse, but this current situation is fall out from this event.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **History** — *The Burn-Out indicates the current situation is an echo or repeat of some previous event. This may be a case of history literally repeating itself, or it may be a more figurative, with things generally following cycles of growth and collapse.*
- **Solution:** **Exhaustion** — *The Burn-Out indicates that sometimes there is no easy solution to a problem, and that the best we can hope for is the enemy to exhaust themselves somehow, perhaps even getting bored and going home. This situation has no solution, unless you can work out how to wear down the opposition forces, making them expend resources and troops.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Former Glories** — Some people and things have seem better days, this is information about somethings former glories. Did they once win a gold medal? A science prize? Were they the largest city port in the west long ago?
  - *Vector (How):* **Red Herring** — False information can be as useful as true information, sometimes. Maybe not this time, as the information is actually incorrect or misleading somehow.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Friends** — Knowledge of a Character's friends and allies can tell you a lot about a person.
  - *Detail:* **Remains** — Remains details are the often overlooked lingering details, it is the trace of paper ash on a saucer, the clinging strands of DNA that respond to UV light, the fingerprints of a killer on a blade, and of course the dismembered corpse of a serial killer's victim.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Worries** — *Worries make a Character think about their issues, dwelling on what questions or issues are stressing them out.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Character may reduce their Stress, Strain or Shock by 1 for each question they ask, or statement they make, that relates to their worries, or each Pip that they dedicate to "Healing Prayers", "Meditation" or similar Stress reduction techniques, or they may pay Chi equal to their current Stress to remove 1 Stress.
- **Trauma Type:** **Burn-Out** — *Trauma can cause a Character to burn-out, whether through fatigue, mental exhaustion, or emotional breakdown. Depression can overwhelm anyone, and will often cause a Character to abandon projects and even relationships. A Burned-out Character should usually be retired from active duty, but may struggle on a shadow of their former selves.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** Trauma causes the Character to Burn-Out, this usually includes relieving all current Stress by creating "Burnt-Out" Hitches that effectively negate the Character’s Annexes. These Hitches can effect only one Annex or all Annexes as the Player, Author, Referee and Yarn-Teller agree.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Collapse** — *An Age of Collapse is occurring. During this age the society suffers some setback or blow, members may be lost, people may flee the area and society, another society may grow more popular, this may be a temporary setback, a compacting, or it can represent a complete collapse to nothingness.*
  > **Age Rules:** During a Collapse a society loses a significant number of members due to some external factor, sometimes dramatic geo-political events such as war, famine, pestilence or plague, other times members simply drift away to find new hobbies, or lands.

## 🂳 Three of Hearts

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **3**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Enigma*
- **General Meaning:** *Threes signify creativity and and communication. They can indicate hope, imagination, inspiration and conversations.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Inept**
- **Normal Position:** *-1 Prof Die on Target's next roll.*
- **Crossed Position:** *Player gains an additional, applicable Proficiency (May fill a slot).*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Blue Aquatic or Black Migratory**
- **Bulmas Type:** **All**
- **Animal Category:** *Meiofauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Small Nematode worms / shrimps*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Gut*
- **Lea Notes:** *A lot of parasite paths at this level*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *2*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Railroad*
- **Stakes:** *Medium*
- **Test:** *Questioning (Enigma)*
- **Obstacles:** *None*
- **Suggested Action:** *Accuse*
- **Stage:** *Either you need to formulate a question or an answer, usually while lots of other people ask theirs. This might be the first stage of the Scientific method, or you may be at a press conference. Otherwise you are looking at a cul-de-sac style turn, that might have a way out, if you are willing to think outside the box (or at least off the road).*
- **Motional Aspect:** *Those magnificent men in their flying machines are trying to get through the Stages faster than you.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The battle is loud and distracting, but there is a rhythm to it. Roll 1d3, during each Phase of the Stage, any Character of either Flowing or Ebbing side that makes an Action that does not play that many cards will automatically take an Unsoakable Distract Wound if Flowing and an Unsoakable Flesh Wound if Ebbing.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The Scrap is a usually Medium Stakes punch-up or street fight, between more than two people (at least Chorus Extras), who each are fighting individually in a chaotic, last-man-standing way. The Scrap can shift to High Stakes if someone pulls a weapon, or starts stamping on necks.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Question** — *The Question card stands for nothing more, or less, than a question. It could be an unspoken question, or a question that is asked over and over again. Sometimes the question will be the same, other times it will be unique. Sometimes the question has an answer, other times it is rhetorical, and just occasionally the answer itself is the question.*
- **Hook Event:** **Question** — *The Conflict Hooks the Character a question. This Question may be one they ask themselves, "What was that noise?", "Who is at the door?", or it might be a question that they overhear, "How many men have you got for the job?". The Hook may even be a mystery in its own right.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona (Question)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona, but via a Question based upon this Facet. For example an Idol will be Questioned about their Feelings.*
- **Gain Reward:** *An truthful, honest answer (sometimes the best Gain of all).*
- **The Fray:** **Questioning** — *Questioning can be a terrible Fray. Inquisitions, interrogations, inquiries, examinations. Questioning can be a Low to Soul Stakes Ordeal, or even a simple Facet Test. Usually the PCs are the one's questioned, as the Challenge is normally greater that way.*
- **The Snag:** **Puzzle** — *The Snag is a Puzzle, some additional complication. Maybe the Fray is set in a Maze, or having completed the Fray the PCs are now faced with a riddle that must be solved to continue. Perhaps Clues must be deciphered, or the PCs have to unlock a combination lock to open a door, or maybe the door itself is hidden, and the Puzzle is a secret room.*
- **Sweeping:** **Deeper Questions** — *The PCs make a Revelation, that reveals more complications and deeper questions for the future.*
- **History Context:** **Shrouded** — *The Question indicates that the history of this situation is shrouded by the mists of time, there will be mysteries, questions and enigmas that hide the truth.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Query** — *The Question indicates the current situation involves trying to find an answer to a question, this question may be simple or a complex cross-examination.*
- **Solution:** **Doubt** — *The Question indicates that the solution to this situation must rely on not taking any fact for granted, trust no one, and question everything that you think you know.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Facets** — Information about the Facets of a Character or Plot, what are they good at, what are they bad at. The information can be relative to the Character themselves (she is much Stronger in attack than defence), or to another (he is much better-looking than you), or be absolute (the standard unit can lift 3 tonnes, but only moves at a slow walking pace).
  - *Vector (How):* **Quandary** — Sometimes the information given  is a secret itself, perhaps hidden in riddling rhymes and puzzlingly ambiguous statements. It might be a coincidental clue in a crossword puzzle, a question asked by a Sphinx, an ancient symbolic prophecy, or a substitution cipher scrawled in blood by a dying man. The information is available, but a clever mind will be required to reveal all.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Injuries** — The information is about injuries, perhaps damage that something has taken in the past, or what *really* happened to the test-pilot...
  - *Detail:* **Questions** — Everyone has questions, these details are questions that either the Embodiment itself would ask, or those who know the Embodiment should be asking. These might include "Who out of all my lieutenants is most likely to be behind this coup?", "What is the optimum number of guards for a secret base this size?", "Where will the enemy strike back?", "When is the best time of year to invade Russia? I mean everyone knows the worst time...", "Which says arch-mage better, polymorphing their king into a frog, forcing their entire kingdom to sleep for a thousand years, trapping their capitol city in a painting or turning them all into statues?", or "How many weapons does one man really need to be carrying?"

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Questioning** — *Asking the right questions at the right time can alleviate a lot of Stress for some people. Knowing what might be on the Test can make revision easier, for example.*
  > **Stress Rules:** A Character may relieve Stress equal to the current Tension Level by asking any Character for advice about their problems.
- **Trauma Type:** **Questions** — *Trauma is bad enough, but sometimes traumas can be compounded by additional problems, such as inquests, political inquiries, insurance problems, as well as disputes over responsibilities and liabities, which all involve a number of very pointed question being asked of the Character.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma creates Questions for the Character to answer, these questions are usually based on the Character’s Annex Facets, and the Difficulty in answering the Question is usually based on the amount of Stress relieved from the Annex, with the Stress acting as a Chi (Pips) Difficulty usually.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Inquisition** — *An Age of Inquisition is occurring. During this age some power arises within the society that calls for some investigation of the society. Often an inquisition is religious or political in nature, rooting out heretics, dissidents, terrorists, or revolutionaries, but scientific and medical inquisitions can also occur. Inquisitions are usually characterised by a loss of individual rights, prejudicial examiners, biased courts and cruel and unusual punishments.*
  > **Age Rules:** Some faction within the society achieves a position of power and uses it to root out supposed opposition, usually in the form of a witch hunt or inquisition against this supposed opposition. Success in this inquisition can be beneficial for a society (if they have been infiltrated by a foreign power for example), but failure to find supposed opposition while being convinced of its existence will have a negative effect upon the society, paranoia and mistrust can quickly destroy a society from within. A failing inquisition will sometimes create new factions who are opposed to the inquisition which can tear a society apart.

## 🂴 Four of Hearts

### 📊 Mechanical Statistics
- **Pips (Value):** **4**
- **Wound Type:** **Distract** *(Wound Level 1)*
- **Associated Facets:** *Heresy*
- **General Meaning:** *Fours signify stability and foundations. They usually indicate some foundation, whether an organization or a building, that may have its foundation weakened, damaged or strengthened by the narrative.*

### 🔮 Tarot & Wyrd Tarot
- **Wyrd Tarot:** **Rally**
- **Normal Position:** *+1 Success Level on any Psychology Test*
- **Crossed Position:** *+1 Failure Level on any Psychology Test*

### 🌿 Lea & Bulmas (Animal & Nature Aspects)
- **Path Color:** **Blue Aquatic or Black Migratory**
- **Bulmas Type:** **All**
- **Animal Category:** *Mesofauna*
- **Example Animal:** *Greenfly / Midge / Maggots / Woodlice*
- **Bodypart Association:** *Antenna*
- **Lea Notes:** *Tiny Arthropods*

### ⚔️ Ordeal & Combat Aspects
- **Ordeal Type:** *Opposed Ordeal*
- **Stages:** *2*
- **Ordeal Spread:** *Forking*
- **Stakes:** *High*
- **Test:** *Knowledge (Orthodox)*
- **Obstacles:** *None*
- **Suggested Action:** *Lie*
- **Stage:** *A large crowd of people, an audience, a stadium or theatre. A convention or conference.*
- **Motional Aspect:** *First to finish wins. Sometimes they race for pink slips.*
- **Tide of Battle:** *The battle is now out of anyone's control. Civilians may be caught in the crossfire, or some other unforeseen problem has occurred. No side has clear advantage.*
- **Fight / Conflict:** *The fight takes place in a single, usually, defensive location. The location is assaulted by a stream, or waves, of lesser or equal (Vex - Cast)opponents. Generally, the defending side is waiting for back-up, or relief to arrive.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Myth** — *The Myth represents those untruths that can gather a truth of their own. It maybe a simple lie that was believed, a complex misunderstanding, or story told as an allegory or warning, such as the stories of Ginny Greenteeth teach children to avoid water, and the myth of the the Big Bad Wolf teach them to avoid the dark woods.*
- **Hook Event:** **Mistake** — *The story hooks the character with an apparent mistake. A misdelivered parcel, or message, or some other confusion. Often opens a comedy.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona (Avoid)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona specifically by using what they like to Avoid as a stick, and perhaps offering a Persona Gain as a carrot if that fails.*
- **Gain Reward:** *A Lore Descendant (as part of a Revelation about themselves)*
- **The Fray:** **Legend** — *The Fray concerns a Legend. Maybe the PCs need to solve an ancient mystery to find a legendary treasure, defeat a legendary Monster, find a legendary place, or otherwise achieve the apparently impossible.*
- **The Snag:** **Fable** — *Either the Fray involves the PCs having to lie for all they're worth, or they make a false Revelation. The False Revelation may retcon the last Revelation (making everything learned false) or it can make the next Revelation false, as the Yarn-Teller prefers.*
- **Sweeping:** **Myth Revealed** — *A Lie from earlier is revealed (such as information from an earlier Revelation, or some assumption the PCs made).*
- **History Context:** **Mythic** — *The Myth indicates that the past situation is based on a legend, folklore, or myth. This may be a huge myth such as Arthurian legends or local folklore.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Untruths** — *The Myth indicates that the current situation has been caused by misinformation, deceit, conspiracy theories, fake news or just lies.*
- **Solution:** **Lie** — *The Myth indicates that the way out of the current situation is to lie for all you are worth, bluff, misdirect, lie, fake, feign, falsify and forge it until you make it.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Lies** — The Information is about lies that have been told, sometimes the truth is revealed, and sometimes we just know that a lie has been told without knowing the actual truth.
  - *Vector (How):* **A Lie** — A Lie reveals the information somehow. Perhaps the falsehood protects someone else, or the lie may be part of an alibi that cannot be true, or maybe the lie is an extrapolation of an actual truth that they didn't realise they were revealing, and do they know they have been caught out?
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Myths** — Myths inevitably form around things, no story is ever retold without some form of mythologising the subject. This alternate information is about the myths that surround the Embodiment, these are exaggerations and misinformation that usually make things seem more powerful than they actually are.
  - *Detail:* **Cultural** — Cultural details tell us about the culture of a Character, or the people that constructed, or live around a Descendant. You might want to reveal they are a warrior people, how is this expressed? Does every adult carry a sword, a shield, or a gun at all times? Do they wear armour as part of their uniform, or is it something more subtle, like chess being the national game, their cultural heroes are all military leaders from their past, or ammo pockets and bandoleers are really in fashion right now?

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Coping** — *Coping mechanisms for stress vary from person to person. Some people drink, or take drugs (both illegal and medically prescribed), some throw themselves into religion, a hobby or work, and a few may cut or tattoo themselves. These coping mechanisms can grow rapidly becoming Flaws or even Woes as a Character relies on them to cope with Stress.*

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Joke** — *The Joke card represents a funny situation. It may be as simple as a pun, or as complicated as a wildly complex sequence of rational, normal seeming events that leads to an ultimately absurd situation.*
- **Hook Event:** **Set up** — *The set up is the beginning of the joke, in a comedy this is usually a misunderstanding, in a shorter joke this is a question, somewhere in between it is a sentence or two that creates a narrative framework that misleads the audience. At its very simplest it is a cliché, such as a bunch of adventurers walk into a bar. Occasionally, it is a physical set up to a prank or stunt that will be important later. And every now and then it is a literal set up, with a situation created to put the PCs in the Narrative.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona (Motivation)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona specifically via their Motivation. An Idol may be tempted by Social Interactions and a crowd of cheering grateful masses.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Friends (a Group of 2-12 Vexes, or 2-6 Chorus Extras)*
- **The Fray:** **Caper** — *Capers are usually Low to Medium Stakes (although Higher Stakes Capers are possible) where multiple deceptions, confusions and complex physical activities occur that are all connected to trying to achieve some goal, often a heist or con-game. Humour is usually part of a Caper, such as swapping priceless jewels for plastic kids jewellery, or million-dollar bonds for monopoly money. In a real criminal enterprise such frivolities would create additional leads for the police, during a Caper though they will just confound, bemuse and amuse.*
- **The Snag:** **Shenanigans** — *Someone, or occasionally something, is out to cause mischief. The Fray may be complicated by additional pranks, deceptions, antics and high-jinx. Then again, perhaps following the Fray there is some additional escapades of tom-foolery that the PCs must suffer through. This Snag can often be a sign that a capricious foe or Rival is involved in the Plot, such as those who carry F&aelig;ry-blood.*
- **Sweeping:** **Comic Relief** — *Comedy is an easy way to release Tension. A joke during a Tense moment can ruin a Thriller though, so use carefully. Comedy Relief can help Characters come to terms with recent events though. Often a Character may be comic relief, a Comic relief Character receives*
- **History Context:** **Funny** — *The Joke indicates that the history of the current situation is funny somehow, there may be a joke, or just some humorous twists and situations.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Jape** — *The Joke indicates the current situation is not a serious one, it may be a joke, a prank or simply a funny story. This is not usually a dangerous situation, although sometimes it might appear so at first. Sometimes the humour may begin to run amok and a Jape can become a farce, where anything that can go wrong will, at least humorously.*
- **Solution:** **Humiliation** — *The Joke indicates that the way out of the current situation is through humiliation, this may mean humiliating some one else (often a bully) or humiliating yourself.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Grotesque** — The information gained is in the form of a grotesque, this is information about how the Embodiment is viewed by others, but not simply a description. This is more a caricature, with the worst features emphasised usually for comedic effect. At their simplest grotesques can seem little more than a "Your Moma" joke, but a truly great grotesque will not only draw attention to some ugliness of spirit, but will draw some hybrid like comparison to an animal, that matches the Embodiment's temperament.

  - *Alternate (Special):* **Humour** — This alternate information is about the humour that surrounds the Embodiment. If they are a character this may be their own preferred style of humour, perhaps even their favourite joke, occasionally (and for Descendants, Locations, etc) this humour will be about or feature the Embodiment, it is a joke told about them, or about the place, or that features them.
  - *Detail:* **Fun** — Fun details are those weird pieces of information that are often surprising and somewhat incongruous with other knowledge about them. This can lead to these details being known as "Fun Facts".

- **Stress Type:** **Joking** — *Stress can make Characters feel tense and a natural way to relieve that tension and Stress is by making jokes, even if they are inappropriate to the situation at hand.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Making a joke will relieve 1 Stress at any time, even if no one laughs because it is so inappropriate a joke or time.
- **Trauma Type:** **Humour** — *Trauma can alter a Character’s sense of humour dramatically, things they previously found funny, may now seem cruel and malicious. Conversely, things the Character previously would have found, trite, sickening, morbid, or boring may now strike them as hilarious. Some Character’s find their sense of humour is destroyed by a trauma, where as others find it to be the ultimate coping mechanism. For others it is a change in styles of humour, moving from whatever they did find funny to puns and wordplay, or sarcasm, satire, prop-comedy, wit, shaggy-dog stories, offensive jokes, sick jokes, crude comedy, physical clowning, sight gags, punchlines, or dad jokes.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma alters the Character’s sense of humour in some way. The Relieved Stress from each Annex will change one thing the Character finds funny, either finding something new funny or not liking a style of humour they previously enjoyed. This is usually handled by the Character relieveing 1 point of Stress by laughing or groaning at a Joke that they previously would not or would have found funny, as appropriate.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Unicorn** — *The Unicorn card represents either a powerful, magical engine of transformation and freedom, that is difficult to find, obtain or master, or it is something entirely imaginary. It is a dream that is chased, sometimes the dream is achieved, often it is not, but may have led you to a better or worse place than you started. Occasionally the Unicorn is nothing but a Nightmare.*
- **Hook Event:** **Unicorn's Promise** — *The Unicorn's Promise is a complex Hook, that can express in many ways. It might be a dream revelation, or a situation that seems dire until someone suggests what sounds like a simple solution, but is actually a more difficult, or dangerous situation than the original, it can be a situation that seems impossible, but that there is some magical solution that is perhaps achievable.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Persona (Gain)** — *The Hook targets the Character's Persona specifically via their Gain, this Hook should offer a chance for them to make this Gain several times ideally.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Dream (sometimes a new dream or goal is the best Gain, other times getting what you dreamed of is better).*
- **The Fray:** **Tame the Untameable** — *This is a Fray that seems impossible. In fact the impossible task is possible, but you have to look at the problem in a whole new way. Just as the fierce Unicorn is entirely untameable, and a vicious killer, and yet if a virgin sits nearby and waits, the Unicorn will approach her, and fall asleep on her lap, allowing itself to be tamed.*
- **The Snag:** **Played** — *The Fray, whatever it was, was just a distraction while the Opposed Embodiment acted to achieve their Ends. Or the PCs discover that they have been misled and have worked towards the Opposed Embodiment's goal, which makes the Fray a ploy used by the Opposed Embodiment. It is usual at this point for the Opposed Embodiment to reveal that they were actually the feeble old man all along, or whoever.*
- **Sweeping:** **Dream / Nightmare** — *The PCs manage to sleep, but have strong powerful dreams.*
- **History Context:** **Alternate** — *The Unicorn card reveals an alternate history, that may differ dramatically to the accepted past, this maybe because the alternate includes magic, f&aelig;ry creatures, ancient astronauts, conspiracy theories, references alternate timelines, or occurs due to reality coalescence or Yarn-Teller side-stepping.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Boojum** — *The Unicorn indicates a situation has arisen that might be blamed upon a fantastic creature, but that there is a chance that the creature itself does not exist. This may be a wild goose chase or Snark Hunt, but there is always something behind the accounts or corpses, even if it is a person, pet or wild animal. Almost always a Boojum will never be clearly identified, and the situation may end with no one really sure what exactly they drove off, away, or killed.*
- **Solution:** **Magic** — *The Unicorn card indicates that the way out of this situation is through magic, it may be a particular magic artefact or spell is required, but often almost any magic will do. In genres normally without true magic, then prestidigitation, illusion and deception will have to be used instead.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **To What Degree?** — How bad will or can it get? Is everyone a traitor or just a select few? How many will die of the plague? How many swords march against you?
  - *Vector (How):* **Dream** — There is a long history of dreams being an important part of a discovery, they can reveal information that it can be difficult to obtain in other ways; it is true that dreams can present information that the mind has previously observed back to the conscious mind after it has been processed in new ways. This can be used to draw attention to something that was previously overlooked, if you throw in unconscious extra-sensory perception as well, dreams can show events that are occurring far away in real time, or can just be the usual nonsense with a hidden message. Information presented in dreams is usually symbolic in some way, rather than easily understood.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Magic** — Sometimes things are magical, they may be created by magic, have access to magic, or simply be magical in nature. The Alternative information here is about the magic that surrounds and is available to the Embodiment. Can they cast spells, have a magical sword, or are they immune to magic?
  - *Detail:* **Impressions** — Impression details can be quite varied. Some impressions like foot-prints can be obvious, letting a hunter know which way the deer or monster went, but some can be subtle, such as the minute scratches around a picked lock. Then again not all impressions are physical, there is a reason some people try very hard to make a good first impression.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Rebellion** — *Stress and its results can make a person feel like their life is spiralling out of their control, so they try to seize control again, often by an act of rebellion. This can result in all sorts of problems as parts of the Character’s life are damaged, discarded, lost, chased away, replaced, or forgotten.*
  > **Stress Rules:** Characters who are rebelling are offered a choice. They may take a new Hitch (Consuming, Vague, Doubt, Odd, Taboo or Scars) with Banes equal to the the Character’s current Stress, or they may increase another Hitch by 1 Bane for Stress equal to the current Hitch Banes, or they may discard parts of their Character such as Proficiencies, Descendants, Allies or even Annexes which will relieve Stress equal to the Cost of the lost part in Chi
- **Trauma Type:** **Nightmares** — *Traumas always make a deep psychological impact on the Character, and can effectively create damage deep into the brain, these scars express themselves during sleep with night-terrors, corrupted or disturbing dreams and reliving waking memories. Sleep itself can become problematic for the Character, as it is not restful at all, unless they can drink or drug themselves into dreamless sleep.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** Trauma creates Night-terrors, disturbing dreams and nightmares for the Character. The Trauma initially converts all Stress into Nightmare Zones, which a placed in the Character’s Psychosocial Dreamspace. Each Nightmare Zone may have between 1 and 5 Nightmare Points within it, these Nightmare Points are created by the Stress that was placed in them. [t13ne type="table" array="nightmareZones" /]
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Liberty** — *An Age of Liberty is occurring. During this age the society grants freedoms, liberties and rights to members of the society, often there is a general relaxation of rules, as police and militias are stood down, take, or are given a more liberal approach to their roles. An Age of Liberty relaxes societies mores, making society more permissive and experimental as a whole, but this does not always sompletely translate to the most conservative members of the society, who may relax a little, but not to the excesses of the more liberal. An age of Liberty has a lot in common with the ages of crime, Decadence, Chaos and Ostentation, in that it is often (although not always) followed by a collapse of some sort.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Liberty society will be pushed towards being more open minded, granting members of the society additional rights, privileges and responsibilities, this is usually achieved by more permissive leadership, less conservatism in society in general also helps, as does a free press, media, uncensored communications, art and culture, and a youth-engagement in politics, although there is always a conservative reaction within the Age of Liberty (which can result in the creation of new stories) that often focuses on some singular aspect of the age as a step too far.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Lady** — *The Lady card represents luck, freedom and liberty. Symbolic of gambling and games of chance, the lady can seem carefree and easy, but she can turn on you in an instant, especially if you disrespect her. The Lady is the card of random factors, bizarre coincidences, and insightful synchronicities, never dismiss her as dumb luck.*
- **Hook Event:** **Coincidences** — *The Hook of this Plot is about lucky breaks, coincidental leads, chance encounters or random factors aligning. The PCs are usually involved in some other business (like a high Stakes Motional Ordeal) when suddenly they run into the next Bad Guy's minions or more coincidentally the return of an old one.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Core** — *The Hook targets the Character's Core. For example: In order to Hook an Enhancer Core, create a situation that only someone with access to that Nimbed could solve.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Cash, Car, Companionship (**Gain Chi**, A Descendant and at least a Cast Extra Ally)*
- **The Fray:** **Games** — *The Fray is a game, it could be a game of chance, skill or athleticism. Games are obviously usually Low Stakes, but higher Stakes games exist. Playing Chess with Death would count as Soul Stakes (at least), a game of Russian Roulette is Extreme Stakes, and so on. Games can be teaching moments, with Mentors teaching tactics in Chess or ethics, morals or any amount of knowledge being passed via the game. Games can be Ordeals or Tests, or can be played out for real if desired.*
- **The Snag:** **Accident** — *A freak chain of events, or lack of attention, causes a Character to hurt themselves. - **Sweeping:** **Lucky Break** — *Lucky Breaks can be big Gains, Revelations or just a rest depending on how badly Pressed the Characters are. Lucky Breaks can grant all three, but there will be some chance or random factor (often a Liberty or Key Test) to make Gains or Revelations*
- **History Context:** **Luck** — *The Lady indicates that the history of the current situation is one of coincidence or blind luck. Sometimes this might mean a simple coincidence such as a character presently sharing a name with another character, other times the coincidences are extreme, where they also coincidently lived in the same apartment some time before, and even look similar, then again a situation can often be created by someone winning a lottery, or more likely an illegal poker tournament.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Fluke** — *The Lady indicates that the current situation is a fluke occurrence, this can be a chance, but relatively common happening, such as bumping into an ex while shopping, to an incredibly lucky stroke of fortune, such as winning the lottery, or surviving a terrible accident.*

- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Yours** — The information is about your own side, or own self. This might reveal your weaknesses or strengths, if you aren't already painfully aware of them.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Chance** — The Information is about a chance or probability of something, it does not represent a fact, or something that necessarily has or will occur, but rather a probability or chance that something will or has happened. Subtle examples might include finding a lemon or potato with iron nails driven through it at a crime scene, indicating someone believed they were being targeted by the evil eye. Blatant examples might include a character stepping into traffic rather than walk under a ladder, or a building not having a thirteenth floor.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Health** — *Stress, especially high stress over time, can have a very deleterious effect upon a Character’s health.   > **Stress Rules:** Healing times for all Heart Suited Wounds adds the Current Stress as a Chi Extend Duration, even a Distract Wound can take a while to shake off when you are Stressed. Additionally all Hearts Suited Wounds that the Character takes will add Current Stress to their Pips, including the Difficulty to treat and Stabilise (increasing the chances of Wounds Festering).
- **Trauma Type:** **Unlucky** — *Trauma can be the first sign that a Character’s luck has changed for the worst. Once Lady Luck has abandonned a Character she does not return her favour quickly or easily.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma relieves all current Stress. However the Stress is replaced by a number Unlucky Failure Levels equal to the total Stress Reduced. While the Character has these Unlucky Failure Levels they can Relieve Stress at any time, converting them into more Unlucky Failure Levels, as long as they add 1d6 Unlucky Failure Levels (or as many as they have left if less) to their last, current or next Action. Once they are out of Unlucky Failure Levels, this Trauma effect is over.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Chance** — *An Age of Chance is occurring. During this age unlikely coincidences and examples of extreme luck dominate the society, there is often an air of gaming, gambling, and an association with underclasses, criminality, and  this creates great unbalances and destabilising forces within a society, as the old fabric may be destroyed as some rich become poor, and some poor become rich randomly.   > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Chance all members of a society will receive Chance Success Levels and Chance Failure Levels that are randomly assigned, this number is created by Liberty and Wyrd Annexes each rolling a die roll based on Facet Values from all members of the society. For societies greater than 15,000 members assume a 6d20 rolled Score Reduced for both the Successes and Failures, this gives each member a Score between 6 and 120 or between 2 and 18 Success and Failure Levels each. These Success and Failure Levels are activated by the Referee, Yarn-Teller or Author/Player randomly throughout the Age.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Mask** — *The Mask card represents the false fronts that we play. There are few people who are completely open and honest with everyone in their life, most of us choose to hide aspects of ourselves from view and don a mask. For some, undercover agents and those running for their lives, the mask becomes their only existence.*
- **Hook Event:** **False Face** — *The Story opens with the Plot presenting a false face, it may be a pretext for something else, or a pretence of some sort, but the plot does not represent itself truthfully. It may hide its Conflict Embodiments (perhaps making the Player Characters not the Pressed Characters), make false Revelations, or false promises in general.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Core** — *The Hook targets the Character's Core. For example: In order to Hook a Traveller, create a barrier that stops them from travelling, or something that draws in Travellers like a roadside attraction of sorts.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Secret Identity (this can just be a disguise Descendant, or a whole lifestyle including Locations, or even a whole new Alternate)*
- **The Fray:** **Masquerade** — *Masquerades can be simple fancy dress parties, or they can be complex political or covert Ordeals full of under-the-table deals, or thinking bigger they can be their own Stories with sub-rosa themes, Quests to deceive, mislead and be underhanded with others, or even a Witness Protection Program or Deep Cover / Sleeper Agent situations that last decades.*
- **The Snag:** **Red Herring** — *There is some false information around, it may have already have misled the PCs, but worse yet the next Revelation they make will be deliberate misinformation by the Yarn-Teller or Plot. Perhaps they will be told the truth in the end, or might even spot the lie eventually, but this isn't a simple lie, it will be close to the truth and they will find supporting evidence.*
- **Sweeping:** **Lost-in-the-crowd** — *No matter the Fray the PCs get to vanish in the wind after it. They change or adopt disguises, head to a safe-house, or vanish into the night. They can rest, recover, shop, create or train for the next Fray.*
- **History Context:** **Facade** — *The Mask indicates that the past of this situation has been changed by time and rumour, or altered deliberately, aspects of the true history have been rewritten, with cover stories being created. This may have been to protect some identities (or alliances), preserve some societal myth, or simply be by the mists of time.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Disguised** — *The Mask indicates that the current situation is based around disguises, masks and perceptions. Perhaps an enemy is disguised among you, a spy or a mole in your organisation, or more often you must don a disguise in order pass undiscovered through enemy territory or spy upon them. Disguises can involve playing a role, pretending to be something you are not, or just not being recognised for who you really are.*
- **Solution:** **Bluff** — *The Mask indicates that the solution to your current situation is to bluff it out, this may be a simple lie, or a complex deception, reliant on many players, trickery and shenanigans.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Mystery** — The information is about some mystery or unknown thing. This might be a secret hidden down the ages, or just who took the last doughnut.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **With Whom?** — You can find out a lot about a person from the company they keep. And it always helps to know how many are in the gang you are hunting.
  - *Vector (How):* **Obvious Lie** — Sometimes information includes something so preposterous that it is an obvious lie. Sometimes the obvious lie can hide a deeper truth, this can happen often when someone reveals they know nothing about a subject they have claimed knowledge of by asking a question they should obviously know the answer too. Worse yet is when the obvious lie is actually the truth, but made to seem so preposterous that it is dismissed out of hand.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Guises** — The information is about how a character or descendant likes to be perceived. Sometimes this can be an alternate name, an alias, codename, pen name, or secret identity that they use. For example, traditionally Robin Hood is known to have employed a disguise as a peasant farmer at tournament.
  - *Detail:* **Disguised** — Disguised details are those cleverly arranged details that misdirect and misinform the unwary observer, this can be as simple as making handwriting unrecognisable by writing with their off-hand, or as complex as arranging the sniper shot to exactly coincide with the cannon-fire in the 1812 Overture.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Delirium** — *Stress can have profound effects on the mind, causing one to see and hear all sorts of things that are not happening. Simply missing too much sleep can cause a healthy mind to micro-sleep and confuse the Character’s real and dream lives, and for some minds the confusion is more easily started, unusual brain chemistry and even normal development can lead to all manner of hallucinatory voices and imaginary friends knocking about the place.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character’s current Stress and the current Tension Level are added together to create an amount of Chi. This Chi must be paid by the Character to side-step the Character partially into a Psycho-social "dream-space" or "metaphorical representation" that they will explore, encountering aspects of their own Character, and perhaps their mental image of close friends as metaphors and hallucinatory Extras. The Delirium continues for a period of time equal to the Current Stress as Intrepid Facet Sway after which the delirium will fade away removing Stress equal to the current Tension Level. Typically the Delirium will reappear anytime the Character’s current Stress plus the Tension Level is high enough to side-step them that far again. Deliriums are typically an extension of the Character, even if they do appear as faery forests, cloud castles, creepy dungeons or alien vistas, so trying to harm them, or the character’s within with magic, for example, will usually harm the Character experiencing the delirium.
- **Trauma Type:** **Masking** — *Trauma can alter Characters dramatically, but it can also cause Characters to pretend it has affected them differently than it actually has. Some people enjoy the attention that comes with trauma, and will exaggerate the effects for more attention. For others, pretending to be someone without the trauma is the game, if they pretend hard enough perhaps the trauma will go away. In some other cases, the Character may assume an unrelated symptom is part of the same thing, which can become incorporated into their diagnosis.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Character decides to mask the trauma in some way. Typically the mask will pretend the effects of the trauma were different to the actual effects in some way, often either more or less profound than they actually were. Characters can create an Alt that will act as the "mask" Character, or they can create a "Masking Behaviour" Annex or Descendant to help them hide what is happening (this will relieve Stress to Chi to purchase the Alt, Descendant or Annex).
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Pretence** — *An Age of Pretence is occurring. During this age the society values lying and pretence more than actual accomplishments. During this age the leaders of the society will also be liars and deceivers, and the society will usually be misled by them, for their own personal goals. The society will treat gossip, stories and anecdotes with equal validity often without checking for evidence.*
  > **Age Rules:** During the Age of Pretence clearly showing disbelief will be viewed as rude, insulting and have a negative impression within the society. Appearing to believe something that is probably false will be viewed positively within this society at this time.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Cloak** — *The Cloak card is symbolic of the warmth and protection of a good coat, privileges of rank, and the hidden aspects of life. This can be those parts that are private, or actual secrets, perhaps even hidden passages, or secret tunnels. It is the card of secrets, thieves and cowards, as well as nobility, cunning, and superheroes.*
- **Hook Event:** **Cloaked Threat** — *The Hook tells the Character directly of some looming, but secretive danger, rumours of a new mob boss, prototype fighter with incredible stats, or any mysterious stranger or player.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitch** — *The Hook targets one of the Character's Hitches, usually a Flaw. For example: In order to Hook a Duty (Honour-bound) Hitch simply threaten the Character's honour, or have them swear to do something.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Secret Alliance (the creation of a secretive Pact Descendant)*
- **The Fray:** **Hide!** — *These Ordeals may involve hiding, or concealing something. The PCs may have to hide themselves from detection, or they may have to conceal something else, perhaps not letting the guards take the kid who just burst into the room and hid under the sofa. As such Hiding can be as much about bluffing as it is physical concealment, but is an Opposed Ordeal of almost any Stakes.*
- **The Snag:** **No Witnesses** — *Whatever happened during the Fray, there is no one to bear witness to the result. This means the truth of what happened will be clouded by speculation and suspicions, mostly directed at the PCs. Alternatively, the Fray is complicated by the PCs having to insure they are not spotted, and that their involvement remains secret.*
- **Sweeping:** **Magic Cloak** — *The PCs may gain an actual cloak of invisibility, or more likely they gain a Revelation that they could not have got any other way, perhaps overhearing or intercepting a communication from the Opposed Embodiment.*
- **History Context:** **Shrouded** — *The Cloak indicates that the history of this situation is shrouded, this may mean that the truth cannot be known, or that the truth is known, but not widely shared. Unlike the Mask there is no cover story or fiction, just lingering mysteries and hidden facts.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Hidden** — *The Cloak indicates that the current situation involves hiding or being hidden. This may involve an enemy force hidden with-in the lord's territory, or having to hide yourselves or some contraband. Occasionally the Cloak will indicate a hidden force, advantage, or strength that is being overlooked.*
- **Solution:** **Skulk** — *The Cloak indicates that the solution to the current situation is most often to hide, and remain unseen, while observing yourself. This may reveal information that will be useful, allow you to ambush your enemies, or simply flee the area undetected.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Secret** — The Information is about some secret. This might be a secret group, door, passageway or perhaps information like a password.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **Obscure** — Obscure information is information that is difficult to find, sometimes this is because someone would prefer the information was not available at all, other times it is because the information is genuinely difficult to find out. Examples might include, finding out the real name of the Dark Lord, or the exact frequencies that will negate that battleship's Electronic Counter Measures.
  - *Vector (How):* **Clothing** — Clothing can convey a lot of information about a person or culture, from the level of trade and wealth they have to specific details of kinship, or favourite TV show.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Comfort** — The information is about the comforts that the Embodiment enjoys, these are usually specific, rather than generic comforts, perhaps that character is known to sleep only in an oxygen tent, on duckling eiderdown, or with a teddy bear, a Monster may take comfort in eating only those people who are overweight, or those who are exceptionally healthy, or bathes daily in a specific spring, pool or grotto, or perhaps a Descendant must be cleaned only with neat grain alcohol, must kept in a secure but silk-lined, rose-oak case, or must be polished with only wax from a particular region's bees.
  - *Detail:* **Hidden** — A hidden detail is placed deliberately and always carries a communication of some sort, whether it is a hidden maker's mark, the references to other movies in a scene's set dressing, an invisible ink, an easily overlooked microdot, or an engineered quantum signature. These are the details that may be obvious if you are looking for them, but are unnoticeable if you are unaware.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Monsters** — *Stress can make monsters of us all, but worse still Stress can make Monsters, not just mountains, out of molehills.*
  > **Stress Rules:** The Character must pay Chi equal to the Character’s current Stress as Yarn. This Chi is used to purchase a new Character that can be a Force-of-Nature Extra with Monster Facets (Enigma, Heresy, Jeer, Liberty, Phoenix, and Nature). These "Monsters" may actually be physical issues such as natural disasters, or they may be aspects of the Characters own Psyche, given hallucinatory or supernatural form. Then again they can be the Character themselves, unaware in their Stress induced black-outs what they are doing. Other times the Character will project their problems on an actual Monster, tying their Arcs together. The only catharsis available to a Character here is to defeat, or at least survive the Monster, which will relieve Stress equal to the Monster Facet Boon.
- **Trauma Type:** **Cloaking** — *Trauma can sometimes hide other problems. For example, during an accident a Character may snap a limb obviously, they are only concerned about the blinding pain coming from the broken limb. Only once the limb is set does the Character (or the medics treating them) become aware of the muscle damage in their back, or the concussion.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma hides a Wound so that it cannot be seen or treated (effectively filling the same Wound Slot with another Wound). The Cloaking Wound is created by relieving Stress, with the Stress relieved equalling the Pips of the Wound, a Limit Card is usually Drawn and Played, that adds Suit and Wound Limit for the Pips. Once this Cloaking Wound has been treated and healed away, the Cloaked Wound will reappear in that Wound Slot. Both the Cloaking Wound and Cloaked Wound occupy the same Wound Slot at once, but the Cloaked Wounds effects and damage are hidden until healed. While a Character has a Cloaking Wound, they may relieve Stress at any time by adding the Pips to their Cloaking Wound, making it potentially Fester, Re-Open or even get worsse as they must Draw a new Limit Card that will replace the old Limit if Higher.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Secrets** — *An Age of Secrets is occurring. During this age a society becomes secretive and self-protecting. A society in an age of secrets may make the society into a secret society, with membership never revealed beyond the society, but this is not guarranteed. Secrecy may protect the society from some external threat, or it may be intended to keep some secret, such as a source, technique or technology to maintain a monopoly. Historically the greatest example is probably the silk trade, which caused the nation of China to maintain an age of Secrets, protecting knowledge of the silk worm for around a millenium.*
  > **Age Rules:** Certain information within or about the society is protected by a veil of secrecy, how far the society will go to keep those secrets will vary with the secret and society, but most will consider killing more acceptable than dying to preserve a secret.

### 📖 Yarn & Story-Weaving
- **Theme/Yarn:** **The Physician** — *The Physician card is representative of medicine, healing and bad news. If a character is injured, or sick they can seek relief and respite through the Physician. If they aren't then the bad news could be simply that the Apple-a-day isn't actually medical insurance, but more likely there is a new Hitch on some character's horizons.*
- **Hook Event:** **Bad News** — *The Hook just consists of terrible bad news, often medical in nature, such as an incurable (or at least perilous) disease diagnosis.*
- **Hook Aspect:** **Hitch** — *The Hook targets one of the Character's Hitches. For example: In order to Hook a Arachnophobe Character, one should simply have to threaten them with a spider, or better yet, make the bad guy into a spider and have word of them reach the PC.*
- **Gain Reward:** *Savant — Powerful Ally (Demon/Solo/Fae/Grand-Mercari)*
- **The Fray:** **Medical Situation** — *This is a medical situation, maybe the PCs need Surgery (or to conduct it), perhaps they need to deal with a large scale accident, disaster, or plague.*
- **The Snag:** **Medical Ethics** — *The Fray is complicated by a question of medical ethics, there are four main forms, Autonomy (the right to refuse treatment), Beneficence (acting in the best interest of the patient), Non-maleficence (Do no harm), and Justice (insuring the most worthy get the necessary help). This may be as simple as rich client verses poor client or as complex as the cure being as bad as the disease.*
- **Sweeping:** **Medical Attention** — *The PCs get all the healing they can take, often plus some recovery time. This sort of intense medical attention can usually reduce, remove or resolve Quirk and even Flaw Hitches, it might reduce a Woe, but can rarely remove it.*
- **History Context:** **Disease** — *The Physician indicates that the current situation has been caused by a disease, illness, plague or similar medical situation in the past. Perhaps the king has had a stroke, and is now bed-ridden, or the kingdom may have been decimated by a plague that has altered the balance of economic power in the country.*
- **Situation/Plight:** **Aid** — *The Physician indicates a situation where another requires aid, or intelligent assistance. This may be healing, diagnosis, treatment, care, nursing, mothering, or even tactical military support, but without this aid there will be probably be injury and death.*
- **Solution:** **Prescription** — *The Physician indicates that the solution to this problem is fulfil a prescription, this might be by purchasing a medicine that a doctor has decided would be right for you, or by following a complex written plan or prophecy that was written before the situation arose, that describes the way out of the situation.*
- **Revelations Aspects:**
  - *About (Fact):* **Help** — The information is about a Character can help the Character, it might be someone who can patch your wounds, or fix your car. They may not be an Ally exactly, but they aren't an enemy either.
  - *Info (Embodiment):* **How?** — The information revealed answers the how question. How are the enemy going to infiltrate the kingdom? How did the monster get in the house? How does one conduct open heart surgery? The answers can be exceptionally detailed or just the broad strokes though, and will usually tell you nothing about the reasons behind anything or even who is doing it. You may know the enemy is among the party guests, but not who they are. The smashed down door tells you how the monster got in, but nothing about what it looks like, or whether it left again.
  - *Vector (How):* **Blood** — Blood is usually a good indicator something bad has happened, but at times it can convey a lot more information to either a mage, a cleric, detective, physician, or a forensic biologist.
  - *Alternate (Special):* **Health** — The information is about the health of the Embodiment. This can be as simple as a splatter of blood revealing that someone (or something) was injured, a terrible cough, sweats or fever indicating some one is ill, or as complex as noticing that the locals all have mud-streaked but perfect, blemish-free skin, apart from the lord who is bathed and acne-ridden.
  - *Detail:* **Cunning** — Cunning details are the careful touches of a design, secret compartments, doors, rooms and elevators, and of course the tales of actual cunning that people tell about a Character.

### 🧠 Stress, Trauma & Lifespan
- **Stress Type:** **Dangerous** — *Stress is dangerous, it can turn a simple mistake into a deadly danger, make a simple shock cause a heart attack, or make a Character hold a loaded gun to their own head.*
  > **Stress Rules:** All Hearts (<span class="t13ne-redcard">♥</span>) Suited Ordeal cards that target the Character for an Attack add the Character’s current Stress as Yarn so 13 Stress adds 66 Pips to each card, but then relieves Stress equal to the original Card Pips, plus all Strain and current Shock (although this new attack is a whole other ball game). Unsuited cards add current Stress as Chi (13 Pips), but remove only the current Stress Reduced (4 Stress), 1 Strain or 1 Shock.
- **Trauma Type:** **Medicinal** — *Trauma can sometimes be medicine, surgery is carefully engineered trauma designed to lessen the damage of other Wounds, but such things can happen randomly. Sometimes a fresh wound will cure, or reveal to medics, a lingering problem allowing treatment to actually cure the underlying problem. It is worth remembering though that despite the narrative trope, a blow to the head will most likely cause a concussion, not cure amnesia.*
  > **Trauma Rules:** The Trauma can affect a Hitch or lingering Wound, using Stress as Chi to directly heal Wounds, and reduce the Banes of Hitches.
- **Age/Lifespan Aspect:** **Recovery** — *An Age of Recovery is occurring. During this age a society heals and recovers from the wounds of the past, rifts will be mended, and the society will resettle into a new harmony. There may be a few scars and other signs of damage left behind, but most members have put the bad times behind them, whatever they were. During this age economic and cultural recovery will also occur, which may advance the society into a new age.*
  > **Age Rules:** During an Age of Recovery a society will recover from whatever harm has previously befallen them. Typically this will mean a period of steady growth in membership, economic, artistic, technological and cultural successes are also possible to recover something previously lost.
