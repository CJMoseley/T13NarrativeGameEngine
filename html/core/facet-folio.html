Facet Folio

T13 like most RPGs abstracts real-world characteristics into something that we model in the game. In the most popular roleplaying games you find six Stats that govern the Character’s Strength, Dexterity, Stamina, Intelligence, Wisdom and Charisma, but then add different mechanisms to determine how lawful, chaotic, good or evil a Character may be. In T13, everything about a Character can be extracted into the Character "Stats", but to do this we require more than six Stats.

T13 uses 24 "Stats" (although they are bound together to make opposed Pairs) that are known as Facets (as in the facets of the character) each Facet has a recognised Anti-Facet that opposes it, and for most Characters their rating in a Facet negatively impacts their rating in the Anti-Facet, automatically providing balance for high powered Stats. A Character with a 16 Boon in Awe is limited to a 10 Boon in Jeer, so they are a little more likely to intimidate or impress someone, than make them laugh.

Optional Rule: In some games, in order to make them more unique, or support a particular setting better, some Facets may have different Anti-Facets, for example, the Author may have decided that in their game, which features the spider-trickester god Anansi, that Gossamer is the opposite of Orthodox and Heresy the opposite of Burden. In such a setting, physical strength and knowledge of truth are opposed, one either goes to the library or the gym, and a Character is either comfortably well-off, or a liar or fantasist. Such changes can have profound effects on how the game plays, and how the world of the game feels during play. Even more extreme changes can be made, where a Facet changes from Yin to Yang, for example if Wyrd became a Yang Facet opposed by Yin Jeer, the the Yang Liberty may be opposed by a Yin Awe. This would create a world where Chaos and Law are not opposed, but the laws of mgaic and man are allied with dreams against the character's inner emotional turmoils.
