Shocks and Shocked Dice

When Stress has reached its limit, when a Character is Overstressed, has Strained their Dice as far as they go, then we enter the realm of Shock. Shocks are the most powerful form of Stress, and are generally dealt with on a point for point basis. A point of Shock is in many ways indistinguishable from a single Twist, and can be considered identical for cancelling purposes.

Shock

When a Die has more Strains than the Stress-Strain Limit then each point of Strain over the Limit is counted as a point of Shock. When a Die has more Stress than its Stress Limit then each point of Stress over that maximum is counted as a point of Shock.

Shock is the most powerful form of Stress, and can cause a Character to lose their ability to act consciously. Shocked Extras are usually considered defeated and unable to continue a fight for example. Shocked Grunts and Heroes are extremely limited in what they can do to recover, being unable to pay Sway or Chi until the Shocks are Relieved. Even Shocked Yarn-Tellers cannot Narrate or spend Yarn, until the Shocks are Relieved.

Shocked Dice

When a Die has any number of Shocks it is called a Shocked Die. A Shocked Die cannot store more Strains, or Stress, until it has lost all its current Shocks. Characters and Descendants that have Shocks cannot perform any Action except rolling their Shocked Dice.

Shocked Dice cannot generate a normal Score to perform Actions, and Characters and Descendants are often effectively locked in Global Reaction Time until their Shocks are Relieved. Shocks are typically Relieved by the Character spending time in GRT, taking a Distress or Trauma effect, or occasionally adding Drama Dice to the Drama Pool.

Relieving Shocks

Shocks are difficult and expensive to get rid of without rolling Shocked Dice, but it is possible to negate them with Chi. Look up the total number of Shocks as though they were Twists and pay the Chi equivalent (the Super-Value to the Boon). This can get very expensive, negating 5 Shocks simultaneously will cost 92 Chi, and as such may only be open to Yarn-Tellers and the most powerful of Heroes. Shocks are usually negated by being rolled, however there is one other way Shock can be Relieved.

Shock can be converted into a period of unconsciousness, this is the preferred method for NPCs, where you rarely want to use Stress cards or Traumas on anything less than a Cast Extra, instead the Character can just lose consciousness. The amount of time spent unconscious is equal to the Extend Duration based on the number of Shocks as Twists. Shock can also be mitigated somewhat by discarding their Style Reserve, although with only Wildcards as Trumps, however the total Chi cost of the current Shocks must be matched to Relieve one Shock. This can be combined with unconsciousnness as follows: if a Character becomes Shocked (3 Shock) during an Ordeal they may elect to fall Unconscious rather than rolling their Shock Dice. This would place them in a Coma for 13 Weeks normally, however they elect to Discard Style Reserve cards, after they have discarded 32 Pips they Relieve 1 Shock, that leaves 2 Shocks which is a Weekend Unconsious, another 16 Pips will reduce that to a Short Scene Unconscious. A final 5 discarded Pips will allow them to awaken groggily in their next Phase.
