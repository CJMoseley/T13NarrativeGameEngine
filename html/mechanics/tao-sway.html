Tao Sway

The least powerful form of Sway is divided in to Yin and Yang, which together we call Tao Sway. Because it is the least powerful it is the most easily generated, often by Facet or Annex Dice Rolls. Although the I-Ching and Geometry can also grant a Character Yin or Yang as well.

Any generated Score is the equivalent of either Yin or Yang, depending upon the action being attempted. So if you add Tao Yin Sway to a Yin Facet action each point of Yin will add +1 to the rolled Score of one of the dice.

For example, if Josephine Bloggs tries to Search a crime scene for clues, she could roll her Key Facet Dice (4d3-3) which will average at 5, she should also add Proficiency Dice ("Senses", "Effort", "Activities" —  gives 2d6 averaging 7) to this roll (or may be a second "die" in the Pool), regardless she rolls 4 and 9. This generates either 9 Yang Sway or 13 Yang Sway depending on the dice rules being used.

The Sway is used to search the room for clues against a Difficulty (determined by the dice system chosen and the Yarn-Teller). The Yarn-Teller may have no clues planned for the room and set the Difficulty to 45 as no clue is present at all and Key has no real chance of magically producing one. More likely the clue is noted (although that may just be as "a clue") and is something Ambiguous or that Josephine is expecting to find, then Difficulty of 6 (or at a push 8) is likely. If a Yarn-Teller has a more detailed clue then perhaps it is partially obscured (Diff 8 or 12) or has been hidden by the perpetrator (in which case a Difficulty should be rolled for the hiding/cleaning up) or if accidentally concealed (Diff 10 or 16). If a clue is inconspicuous like forensic evidence so often is, then that is harder still (Diff 12 or 21 and more likely 21 unless the perpetrator has long red hair that is easily spotted without equipment), if the clue she needs is locked away she will need (Diff 14 or more likely 26 as that is very much magic or super-technology territory). The Yarn-Teller decides that looking for clues is Intrepid for Key and the clue is only obscured, setting the Difficulty to 8.

If using Normal Dice Pools that would be One Success and One Failure at the Diff of 8 - that is a pass with a failure level. If the rolls are added to generate a Score then that’s a 13 which is squarely in the Moderate Success area, even if the search were for some reason low-powered. Josephine could spend some of her Yang to increase the Score, each Tao Yang would add +1 to her roll.

Key Sway (Insight) if she had any and Bold optional rules were active could add +2 per point (and would halve the Difficulties). In a Pool roll she could pay 4 and have 2 Successes, in Addition mode she would have to add 3 to have a Complete Success. That is assuming she had no Key Facet Sway available.

Descendants and Extras can only store as much Tao Yin and Yang as their Master Annex’s maximum dice roll. See Trophy Descendants.

It is also possible to roll to generate Tao Sway, in which case the appropriate Facet will generate Sway as a Score. The Difficulty of this roll is usually set at 2 (unconscious Test), but circumstances can set it to much higher Difficulties (being secretive so that no one could sense it would add 16 or 32 depending on the power level) .

In general, the amount of Tao Sway that can be stored by a Character is determined by their Facets.

Each Boon of a Yang Facet can store 1 point of Yang and vice versa for Yin. These Boons are affected by the Character’s Scale so more powerful Characters can usually store more Sway. However, it should be noted that once stored in this way Tao Sway becomes Facet Sway, which may behave differently to Tao Sway in some circumstances.

Tao Sway can also be stored in Descendants

Each Boon of a Yang Facet can store 1 point of Yang and vice versa for Yin. These Boons are affected by the Character’s Scale so more powerful Characters can usually store more Sway.

Sway Table

The Sway Table is where this information comes together and you can look up how much Tao Sway / rolled Score is required to do something. This allows you to calculate Difficulties. Generally, if you are working with Tao Sway it will usually be considered Banal at whatever it is trying to accomplish, but this may be modified by the Yarn-Teller or Referee as required for the genre and setting. Facet rolled Scores are even more likely to be modified, with the genre and setting wildly affecting whether a Difficulty should be calculated from Banal, Intrepid, Bold or Monstrous Sway effects.
