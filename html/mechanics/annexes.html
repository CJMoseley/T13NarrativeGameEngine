Annexes

Annexes in T13 are constructed by combining Proficiencies together, first two Proficiencies are combined to create a Skill, then as more Proficiencies are added the Skill becomes a Talent, or even a Power. Annexes can be thought of like a musical chord, made up of individual notes (or Proficiencies), but that all work together through harmony and dissonance to create a unique and more powerful and complex sound.

Proficiencies are like a unit of knowledge and experience. Imagine an early human, who has a Burden Proficiency in "Earth" and a Quiet Proficiency in "Plants" because they are both things they have knowledge of. By combining this knowledge of Earth and Plants they create a new Quiet-Burden Annex that is called "Herbal Supplies", which will now let them roll a bigger die than either their Burden or Quiet Facet Dice, when they are trying to find or identify herbs and plants.

Crisis Annexes

Crisis Annexes are created during play, when a Character rolls a Proficiency Crisis (or plays a Crisis card) they may fuse the rolled Proficiencies together and create a Skill or Talent (depending on how many were rolled).

For example, if a Character is trying to pick a lock, and has Proficiencies in "Locks" (a Key Prof) and "Tools" (a Craft Prof) they would roll 1d6 as a Proficiency die. If this die rolls a 1 or a 6 that creates a Crisis, "Locks" and "Tools" can be combined to create "Lock-picking" (C-K) or "Locksmithing" (K-C). If they also had a "Silent" (Quiet Prof) that they also used, they would roll 2d6, a crisis would only occur on a 2 or a 12 but could create either Skill as a Talent with a Quiet Umbral, or perhaps a new Talent, such as "Sensory-Trance" (QCK), "Flow-State" (QKC) "Woodcraft" (QKC) or "Writing"(CKQ).

Normally the created Annex should be useful to the action that was being attempted at the time. In the case above, that could mean the Lock-picking or Locksmithing are the most probable, but Sensory-Trance is a possibility if they focused on listening to the tumblers, or Flow-state as they were just in the flow... but the Woodcraft and Writing annexeswould not be. It is also best if the Channelling Facet of the Annex reflects the Facet Test that was called for, this prioritizes Lock-picking (or Sensory-Trance) over Locksmithing or Flow-State as the created Annex.

A kind Referee might allow the new Annex to be rolled and replace the Facet from the original roll, or even add it to the Pool as an additional die, but this should perhaps be reserved for the re-attempt after a Stalemate.

Anatomy of an Annex

Annexes are, like Hitches, a type of what we call a Knot in T13. Knots are built from the smaller components, Threads. For most Character Annexes, the most important of the Threads are Proficiencies, and it is these Proficiencies become tied together forming an Annex.

Annexes always have a Root Proficiency and a Channel Proficiency. The Facets of these Proficiencies govern what the Annex can do and achieve.

Skills consist of just the Root and Channel (with one of them theoretically acting as a Tangle), but other Annex types add Umbral and Nimbed Proficiencies to the Annex. Which can affect what the Annex can do and how big the Boon of the Annex is.

Talents have to have at least 3 Proficiencies working together. The Root, the Channel and at least one Umbral (and one of these will be doing double duty as a Tangle (often the Root). Larger Talents held by more powerful Characters can add Nimbeds too. Very powerful Characters (with lots of Hitches) will have Talents almost as powerful as otehr Character's Powers.

Conversely weak Powers can also have as few as 3 Proficiencies, in the case of Characters with only one Hitch, but most start around 5 Proficiencies.

Super-Annexes are much more powerful than normal Annexes, with higher rolls and more Nimbeds. In fact, in a Super-Annex the Root and Channel add 3 Nimbeds each automatically.

Tangle

The Tangle of the Annex is a complication that adds a cost, limit, or restriction to the use of the Annex. Each Tangle interacts with the Umbral and Nimbed Proficiencies in a different way, but Tangles do not effect the Boon of the Annex directly. Every Annex has a Tangle which is most commonly also the Root of the Annex.

The Tangle can be any Proficiency already in the Annex, whether the Root, the Channel, an Umbral or even a Nimbed, and can be changed whenever the Annex is redefined. While technically a Skill has to have a Tangle, it will have no effect until the Annex has at least one Umbral or Nimbed Proficiency, which means you can redefine the Tangle when you make a Skill into a Talent, or Talent into a Power, anyway.

Umbrals

Any Umbral Proficiencies will also add their value to the Annex Value. So a Quiet of 16 could add another 83 value. That’s Value 232 which is Boon 29! However, Umbrals always add an additional cost or penalty to using the Annex, which varies by Facet and which type of Umbral you choose. Umbrals also interact with the Tangle of the Annex, as noted above, to add additional restrictions, limits and costs.

Umbrals have various costs based on the Umbral Boon of the Facet. The Umbral Boon is the Facet Boon ignoring any Scale modifiers or Monster Facet multipliers. A Character might be Scale 5 with an Umbral Boon of 13, this Umbral would count as Boon 13 for all Umbral costs, but would add not 66 Value but 111 Value (the Value of Boon 18).

Nimbeds

Nimbeds are Proficiencies added to an Annex like Umbrals, but rather than adding their Value to the Annex increasing its Value the Nimbed adds some trick or extra ability to the Annex. The Nimbed abilities always expand what you can achieve with an Annex, and can never act as any sort of limit. Nimbeds are completely positive in nature, but that means they must be limited somehow, otherwise everyone would just add every Nimbed to everything. There are two limits therefore placed on Nimbeds, the first being the limit of how many Proficiencies can be placed in a Talent or Power, the second limit is based on the number of Umbrals the Annex has and varies as follows by Game Type.

  • Banal — Each Umbral allows 1 Nimbed in the Annex. 3 Umbrals could support 3 Nimbeds.
  • Intrepid — Each Umbral allows 2 Nimbeds. 3 Umbrals could support 6 Nimbeds.
  • Bold — The Total number of Umbrals can support a number of Nimbeds equal to the Value of that Boon. 3 Umbrals could support 8 Nimbeds.

Nimbeds do not add to the Value of the Annex, but do add some trick or special ability to the Annex. If you have multiple Nimbeds of the same Facet then the Nimbeds add an Edge or a Glow

Two Nimbeds of a Facet generally add an Edge.

Three or more Nimbeds of the same Facet add a Glow.

Four or more Nimbeds of the same Facet adds an Automatic Success Level of the appropriate Facet.

Calculating Boons

Annex Boons are generally calculated by adding Facet Values together for the Root and Channel Facet for a Skill. It is normal, if you don’t know your own Facets (perhaps you are using an Extra), to use the Stats of the Plot, or Game, or you may assume a Boon of 13 (value 66) that is modified by your current Scale.

If a Character has a Craft of 13 (66) and a Key of 16 (83) then that gives Lock-picking a Value of 149 which gives a Boon of 22. If that Character is raised to Scale 1, then their Craft becomes effective Boon 14 (74) with a Key of effective Boon 17 (92); that creates a lock-picking Value of 166 or Boon 24, raising it by 2 Boons. However, adding another Scale raises that Boon to only 25.

Optional Rule — Monster Facets work differently, as Monster Facets represent a blending of Plot Twists and Character Boons. Monster Facets multiply the Value of the Monster Facet Boon by 1 + the number of Monster Facets. This means a Maker Monster would have their Monster Craft go from Boon 13 and 66 Value to a Value of 132 and an effective Boon of 21. A Lock-picking Skill built with this Monster Craft would have a Boon of 27. However, each Monster Facet increases that multiplier, so a Maker-Hunter Monster would have a Monster Craft of (66×3 = 198) or effectively Boon 26 and a Monster Key Value of 249 (Boon 30) and would pick those locks with a Boon 42 Annex.

Super-Annexes work differently, directly adding the Facet Boons, so the Lock-picking Super-Skill would have a Boon of 29 (see Super-Annexes)

Talents

When you are rolling Proficiencies in an Annex roll (perhaps after creating that Lock-picking Skill) if another Proficiency Crisis occurs, you may add one of the Crisis Proficiencies to the Annex, or create a new one as you prefer. This can also occur if one of the Proficiencies that you are using happens to be in an Annex already. It is normal to take this opportunity to spend some Sway to buy a new Proficiency for the Annex. E.g. if you use the Key Proficiency "Lock" from Lock-picking when trying to build a lock, and the "Tool" Craft proficiency too, and have a Crisis then you can create Locksmithing. This doubles up your "Lock" and "Tool" proficiencies, so for a small price you could change "Tool" for "Smithing" in the new Annex, or "Lockpicks" in Lock-picking, "Locks" could be changed to "Skeleton-keys", "Clasps", "Locking" or "Lock-smithing" or something else entirely as required and agreed with the Referee.

Talents are limited in how many Umbrals and Nimbeds they may have based on one of the following rules:

  • Talents may have One Umbral and One Nimbed (this is simplest and works well for most Grunts and early-game Heroes).
  • Talents may have One Umbral and the appropriate Number of Nimeds this unlocks (1-2). Works well for most Grunts and Heroes.
  • Talents may have additional Proficiencies (Umbrals and Nimbeds) equal to the number of Proficiencies or Hitches in the Master Annex Reduced (Score of the Boon). So a Descendant with 5 Proficiencies in the Master Annex may have 2 additional Proficiencies, where as a Character with 3 Hitches can only have 1 additional Proficiency. This is more complex, but scales well to make Yarn-Tellers Talents like Grunt Powers. It should be noted that Resolved Hitches (in the form of additional Personas, Cores, etc) count as Hitches for this purpose.

Powers

Any Talent that gains more than these (depending upon which limit you use) must become a Power instead. Powers are essentially just bigger Talents with more Proficiencies in them. Even Powers have a limit though, which can be decided in the following ways:

  • Hard Limit: No more than 4-24 Additional Proficiencies are possible - this hard limit will vary by game, genre, era or universe as the Referee and Yarn-teller require.
  • Variable Limit: Powers may have no more additional Proficiencies than the Character or Descendants Master Annex has Proficiencies or Hitches. So a Character with 2 Hitches can only have 2 additional Proficiencies in their Powers.

Super-Annexes

Additionally, Yarn-Teller characters can have Super-Annexes, which might be a Super-Skill, Super-Talent or Super-Power, depending how they assign the Super-Annex.

Each Proficiency in a Super-Annex adds the Nimbed, Edge and Glow of its Facet, and Umbrals and Tangles still add Value to the Annex. Super-Annex Root and Channel add their Boons together directly to find their combined Value. E.g. Super-Lock-picking would add Craft 13 and Key 16 to give Boon 29 and then add additional Value from any Umbrals (such as Quiet mentioned above) on top of a Value of 244, so the Super-Talent would have a Value of 244+83=327 that’s Boon 35. It would also have all the Nimbeds of Craft, Key and the Umbral Quiet would also add Quiet Nimbeds

Normally a Yarn-Teller may have 1 Super-Annex (although there are some ways to get more).

Purchasing Annexes

Let’s face it, sometimes you need a new Skill, but don’t want to have to wait until a Crisis comes about in play, perhaps you are creating an experienced Character, or adding a new Alternate to a Character, and it doesn’t make sense to make them all start as Fresh Grunts, or then again maybe you have free slots and can get some training from a Guild or Mentor. There’s lots of reasons you might want to purchase an Annex.

Annexes have a fairly easy to calculate Chi Cost:

The Base Cost + The Boon of the Annex + The Cost of every Proficiency + The Nimbed Boons - The Umbral Boons

Interactive Component: chicosttable

This means that Lock-picking Skill discussed above could be purchased for a total of : 2+22+2= 26 Chi. These Annex costs are also used to calculate the costs of Descendants, and Characters that have them (although usually Reduced if not a Master Annex).
