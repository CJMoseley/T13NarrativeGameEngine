# Wormhole Racers: Concept and Core Loop

## Concept
Wormhole Racers is a racing game where the race course is a three-dimensional vortex that responds to the Racing vehicles inside of it.

The Raceway is a tube with an irregular geometry designed to mimic the inside of a vortex. The tube forms itself between two points in three-dimensional space and will shake, writhe, and twirl during the race, with the individual racers affecting the vortex in different ways. Ripples on the walls (floor and ceiling) of the vortex will impede and accelerate the racers and can provide ramps and surfaces for jumps and leaps. Gravity within the vortex operates outwards away from the centre, making flying exceptionally difficult; instead, ships roll and ride on energy shields that can be rotated and deformed to allow the ship to steer, slide, and jump.

### The Universe
The game is based in a Universe where faster-than-light travel across the Galaxy is only possible through Wormholes—Hyperspatial structures generated by Subspace Vortex Generators. Different Vortex Generators have different harmonic frequencies, and multiple generators from multiple ships is both necessary to race the ships but creates chaotic and turbulent vortices as every tuning interacts.

The Galaxy will be procedurally generated with a number of species. These are presented as 2D animations rather than 3D, allowing individual portraits to be constructed from sprite sheets.

### The Crew
The game is based on a ship with multiple player character roles (ideally multiplayer, or single player with NPCs):
*   **Pilot:** Pilots the race parts.
*   **Navigator:** Sets destinations, decides cargo (often the pilot in single-player).
*   **Engineer:** Fits upgrades, tunes ship wiring (creating subharmonics), fixes systems during races, and retunes engine components on the fly.
*   **Weapons Operators:** Gunners (weapons fire impacts the vortex).

## Core Game Loop
1.  **Race:** Race other ships along a Vortex to a new system. Leagues unlock by completing "Stories" (tutorials/skill gates).
2.  **Interact:** Arrive in a system and interact in space docks and POIs (League Offices, Scrapyards, Shops, Bars, etc.). Different plots seed different options (e.g., Criminal stories add smuggling/illegal races).
3.  **Upgrade/Repair:** Perform jobs or trade to gather upgrades/repairs. The engineer modifies the ship here.
4.  **Orbital Leagues:** Participate in local orbital races (circular races distorted by planetary bodies). If no race is scheduled, players must travel to another system, potentially hitching a ride or sneaking alongside larger vessels if they lack the power to open a wormhole.
5.  **Events:** Random events based on active stories (Pirate raids, War stories, Racing challenges).

## Key Development Points
1.  **Modularity:** All systems must be constructed in a modular manner.
2.  **Prototyping:** Systems will require separate prototypes (e.g., ship handling vs. wormhole generation).
3.  **Testing Modes:** Requires a space flight system, wormhole system, and a "Ground Race" (shield rolling only) for testing.
4.  **Iteration:** The main menu and game modes will iterate. Potential for "Hyperspace" races (flying over hills/inverted hills).
5.  **Stories:** Built-in stories (e.g., Rookie, War, Invasion) guide the player.
6.  **Architecture:** Complex interacting systems, potentially built as communicating JS files/modules. Final production likely in Unreal, but JS prototype is viable.
7.  **Loading:** A loader system is core, revealing UI elements as modules load.
8.  **Distributed Calc:** Players share calculations (at least 3 performing tunnel calcs).
9.  **Crew Interaction:** 3D models in suits for physical presence, 2D cartoon portraits for dialogue.
