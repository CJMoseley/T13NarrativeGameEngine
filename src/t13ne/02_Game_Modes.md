# Game Modes and Leagues

*   **Planetary Leagues:** Every inhabited planet has one. Combination of ground races and orbital wormholes (elliptical loops distorted by system bodies). Small ships, limited power/range, cheap components, 1-5 crew.

*   **Local System League:** Larger ships (1-8 crew), more expensive. Races orbit the star, twisting around planets.

*   **Local Interstellar League:** Rules vary by galaxy region. Larger, powerful ships, often with robot crew backups. Ships have Molecular Scoops to mine resources/fuel from atmospheres or wormhole walls (essential for engineers).

*   **Rim Leagues:** Long races on the outer rim. "Wild" with few rules; criminal nature, weapons fire, and armour encouraged.

*   **Arm / Spur Leagues:** Tightly controlled races along spiral arms. Limited ship builds/weaponry. Armour usually mandated.

*   **Core League:** Galactic core. No weaponry allowed. Ships must be small and nimble to navigate complex wormholes warped by neutron stars and black holes. Visually distinct (black holes/stars visible through walls).

*  **Development Races:** During development several race types will be prototyped with test tracks. These need a countdown, timer and an escape method as testing should end. They should also have a distance to end marker in the UI. 

*  **Straight:** a simple tube that prototypes a static wormhole, for early physics develeopment.
*  **Planet Surface:** a planetary surface that can be flown over or rolled over as required.
*  **Obstacle:** a wormhole with constraints used in testing how far the wormhole can be allowed to deform and mutate over the course of the race.
*  **Active Wormhole:** the final true wormhole environment, but arcade mode, where all ships are test models. 
*  **Hyperspace:** Hyper space is a variant on the planetray surface and wormhole, consisiting of two "planes" that are infintely wide, but close enough that the mountains of each side should touch the other, this can create closed cavern like races or open plans, gravity here works towards the planes, no matter what twist the hyperspace may have applied to it. (they can appear vertical or horizontal or anywhere inbetween).
*  **Space race:** A test flight system for in system flying and inter-system warp races (boring straight races warp pulsing through real space)
